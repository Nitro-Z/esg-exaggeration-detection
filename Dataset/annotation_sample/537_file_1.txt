Editorial Policy
About this Report
This is the fourth Sustainability Report issued by Nantex Industry Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as Nantex Industry, Nantex, 
and the Company). Through the issuance of the ESG Report, all the stakeholders can understand Nantex’s efforts and 
achievements in the promotion of sustainable development. This Report is divided into four major chapters of “Sustainable 
Management”, “Sustainable Governance”, “Sustainable Environment”, and “Sustainable Workplace”, disclosing in 
detail Nantex’s management and current situation in economic, environmental, and social aspects in 2023. The issuance of 
the Report enhances our communication channels with our stakeholders and demonstrates our commitment to promoting 
sustainable development and fulfilling our corporate social responsibilities.
Report Scope and Calculation Basis 
The content of this Report covers Nantex Industry’s relevant operating systems and activities between January 1 and 
December 31, 2023, focusing on the management and performance of the material topics. The scope of disclosure mainly 
focused on Nantex Industry’s operating bases in Taiwan (Kaohsiung Linyuan Plant and Tainan Office), and the environmental 
information disclosure mainly focused on Kaohsiung Linyuan Plant. The statistical data disclosed in the content are mainly 
the results of our internal statistics and surveys, and are presented using internationally recognized indicators. If there are any 
estimates, they will be noted in each relevant chapter.  The source of the data in economic aspect is from the consolidated 
financial statements published in 2023 Annual Report of Nantex Industry Co., Ltd. The financial data is calculated in New 
Taiwan dollars using publicly available information after verified by PwC Taiwan. In addition, various management systems, 
including ISO 9001 quality management system, ISO 14001 environmental management system, ISO 45001 occupational 
safety and health management system, and ISO 14064-1 greenhouse gas inventory are subject to regular internal audits, and 
all have been verified by third-party verification agencies. When compared with our 2022 Report, historical data in this 2023 
Report may be restated in certain instances, which will be explained within the text. 
Editorial Principles and Verification
This Report followed the latest GRI (Global Reporting Initiative) Standards (GRI Standard 2021), Taiwan Stock Exchange 
Corporation Rules Governing the Preparation and Filing of Sustainability Reports by TWSE Listed Companies, Sustainability 
Accounting Standards Board (SASB) announced by International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB), and Task Force on 
Climate-Related Financial Disclosures (TCFD), and in accordance with the editorial principles, Nantex disclosed the relevant 
strategies, goals, and specific actions related to the material topics.  The content presented in this Report is based on the 
latest GRI requirements to identify the significance of the impact of sustainability issues in terms of economic, environmental, 
and people (human rights) aspects as a reference basis for information disclosure of this Report. The index of the disclosed 
content is detailed in Appendix I to III. Nantex’s ESG Report is prepared in accordance with the internal control operating 
procedures. The President’s Office- Industrial Safety Team serves as the preparation secretary, and is in charge of the overall 
planning, communication and integration, and data collection. All relevant departments of the Company jointly participated 
in the editing, revision, and proofreading of the content of this Report. This Report has not been verified by an impartial third 
party. To ensure the accuracy of the content, the completed report is reviewed and approved by the chairman and president 
and submitted to the board of directors for approval on August 7,2024 before its issuance.

Message from Management
In 2023, the global market was still in a sluggish state, posing considerable risks and challenges to the operation of the 
Company. The products were still affected by the production capacity of the downstream manufacturers, and the industry 
production capacity remained low. However, for this fiscal year, the Company still remained a certain level of profitability. The 
consolidated annual revenue was NT$8.942 billion, a decrease of approximately 23.37%. The consolidated net profit after tax 
was NT$9.47billion, with the EPS after tax at NT$ 1.45. 
Nantex advocated the initiative of net zero emission by 2050 this year. In addition, to fulfill our commitment to sustainable 
management, starting from 2023, we have begun purchasing green energy certificates for partial electricity use within our 
facilities. We also adjusted the production capacity internally to control energy consumption and at the same time working 
on energy conservation and carbon reduction. In 2023, the Company also completed third-party verification of ISO 9001 and 
ISO 14001 to ensure the product quality and the effectiveness of the environmental management system implemented in 
the plant. Moreover, the GHG inventory also underwent the third-party verification to ensure the accuracy of the statistical 
data. In 2023, the total environmental protection investment within the plant amounted to NT$ 5.044 million to reduce the 
environmental impact of the Company’s production process.
In 2023, Nantex obtained the ISO 45001 re-validation and CNS 45001: 2018 occupational safety and health management 
system certification in terms of its occupational safety and health management system to ensure the safety of the employees. 
In addition, the Process Safety Management (PSM) system has been promoted in the plant to avoid serious incidents such as 
leakage of toxic chemicals, fires, and explosions. Through the implementation of various in-plant safety systems, education 
and training, safety promotion, irregular safety audits, emergency response drills, and other activities, every employee at 
Nantex can have a certain level of safety concept to reduce accidents caused by various unsafe behavior and environments. 
There were no major occupational accidents or process-related safety incidents at Nantex in 2023.  
Despite the sluggish global market in 2023, Nantex Industry still maintained its profits. However, in the face of the changes in 
the overall environment, the Company continues to pay attention to the changes and make according adjustments. Employee 
development training is continued to enhance the professional skills and safety awareness of all our employees, so as to 
create a good working environment and employee benefits to enable the Company to maintain a high degree of productivity. 
In 2023, through various internal activities, there were no occurrence of major occupational accidents, fires, or toxic chemical 
leaks, nor were there any labor disputes throughout the year. In the future, Nantex will uphold the concept of sustainable 
management and create a harmonious and good labor-management relationship to achieve the goal of sustainable 
management of the Company. 
In recent years, countries around the world have responded to the goal of net-zero emissions. Nantex Industry is a large 
energy user, and has actively implemented various energy-saving and carbon reduction measures in recent years. In 2023, 
part of the electricity consumption has been converted to the purchase of green electricity to reduce the generation of 
GHG. In addition, in response to the trend toward a high value-added and sophisticated industry, the Company continues 
to conduct R&D on corresponding materials to enhance the competitiveness of our products. In addition to continuing the 
implementation of occupational safety management system within the company, we also promote the in-plant process safety 
management system to avoid fires and toxic chemical incidents, ensuring a safer and more secure living environment for 
company employees and nearby residents. Moreover, Nantex still maintains high-quality products and continues to promote 
the quality management system in the plant. Furthermore, through product customization, after-sales service and professional 
consulting, we assist our clients to jointly make profits. Through the issuance of the ESG Report, all the stakeholders that are 
concerned about Nantex can have a better understanding of the Company’s implementation and promotion of ESG in all 
aspects. Our employees diligently carry out their respective duties, embodying the principles of sustainable development.

Stakeholders Communication
Nantex Industry adopted the AA1000 Stakeholder Engagement Standards (AA1000 SES) in 2021 to identify its stakeholders 
through five principles of Dependency, Responsibility, Influence, Diverse Perspectives and Tension. In 2022, to align 
stakeholder identification with the latest GRI Standards 2021 principles, the definition of stakeholders was redefined as 
“individuals for groups whose interests have been impacted by the organization’s activities”. During the stage of “assessing 
the significance of sustainability impacts”, an evaluation was conducted to assess the potential impact of each significant 
issue on individuals or groups, ranked according to the level of impact. The stakeholders identified included Government 
Agencies, Shareholders, Employees, Customers, and Contractors/Suppliers. The Company convened the heads of all units in 
2023 to discuss and resolve in the meeting, and compared with the previous year, there were no changes in the stakeholders 
in 2023.
Stakeholder Communication and Interaction
To understand the stakeholders’ needs and their expectations for the Company through appropriate communication channels 
and methods is an important foundation for corporate sustainable development and the fulfillment of corporate social 
responsibility. Nantex Industry attaches great importance to the rights and suggestions of its stakeholders. Topics of concern 
to the stakeholders are collected based on the daily interactions between each unit and the stakeholders. Through the various 
channels such as the ESG Report, the official website, and public information, Nantex provides appropriate responses to the 
opinions of its stakeholders and use them as a reference for the Company to make management decisions. It is hoped that the 
results of sustainable management can be shared with all the stakeholders.

 Product Health and Safety
Material Topic
Product Health and Safety
Purpose of
Management
Nantex Industry’s main product is latex, which has obtained favor from global medical equipment and 
glove manufacturers. Therefore, it is crucial that the product can pass relevant regulatory certifications 
and customer requirements. The Company is dedicated to providing customers with safe products. All 
our products have passed relevant health and safety regulations, such as EU RoHS, REACH regulations, 
etc. Our customers can obtain the Safety Data Sheet, Product Specifications, Technical Data and related 
certificates on our official website to ensure the safety of the products.  
Description of 
Positive Benefits
Nantex Industry’s products are mainly used for human body. In order to reduce the harm of latex gloves to 
the human body, the Company will rigorously examine the rationality of relevant raw materials and process 
formulations to ensure compliance with domestic and international regulations and customer requirements 
as our utmost mission, and is dedicated to providing customers with safe and reliable products.  
Description of 
Negative Impacts
Some of the raw materials of our products contain chemicals classified as substances of concern, 
environmentally hazardous substances or publicly regulated dangerous goods. If not properly managed, 
in addition to potential government regulatory penalties, they may potentially lead to allergic reactions 
and other health issues with prolonged human exposure. 
Specific Actions 
Taken in 2023 
	
⊙The products are sampled and tested by third-party notary units in accordance with internal execution 
norms or customer requirements. 
Management 
Evaluation
Mechanism 
	
⊙Every year, the product inspections are conducted in accordance with internal safety regulations 
and chemical management processes. In addition, samples are sent to third-party notary units for 
testing according to customer-specific standards to ensure product quality meets both domestic and 
international regulatory standards as well as customer requirements. 
Performance 
Results 
	
⊙In 2023, there were no incidents of non-compliance with product safety regulations or voluntary 
agreements. 
	
⊙In 2023, Nantex conducted health and safety assessment of all the products provided to customers.

Product Introduction
▸NANTEX® Latex
NANTEX® Latex is also referred to as Synthetic Latex for short. The production technology was introduced by Nippon Zeon 
in early days, and after years of changes in market applicability and demand, the originally imported products have been 
discontinued. Currently, all the products being manufactured and sold are developed in-house. Except for a small part of the 
NBR and SBR latex is reserved for our own use, the rest is distributed in South Korea, Thailand, Indonesia, Pakistan, Australia, 
and China.   
NANTEX® Latex is mainly carboxylated NBR (CNBR) latex.  Due to its excellent physical properties and advantages including 
oil resistance and absence of proteins and other impurities, the Nantex NBR latex for gloves is found perfect for use in dipped 
gloves for examination, medical, household, and industrial purposes. Thanks to years of intensive research and development 
in glove latex products, and a clear understanding of the needs of the market, Nantex has gained acceptance and approval 
by some of the biggest glove markers for both quality and service.  Along with our fully owned subsidiary, Zhenjiang 
Nantex Chemical Industry Co., Ltd., our combined production capacity has grown significantly, making Nantex one of the 
top producers of NBR latex for glove dipping.  Apart from glove dipping, CNBR can also be used in non-woven fabrics and 
abrasives.
Nantex also produces carboxylated styrene butadiene rubber (CSBR) latex for applications such as paper coating, carpet 
backing, cement modification, and shoe insoles, non-woven fabrics  and abrasives

▸NANTEX® NBR
Nancar® NBR is a copolymer of Acrylonitrile and Butadiene. It has excellent oil resistant properties. Nantex began production

Product Health and Safety
Nantex Industry’s main product is latex. It provides latex products to downstream manufacturers, who then manufacture 
them into end products for sale to consumers. Take the medical gloves, one of the end products, for example, if they contain 
harmful chemical substances, they could have negative impacts on the Company’s reputation and the consumers. Therefore, 
our company has established relevant management regulations and procedures to ensure effective management of chemical 
substances and mitigate their potential hazards to the customers and employees.
Management of Hazardous Substances in Products
Nantex Industry’s products are all manufactured in accordance with domestic and international regulations, including 
the preparation of Safety Data Sheets (SDS). We follow strict internal safety standards during the production process and 
have implemented comprehensive chemical management procedures to ensure that all departments in the plant adhere 
to regulatory requirements, thereby protecting the health and safety of our employees.  We also acquire the necessary 
certificates and documents for any additional standards requested by our customers (such as RoHS, REACH, PAHs, PFOA, 
PFAS, POPS, etc.). If our customers require third-party testing reports, we will provide the relevant test reports or issue a 
commitment certificate for restricted use of chemical substances to ensure that our products meet applicable international 
standards.  
In 2023, 100% of the products provided by Nantex underwent comprehensive health and safety evaluation, including 
EU RoHS, REACH regulations, conflict of minerals policies, TL1010 automotive interior material combustion performance 
tests, and Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons (PAHs) testing. Our customers can find “Safety Data Sheets”, “Product 
Specifications”, “Technical Data” and related certification documents for each product on our official website to ensure the 
safety of our products.  
The raw materials we use include butadiene and acrylonitrile, which belong to Category I and Category II hazardous 
substances of the Globally Harmonized System of Classification and Labeling of Chemicals (GHS). However, after undergoing 
chemical reactions in sealed equipment, the products no longer contain Category I and Category II hazardous substances. 
Products produced by Nantex do not contain any substances classified as Category I and Category II substances of the GHS 
specification. In 2023, Nantex did not violate health and safety regulations.  
Abatement Strategy in Products
Chemical substances are used extensively in various aspects of daily human life. In response to concerns about risks that 
chemical substances pose to human health, Nantex aims to gradually reduce the use of high-risk chemicals in our facilities 
through our R&D efforts. For example, the Company’s latex products can be categorized into natural and synthetic latex. 
Due to the potential for allergic reactions in some individuals upon contact with natural latex gloves, which may result in 
symptoms such as itching, redness, and in some rare severe cases, shock or life-threatening conditions, the Company has 
chosen to use synthetic latex instead to minimize the risk of allergies. 
On the other hand, some chemicals may affect the human endocrine system, which are referred to as Endocrine Disrupting 
Chemicals (EDCs), also known as environmental hormones, and can affect the growth and development of living organisms. 
In the past, due to the limited awareness of chemical substance regulation and a lack of advanced testing methods, the 
rubber industry often used many materials containing these environmental hormones during manufacturing. In recent 
years, due to the increased safety awareness and improved safety regulations, we have progressively reduced the use of 
materials containing environmental hormones. As of the end of 2023, the Company used environmental hormone-containing 
materials only in one product specified by a customer. All other products are free from such materials. We are committed 
to continuously improving and optimizing our product formulas to ensure they meet all regulatory standards and do not 
compromise human health and safety.

Internal Audit Mechanism
The Internal Auditing Department of Nantex Industry reports directly to the board of directors, and is composed of a 
dedicated audit supervisor and a sufficient number of full-time auditors. The appointment, dismissal, and compensation 
of the auditor supervisor shall be approved by the board of directors. The company’s internal audit is divided into planned 
and specific audits. Planned audits are developed on a yearly basis based on risk assessment results, and relevant units will 
be notified of the audits after approved by the board of directors, and such audits will be carried out according to the plan. 
Specific audits can be assigned by the board of directors and the Audit Committee as needed to examine particular issues. To 
implement a self-monitoring mechanism and improve the quality and efficiency of the internal audit department, each unit 
conducts an annual self-evaluation, and after the audits, the results will be reviewed by the internal audit unit.  
The company’s internal audit supervisor attended 4 Audit Committee meetings and 5 board meetings in 2023. In addition 
to reporting on recent audit tasks, the internal audit supervisor also reports to the independent directors on the overall audit 
work performance of the previous year at the first Audit Committee meeting every year. Additionally, detailed communication 
with each independent director is conducted concerning the audit findings, internal control deficiencies identified, and the 
status of improvements and follow-up actions on any anomalies discovered. In addition to providing written audit reports 
and follow-up reports to independent directors, the internal audit department will also engage in direct communication with 
independent directors through email, phone, or face-to-face meetings when needed.

3.5 Pollution Prevention and Control 
Management
Air Pollution Control
To reduce air pollution emissions and achieve eco-friendliness, Nantex dismantled the fuel-fired steam boiler in Linyuan 
Plant in 2017 and replaced with purchased steam. Therefore, there has been no emissions of sulfur oxides (SOx) since 2018. 
In 2020, to expand production capacity and lower air pollution emissions, we invested in the seventh phase expansion (EP7) 
project of the Synthetic Rubber Latex Plant and added a regenerative incinerator with a three-tank design (to increase VOCs 
treatment efficiency) and selective catalytic reduction denitrification (SCR) to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. The 
main air pollutants of Linyuan Plant include nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate pollutants and volatile organic compounds 
(VOCs), of which VOCs emissions account for the majority. The R22 refrigerant used in the company is a controlled substance 
under Montreal Protocol, for it is one of the components that destroys ozone. We will gradually replace the refrigerants 
with eco-friendly ones. In 2023, all the air conditioners were replaced with R32 refrigerant. The company entrusts certified 
environmental testing and measurement agencies to conduct regular inspections and calculate emissions based on regulatory 
intervals, and reports to the Environmental Protection Agency to pay the air pollution control fees as mandated. In addition, 
in accordance with the provisions in Air Pollution Control Act, we have appointed a dedicated air pollution control officer and 
one deputy to implement and enforce the required control measures as prescribed by the regulations.

Sexual Harassment Prevention Measures
To uphold gender equality and personnel dignity in the workplace and to strictly prohibit any form of sexual harassment, 
Regulations Governing Prevention, Complaint and Punishment of Sexual Harassment have been established and are 
applicable to all personnel in the company. If any employee encounter any of the following prohibited matters, he/she may 
submit  a written complaint to the unit supervisor or the personnel unit. In consideration of protecting the complainant’s 
rights, all processes will be conducted in a confidential manner, and any information that could reveal the complainant’s 
identity must be kept confidential. In accordance with the complainant’s statements, relevant department heads or 
personnel unit have to form an investigation team within 7 days and provide written notification to the parties involved within 
10 days to attend and clarify the facts. Individuals who may have a conflict of interest in the case must recuse themselves 
from being members of the investigation team. If the investigation validates the allegations, disciplinary actions will be taken 
according to company policies. In severe cases, termination of employment may be carried out under Article 12, Paragraph 
2 of the Labor Standards Act. If the case involves criminal conduct, it will be referred to judicial system. Should the claim be 
proven to be acts of retaliation or false accusations, appropriate disciplinary action will be taken against the accuser based on 
the severity of the case, with termination of employment possible for serious offenses.

4.5 Employee Safety and Health
The company is dedicated to employee safety and healthy working environment. In terms of work safety, through regular 
education and training, publicity and on-site supervision, employees can develop correct safety awareness when they work 
and their alertness to potential hazards can be enhanced to prevent accidents caused by their own unsafe behaviors. As 
for employee health, we organize employee health checkups to enable them to understand their own physical conditions. 
Coupled with the monthly consultations with on-site physicians, our employees can consult on relevant medical issues. We 
also organize various health lectures and health promotion activities from time to time, so that our employees can ensure 
their own physical, mental, and spiritual development when they focus on their work. 
Occupational Safety and Health Management System
To comply with domestic environmental, safety and health regulations and reach the goal of zero disaster, Nantex Industry 
passed the OHSAS 18001 Occupational Safety and Health Management System and TOSHMS (Taiwan Occupational Safety 
and Health Management System) certification from Bureau of Standards, Metrology and Inspection, MOEA in April, 2010. 
To align with the international standards, we completed the conversion of the Occupational Safety and Health Management 
System in December, 2020, and passed the verification by Metal Industries Research & Development Center in 2021, obtaining 
dual certifications for ISO 45001:2018 and CNS 45001:2018. In 2023, a surveillance audit of the Occupational Safety and Health 
Management system was conducted to verify that the management system adheres to continuous improvement principles of 
the PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle.
The scope of the Occupational Safety and Health Management System applies to all workers at Nantex, including our 
employees, dispatched workers, contractors, visitors, and other personnel. The management system is audited internally and 
externally once every year, and the comprehensive re-certification and external document review of the management system 
is carried out every three years. The scope of the verifications includes the entire facility, including the internal employees, 
employees of contracted service providers, and those engaged in temporary construction, repair, or maintenance work, 
ensuring effective occupational safety management.

Process Safety Management
To avoid fires, explosions, and other hazards caused by leaks of toxic or flammable chemicals, the plant has started the

The plant's hazard control of chemical processing includes 14 operational controls, including Process Safety Information

performing PSM business receive relevant PSM related education and training. Due to the personnel rotation during the

hour PSM training where trained and experienced senior staff served as the lecturers. Records of the training were maintained.

review various in-plant PSM implementation operations and put forward suggestions for improvement, which will be listed in 
the process safety management meeting for regular tracking of the improvement progress, so as to facilitate the improvement 
of the in-plant PSM and prevent major industrial accidents. During the reporting period, there were no incidents of toxic 
chemical leaks, fires, or explosions in the factory.

Hazard Identification and Risk & Opportunity Assessment Control
To avoid the operations, activities, services, and facilities from endangering the safety and health of personnel or causing 
financial losses to the company, we proactively identify hazards that can cause hazards to workers in accordance with the

Statistics of Occupational Accidents 
There were a total of 2 recordable occupational injuries at Nantex in 2023, both happened to male workers. One incident 
happened when the locking the level gauge in the reaction tank, the level gauge dropped, hitting the worker who was holding

cylinder on the reaction tank has been repositioned to improve the working space (the original spacing between the discharge 
valve cylinder and the level gauge was too close, causing insufficient working space). A lift platform has been used to assist 
with the installation, aiming to avoid similar incidents in the future.

4.6 Emergency Response Management
Nantex Industry identifies potential emergency situations and prepares and responds in accordance with the Management 
Measures for Environmental Considerations, Hazard Identification and Risk and Opportunity Assessment Control Operation 
Procedures, regulations, and other requirements. The Industrial Safety Department has established written emergency 
response procedures to assist all departments with the creation of their own emergency response manual. The department 
collaborates with the service center of the Industrial Park and the regional joint defense (or related units) so that when 
an accident or emergency occurs, we can provide emergency response guidelines to promptly take necessary response 
measures, conduct emergency handling, and organize mobilization to effectively prevent, control, or reduce the impact of 
accidents or emergencies and environmental impact, reduce the company losses to the minimum, to ensure the safety of the 
plant personnel and community residents as well as mitigate the impact on the environment.
In-plant Emergency Response Measures
The organizational structure of emergency response in Nantex Industry's plant is divided into two frameworks, regular shift 
time and non-regular shift time (including night shifts and holidays). When a disaster occurs, the on-duty supervisor will assess 
the situation and determine whether to implement the response plan, while the factory manager serves as the commander 
for emergency response in the plant. When the disaster extends beyond the factory to the third stage, the factory's response 
structure remains the same as in the second stage. However, the factory's response commander has to coordinate with the city 
commander for joint disaster management. If necessary, the factory should follow the city commander. During non-regular 
shifts, the duty supervisor will act as the commander. If the disaster escalates to a point where the current shift personnel 
cannot handle the situation, relevant staff will be recalled to the factory to assist with disaster response. The duty supervisor 
will only transfer command once higher-level command personnel arrives at the factory. 
When an emergency event is confirmed and judged to be immediately uncontrollable, an alert shall be issued, and the 
full-scale emergency response across the plant should be initiated. The Emergency Response Center shall be immediately 
established, and all other response teams in the plant should report to their designated locations to begin operation. The 
primary Emergency Response Center is located on the southeast side of the main warehouse (next to the office of the Utility 
Section). If the Emergency Response Center is affected by the disaster, it will be relocated to the secondary Emergency 
Response Center located on the south side of the Filling Plant. If both Emergency Response Centers are compromised, 
the chief commander will determine the need to set up a temporary command center. If a leader of any response unit is 
unavailable, the designated deputy will take over their duties and powers, which will be equivalent to those of the original 
leader. In 2023, a total of 6 emergency response drills were held in the factory, and the contents of the drills included the 
butadiene storage tank area (twice), the acrylonitrile storage tank area, methyl methacrylate storage tank, liquid ammonia 
refrigeration area, and chemical warehouse response. The number of participants in these drills totaled 132.

4.7 Social Contributions
In the Linyuan Mangrove Ecological Zone located on the west of the Gaoping River Estuary, there has been a plan to artificially 
restore mangrove plants since 1994, with four types of existing mangroves in Taiwan, namely Avicennia marina (Forssk.) Vierh, 
Kandelia obovata, Lumnitzera racemosa, and Rhizophora stylosa. Fruitful results have been achieved. The ecological zone 
has been the perfect place for nearby schools and community residents to engage in ecological teaching, local teaching, 
recreation and leisure. The Mangrove Ecological Zone is adjacent to the petrochemical industrial zone, and is a unique and 
only international ecological conservation achievement recognized globally, making it another pride of Taiwan. Nantex 
Industry is located in Linyuan Industrial Park, and the production activities, products and services by the company are in 
compliance with the requirements of the competent authorities and regulations, leaving no significant negative impact on the 
ecology. In addition, no complaints related to ecological protection have been received.

infrastructure activities held by Linyuan District Office. Starting from 2022, the scope of our sponsorship has expanded to 
activities of temples, associations and societies, and related activities of the neighborhood representative in Linyuan District.
