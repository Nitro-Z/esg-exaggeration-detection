About the Report
This is the fourth Sustainability Report of Wayi 
International Digital Entertainment Co., Ltd. (hereinafter 
referred to as “Wayi International”, “Wayi”, or “the 
Company”), which published its first Sustainability Report 
in both Chinese and English in 2022. The report will be 
also published on Wayi’s official website ( https://www.
wayi.net/5_duty04.html ) for Wayi’s stakeholders and 
information users.
This report aims to transparently disclose non-
financial information of the Company in the aspects of 
environment (E), society (S), and governance (G) based on 
5 dimensions: corporate governance, product innovation 
and management, workplace atmosphere environmental 
friendliness, and social care, in order to fully demonstrate 
the Company’s efforts and achievements in sustainable 
development to the actual or potential stakeholders 
of Wayi. We hope that this report can truly present the 
Company's commitment to sustainable development, and 
we also look forward to stakeholders providing valuable 
feedback as an important reference for the Company to 
improve and enhance its sustainability strategies.
The reporting scope of this report is Wayi International Digital Entertainment Co., Ltd., includes the Taipei 
headquarters and the Taichung branch, but excludes the subsidiaries listed in Wayi’s consolidated financial statements. As 
of 2024, these subsidiaries had no actual operating activities and are expected to be included in the disclosure scope in 
2025. The data on environmental and social metrics was collected and summarized statistically by various departments, 
with internationally accepted metrics utilized as the calculation unit; the financial data of economic metrics was calculated 
in New Taiwan Dollars (NTD) according to the International Financial Reporting Standard (IFRS), and was disclosed 
after the verification and certification by Deloitte Taiwan. The information disclosed is consistent with the data in Wayi’s 
consolidated financial statements. Special circumstances, if any, are explained in each chapter.
Entities included in Wayi’s consolidated financial statements are:
1. Wayi International Digital Entertainment Co., Ltd.
2. WAYI SOFTMAGIC INVESTMENT (ASIA) LTD. (wholly-owned subsidiary of Wayi)
3. WAYI SOFTMAGIC INVESTMENT (CAYMAN) LTD. (wholly-owned subsidiary of ASIA)
4. Hua Zhi Software Private Ltd. (a subsidiary 51% owned by Wayi)

This report was mainly prepared in accordance with the GRI Standards 2021 announced by the Global Sustainability Standards Board (GSSB), and there is no applicable GRI Sector 
Standards. In addition, the content structure of this report was established according to the frameworks of the SASB Standards released by the International Sustainability Standards 
Board (ISSB), the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD), and the directive policies specified by Taipei Exchange in “Rules Governing the Preparation and Filing of 
Sustainability Reports by TWSE Listed Companies”, and was consistent with the United Nation’s 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). According to the reporting principles, Wayi’s 
relevant strategies, goals and specific actions related to the material topics are disclosed. Furthermore, detailed information of GRI, SASB, and TCFD content index are provided in 
Appendix 1, 2, and 3 of this report.
Internal Review
The Company is committed to practicing sustainable development and aligning with international trends by establishing a formal functional "Sustainable Development Committee" 
responsible for promoting, formulating, and deciding on risk assessments and countermeasures in various aspects such as environment, society, and corporate governance. The 
Company has also established the "Sustainability Committee Organizational Charter" to achieve the goal of sustainable operations and to implement due diligence.
The "Sustainable Development Committee" instructs the management (General Manager and the heads of different centers) to oversee the work on various sustainability issues, 
and establishes the "Sustainability Task Force" to promote implementation. The content and related data of this report were compiled, reviewed, edited and revised by each sustainability 
task force based on the division of work, and further reviewed by the top executive of each responsible department to ensure the accuracy, correctness and coverage of all important 
sustainability issues of the disclosed information.
The Company’s Board of Directors approved the internal control system and audit guidelines for  “Sustainable Information Management”  on November 13, 2024, and also approved 
the 2025 annual audit plan, with the audit of sustainable information scheduled for September 2025.
External Verification and Statement
This report was verified by an independent third-party organization, SGS Taiwan Limited, in accordance with AA1000 AS v3 Type 1 moderate assurance level, focusing on the 
inclusivity, materiality, responsiveness, and impact of the information disclosed in the report. The verified report was finalized with the approval of the General Manager and was 
subsequently submitted to the Sustainable Development Committee and the Board of Directors in August 2025 for consent and approval for issuance. The assurance statement issued 
by the independent third party can be found in Appendix 5 of the report.

Commitment to Sustainable Management
As global attention to ESG issues continues to rise, Wayi, as a pioneer in the domestic online gaming industry, also 
bears the responsibility of promoting sustainable development. We firmly believe that a company’s success stems not only 
from innovation in its business model but also from its positive impact on society and the environment.
In terms of the environment, we are committed to reducing our carbon footprint during operations, actively adopting 
cloud service technologies, and optimizing server performance to lower energy consumption. At the same time, while 
providing digital services, we consider how to reduce paperless inefficiencies and other forms of resource waste. In 
addition, for our external products, we prioritize the use of environmentally friendly materials and incorporate the 
promotion of local characteristics, working together to continuously contribute to the sustainability of the environment in 
which we live.
On the social front, we value the positive impact of game content and are committed to creating a healthy, inclusive, 
and diverse online gaming environment. In platform operations, we take the lead in the industry by actively implementing 
real-name verification, ensuring that only players who meet legal requirements can access the game content, rather 
than passively leaving it to players to determine their own eligibility. Internally, we have established a dedicated content 
review mechanism and audit process to ensure that game content complies with legal requirements and addresses 
societal concerns regarding sensitive issues such as children and adolescents. In addition, we have renovated the office 
environment to provide employees with more friendly working conditions and actively encourage them to participate in 
activities supporting underprivileged groups and charitable causes, fulfilling our responsibility as a member of society.
In terms of governance, we uphold the principles of transparency, integrity, and accountability, striving to establish 
a sound internal governance framework to ensure that the Company’s operations align with our own expected ethical 
standards. We also regularly publish Sustainability Reports to demonstrate our efforts and achievements in sustainable 
development to stakeholders. We aspire to continuously and steadily create value, rewarding shareholders while taking 
care of our employees, thereby realizing a positive and active management cycle as a benchmark in the industry. This is our 
commitment and the goal of our management team.
Chairman  Shu-Kai Shih

1.1 About Wayi
Wayi International Digital Entertainment Co., Ltd. was 
established in 1993. The Company's stocks were publicly 
listed on the Taipei Exchange (TPEx) for trading in March 
2004 as a listed company in the cultural and creative 
industry, and the stock code is 3086. The Company's 
headquarters is currently located in Neihu District, Taipei 
City. A branch office has been established in Xitun District, 
Taichung City. During the early stage of establishment, the 
Company released localized Japanese standalone games. 
In 1997, the R&D Department was established to develop 
proprietary products. Through the collaboration with 
Tong Li Publishing, we converted the well-known manga 
“Destined Emperor” into a computer game. Subsequently, 
in July 1999, we created www.waei.net and launched the 
pioneering domestic online game “Ren Zai Jiang Hu”. 
Thereafter, “StoneAge” was launched and became the 
first domestic online game with 10,000 players, turning 
online games into the core business of Wayi. Other 
businesses and services developed include online game 
cash flow systems and esports. In 2018, we collaborated 
with Wanin International to cooperatively engage in 
technology and game development. In addition, with the 
license of “81Keys”, we developed the business model 
of joint operation development. In 2023, the “League 
Funny” gaming community discussion platform and its 
development and operations team were incorporated 
as the foundation for developing a comprehensive entertainment platform in the future. In the first half of 2024, the 
development and construction of the platform commenced, and on May 29, the trial operation of the BanaBana platform 
was announced. While continuing to optimize and develop new functions, products were gradually launched to provide 
more diverse digital entertainment services.

Brand Value
Future Vision
To cope with the internationalization trend and to achieve the corporate vision of a comprehensive digital entertainment service leading brand, Wayi International launched 
the “Brand Transformation Event” in April 2003, in order to establish a whole new corporate image for Wayi International with a new CIS identity system and brand promotion plan. 
First, the corporate logo of  “Wayi”  replaced the original “Waei” in order to overcome any inconsistency in the pronunciation of the name and to allow people to understand that the 
name of “Wayi” is tied with the word “Joy” in the Taiwanese pronunciation, thereby further understanding the meaning that the brand of Wayi plans to convey to the consumers. 
Accordingly, based on the core of such meaning, Wayi extends its business in all aspects of digital entertainment and aims to create a gaming world that is full of adventures and 
imaginations for all gamers.
Wayi positions itself with the concept of “Constructing Dream Club for Gamers” and adopts the brand philosophy of Fun, Trustworthy and Relationship. In addition, the Company 
implements a business model that is deep-rooted in Taiwan and optimized for global market expansion, in order to provide comprehensive digital entertainment services of high add-
value to all people. Furthermore, the Company also aims to become the No. 1 digital entertainment brand in Asia in the next ten years.

Operation Planning
In 2024, Wayi began adjusting its operational focus. In the past, its core operations centered on publishing overseas products. However, with the evolution of technology and the 
impact of mobile devices and cross-platform development, the traditional structure of game development, publishing, and licensing operations has undergone changes. To keep pace 
with industry trends and enhance corporate value, Wayi is gradually shifting its focus toward developing an entertainment platform. In terms of content acquisition, the Company no 
longer relies solely on licensing to obtain game products but is expanding through joint operations, investments, and self-development. This not only diversifies the sources of products 
but also strengthens the Company’s capabilities in producing its own content and creating intellectual properties.
Ragnador ( https://ragnador.wayi.com.tw ):
“Ragnador: Youwa-shiki Kotei to Shuen no Yasha-hime” is a role-playing game that 
combines Japanese-style aesthetics with mystical elements. In this game, players are 
summoned as saviors to “Yokai World,” the sanctuary of youkai, where they explore a 
world filled with mystical powers and supernatural beings. Together with their youkai 
companions, they join forces to overturn the sorrowful fate of the youkai in this online RPG.
• Publishing operations: Covering game operation planning, publishing, marketing and promotion, community management, QA, and customer service. In 2024, Wayi continued 
publishing “Kemono Friends 3” and added “Ragnador” to its portfolio. In the same year, the operation of “La Corda d'Oro Starlight Orchestra” officially ended.
• Game development: Focusing on the development and planning of self-produced games, negotiations for international IPs, and operational collaborations.
• Strategic cooperative development: Formed a strategic alliance with Wanin International, a major Taiwanese game development company, and its subsidiaries to carry out in-depth 
collaboration in multiple areas, including art design, media operations, game innovation, and payment services, thereby continuously expanding market influence.
•  Game community platform: Operating the game community platform “League Funny,” which provides entertainment, news, live streaming, community groups, and other related 
game information and interactions to build traffic, expand membership, and serve as a channel for project marketing.
• Comprehensive entertainment platform: Including joint game operations, online comic publishing, marketing and promotion, community management, and comprehensive 
customer service. In May 2024, Wayi officially launched the trial operation of the new BanaBana platform. The platform currently has around 20,000 members and mainly offers two 
major services: the Game Section and the Comic Section.

1.2 Sustainable Development Organizational Structure
To ensure corporate sustainability and fulfill its social responsibilities, Wayi established the “Sustainable Development Best Practice Principles” in 2021 as a guideline for the Board 
of Directors, all employees, and the Company’s overall business operations. These principles set forth relevant policies, systems, and management plans to address risks and impacts 
related to corporate governance, economic, environmental, and social issues, with a commitment to continuous improvement. In 2023, the “Sustainable Development Committee 
Organizational Charter”  was established, and in August, the functional “Sustainable Development Committee” was formed. This committee is responsible for coordinating the 
company’s sustainable development direction and goals, promoting related initiatives, and conducting regular monitoring and control. The execution results are also reported to the 
Board of Directors to enhance Wayi’s corporate image and achieve the Company’s goal of sustainable operations.
The Sustainable Development Committee assigned the General Manager and the heads of each division to establish a “Sustainability Task Force” based on actual operations. 
The task force consists of three working groups: environmental, social, and corporate governance, which are responsible for promoting the management of sustainability issues in each 
respective area.

Issues of Concern to Stakeholders:
In addition to the routine department operating activities and stakeholder engagement, to better understand and respond to 
the needs and expectations of stakeholders, Wayi distributed stakeholder concern questionnaires on the 18 sustainability issues 
to collect the views and suggestions of various parties on the Company's sustainability issues, in order to identify and assess the 
materiality of such related issues. A total of 171 questionnaires were retrieved this time. The full score of each sustainability topic was 
5, and 9 sustainability topics with a score above 3.65 in the questionnaire analysis results were regarded as key topics of concern to 
stakeholders.
Materiality Assessment:
The Administration Center CFO, members of Sustainability Task Force, and external consultants convened a material topic 
identification meeting to discuss the 18 sustainability issues, 9 issues of concern to stakeholders, and the 2023 material topics. At 
last, in consideration of the overlapping and aspects of the issues, 14 major sustainability issues were identified for the materiality 
assessment. The respective internal and external impacts generated by the operating activities of Wayi on the 14 major sustainability 
issues were investigated in accordance with the GRI Standards, with the significance thereof assessed, i.e.:
• Assess the “actual”, “potential”, “positive” and “negative” impacts of the Company's operating activities on the external economy,  
   environment and people (human rights) with respect to each topic.
• Assess the “positive” and “negative” impacts on the internal operation of the Company that may be caused by each topic.
	
Impact Score Calculation
	
• Negative impact materiality score:
	
   External negative impact level (severity*probability)*internal negative impact level.
	
• Positive impact materiality score:
	
   External positive impact level (scale and scope*probability)*internal positive impact level.
	
•  For sustainability topics involving human rights impacts, the calculation weight of the score was increased, and the positive 
and negative impact scores of each topic were calculated.
Stakeholders Affected:
•  Assess each sustainability topic materiality process, and also identify individuals or groups whose rights and interests have been or  
   might be affected by the operating activities of the Company with respect to each topic.

Stakeholder Communication and Engagement
This report follows the GRI Standards (GRI Standards 2021), defining stakeholders as “individuals or groups whose rights have been or may be affected by the organization’s 
activities.” In 2024, considering operational activities and organizational development goals, the members of the Sustainability Task Force and department heads conducted internal 
discussions. Referring to industry stakeholder practices and the five principles of the AA1000 SES Stakeholder Engagement Standard (dependency, responsibility, influence, diverse 
perspectives, and tension), five main categories of stakeholders were identified: investors/shareholders, gamers/consumers, employees, business partners, and the general public.

concerns through various communication channels, including telephone, email, surveys, and the website. Internal departments then coordinate and discuss the feedback to determine 
appropriate responses based on the nature and impact of the issues. The status of stakeholder communication is reported to the Board of Directors on a regular basis, at least once a year.

Board of Directors
As the highest governance body in Wayi, the Board of Directors provides instructions for the Company’s strategy, supervises the management, and is accountable to the Company 
and shareholders. In terms of the operation and arrangement of the corporate governance system, the Board of Directors exercises its functions and powers according to laws and 
regulations, the Company’s Articles of Incorporation, or the resolutions of the shareholders’ meeting. There are 3 official functional committees established thereunder, i.e. the 
"Remuneration Committee", the "Audit Committee" and the "Sustainable Development Committee", in order to support the operations of the Board of Directors and report to the Board 
of Directors on a regular basis.
To materialize corporate governance, reinforce internal control, and audit operations, the Audit Department has been established under the Board of Directors. The audit supervisor 
regularly attends the Board of Directors’ meeting to present an audit report. The monthly internal audit work report and the quarterly audit follow-up report must be submitted to 
the independent directors for review. If major violations are discovered or the Company is likely to sustain significant damage, a report is prepared immediately and submitted to the 
independent directors.

Election of Directors
The Company performs the re-election of all directors every three years according to the regulations of the “Articles of Incorporation” and “Procedures for Election of Directors”, 
which stipulate that the candidate nomination system shall be adopted for the election of directors, that 5 to 9 directors shall be elected, and that the number of independent directors 
shall be no less than 3 and no less than one-fifth of all the directors. In addition, according to the provisions of the “Company Act” and the “Regulations Governing Appointment of 
Independent Directors and Compliance Matters for Public Companies” in Taiwan, the qualification of all candidates of directors and independent directors shall be reviewed by the 
Board of Directors, in order to ensure the transparency of the director nomination and election procedures. The Board of Directors verifies the professional qualifications and 
independence of the candidates, followed by approval through resolution, and the directors are then elected from the candidate roster at the shareholders’ meeting. Through such 
procedures, the Company ensures the professionalism and independence of the Board members, the shareholders’ equity, and the principle of fair treatment of shareholders.
The current directors are the 11th term of directors elected on June 23, 2022. There are a total of 7 directors, including 3 independent directors, with a term of office from June 
23, 2022 to June 22, 2025. The Chair of the current Board of Directors concurrently serves as the General Manager, for the purpose of increasing business performance and executing 
decisions. At the same time, the Chair discusses and exchanges opinions with the directors on major business decisions in the Board meeting to materialize corporate governance. 
Among the Board members, the Chair is the only one that concurrently acts as a managerial officer of the Company; the remaining directors are not concurrently acting as employees 
or managerial officers. In August 2024, the Board of Directors authorized independent directors to sign off on internal audit reports.
In response to the Sustainable Development Action Plan for listed companies issued by the FSC in 2023, which promotes board gender diversity and stipulates that starting in 
2025, at least one-third of directors on the Boards of listed companies must be of either gender, Wayi’s Board of Directors currently has seven members, of whom only one is female. 
The number of female directors is expected to be gradually increased during the Board re-election in June 2025.

Diversity Policy and Independence

be considered in terms of the composition of the Board of Directors. It is advisable that no more than one-third of the directors shall concurrently serve as a managerial officer of the 
Company. In addition, an appropriate diversification policy shall be formulated based on the Company's operations, business model and development needs, including but not limited 
to the following 2 dimensions of criteria:
1. Basic requirements and values: Gender, age, nationality, culture, etc.
2. Professional knowledge and skills: Professional background (such as law, accounting, industry, finance, marketing, or technology), professional skills, industry experience, etc.

2.4 Risk Management
To safeguard shareholder rights and ensure sustainable development, Wayi responds to the impacts and challenges of global economic changes by regularly conducting risk assessments on economic, 
environmental, and social issues, and formulating related risk management strategies. Through annual Risk Management Task Force meetings, the Company discusses strategies needed to address long-
term development risks and reviews risk management procedures, making rolling adjustments to response measures as necessary. From daily operations to functional management levels, key risks that may 
affect company operations are identified along with corresponding solutions to reduce the likelihood of operational disruptions. The consolidated risk management status serves as a basis for the following 
year’s strategic management, performance evaluation, and audit planning. In addition, the status of risk management operations is reported to the Board of Directors at least once a year. Wayi is committed 
to providing safer and more resilient risk management to achieve the goal of sustainable development for the company, shareholders, and employees.
I. Board of Directors:
        As the Company’s highest supervisory unit for risk 
management, its responsibilities include approving risk 
management policies and procedures, overseeing the overall 
implementation of risk management, and ensuring effective risk 
control.
II. Audit Committee:
       The Company’s risk management review unit is responsible 
for assisting the Board of Directors in reviewing risk management 
policies and procedures, examining the overall implementation 
of risk management, raising risk management issues and related 
recommendations for Board decision-making, and reporting on 
the status of risk management operations to the Board at least 
once a year.
III. Risk Management Task Force:
       The Risk Management Task Force, established under the 
Audit Committee, serves as the risk management execution 
unit. Its responsibilities include implementing risk management 
procedures. The CFO acts as the convener of the Task Force, while 
the heads of each department serve as members. The convener is 
responsible for assigning tasks and assisting in the establishment, 
promotion, maintenance, and review of the risk management 
mechanism, as well as conducting comprehensive assessments 
and providing recommendations on operational and emerging 
risks. The Task Force reports its implementation status to the Audit 
Committee at least once a year.

In response to the impact resulting from climate change, the Management Department has been the one taking care of the implementation of GHG inventory since 2022; it reports to the Sustainable 
Development Committee and the Board of Directors on a regular basis, and completes the disclosure of information on GHG inventory and verification according to the time prescribed by laws. Since 2024, 
the Company initially identified climate-related financial risks with reference to the four core elements of the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD): governance, strategy, risk management, 
and metrics and targets.

reviewing and guiding sustainability strategies, action plans, 
and annual goals, including those related to climate issues, 
and supervises the implementation of GHG inventory and 
verification schedules on a quarterly basis. The Board of Directors 
has established the Sustainable Development Committee (a 
functional committee) consisting of 4 members, including

Chairman. The committee is responsible for formulating the 
direction of sustainable development (including climate-related 
issues) and planning related strategies for implementation. It also 
conducts risk assessments and discusses countermeasures from 
environmental, social, and corporate governance perspectives, 
reviews the implementation of sustainable development, tracks 
the effectiveness of strategies and actions, and makes adjustments 
when necessary. The results are reported to the Board of Directors 
to strengthen the decision-making and oversight mechanism. 
The committee assigns the General Manager and the heads of 
each division to oversee various sustainability issues (including 
climate-related issues). Based on actual operational needs, 
different “Sustainability Task Forces” are formed to promote 
implementation, and cross-division meetings are convened 
occasionally for discussion and coordination. The implementation 
of the scheduled 2024 GHG inventory and verification was

times this year.

3.1 Value Chain Management
In response to market trends and the Company’s long-term strategic planning, Wayi adjusted its operational focus in 2024 by officially expanding into the R&D field. In addition to 
its existing midstream game publishing operations, the company began investing in upstream R&D activities, focusing on two major areas: platform development and game 
development. In 2024, within Wayi’s operations in the game value chain (upstream: game R&D, midstream: publishing operations), the proportion in the first half of the year was 10% 
for R&D and 90% for publishing operations. By the second half of the year, the ratio shifted to 30% and 70%, respectively. The Company will gradually increase the share of R&D in its 
overall business to strengthen its capability in developing proprietary content and enhance long-term competitiveness. The 2025 operational target ratio is set at 40% for R&D and 60% 
for publishing operations, with the Company transforming into an entertainment enterprise spanning both upstream and midstream in the pan-entertainment industry. For detailed 
information on operational items, please refer to page 59 of the “Wayi’s 2024 Annual Report.”
In 2024, Wayi ventured upstream into self R&D game planning and development, while also expanding opportunities for international IP negotiations and operational 
collaborations. Through a strategy that combines original creations with licensed content, the company aims to enhance control over content and strengthen long-term competitiveness. 
In the midstream, the Company has developed and operates digital platforms, including the game community platform “League Funny,” which attracts users through integrated 
interactive content such as gaming entertainment, news, live streaming, and community groups, thereby building stable traffic and a solid membership base to support project 
marketing. The “BanaBana Comprehensive Entertainment Platform” encompasses joint operations, online comic publishing, community management, and customer service.
Wayi adopts a cloud-based architecture for building its digital platforms, deploying and maintaining them through rented cloud servers, while leading overall platform marketing, 
traffic-driving strategies, and content integration. By connecting upstream content providers (such as game developers and music, film, and animation companies) with downstream end 
customers (members/players), the Company has established a digital platform-centered value chain business model that offers users a diverse and integrated entertainment experience.

Supplier Evaluation Principles

was rated B, and another supplier, due to certain indicators not meeting the standards, was rated C. According to the Company’s supplier management regulations, suppliers rated C 
will enter a continuous observation and guidance mechanism and may continue on a trial basis. However, if they fail to achieve a B rating within three evaluations (including the initial

all 20 partner suppliers this year were able to sustain stable cooperative relationships, with no major irregularities observed.

As Wayi offers digital entertainment services to gamers/consumers, ensuring cybersecurity is an important responsibility of the

The policy objective is to ensure the smooth operation of the Company’s business by preventing information or cybersecurity systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, destruction, alteration, 
or other infringements, thereby safeguarding their confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Specific management measures include classifying according to risk assessment results, taking into account the level

losses or impacts resulting from cybersecurity incidents, nor were there any complaints related to the infringement of customer privacy. If an urgent information security vulnerability arises, an announcement 
will be issued immediately and information security awareness will be communicated to all employees. At the same time, the number of unplanned network service interruptions will be quantified on an annual 
basis to meet Service Level Agreement targets. For detailed information on cybersecurity risk management, please refer to the company website under Corporate Governance/Cybersecurity Risk Management.

Personal Data Protection
Wayi regards the strict protection of personal data as an important social responsibility. To ensure the security and management of personal data and prevent it from being stolen, 
altered, damaged, lost, or leaked, the Company has established the “Personal Data Protection Policy” in accordance with the “Personal Data Protection Act”, internal controls, and 
relevant regulations, while continuously planning for a zero-trust policy. The scope of this policy applies not only to all employees of the Company but also to partner vendors and related 
personnel.
I. The collection, processing, and use of personal data are conducted only in compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act and relevant regulations.
II. The collection, processing, and use of personal data must comply with the principles of transparency and openness.
III. Personal data shall be collected, processed, and used only for the purposes of collection and within the specified purposes.
IV. Within the scope of business operations, only the minimum necessary personal data shall be collected.
V. The collection, processing, and use of any personal data whose legality has not been verified are strictly prohibited.
VI. When the specific purpose of personal data no longer exists or the retention period has expired, the processing and use of the personal data shall cease, or  the data shall be    
      deleted.
VII. Inform data subjects of the purpose and intended use of personal data collection prior to collecting it.
VIII. When personal data is modified or updated, it shall be handled proactively or upon the request of the data subject.
IX. When personal data is shared or disclosed to third parties, a relevant confidentiality agreement must be signed.
X. Implement outsourcing supervision to ensure that all outsourced operations comply with the Personal Data Protection Act and related regulations.
XI. Implement adequate security measures to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal data.
XII. Establish relevant procedures to respond to incidents of personal data being stolen, altered, damaged, lost, or leaked.
XIII. Establish a contact point to ensure that data subjects can exercise their rights or file complaints and inquiries.
XIV. When personal data is transmitted internationally, it shall be handled in accordance with legal and regulatory transfer restrictions.
XV. For personal data files that are of a special nature or are subject to legal provisions prohibiting disclosure, disclosure may be restricted or withheld in accordance with the  
       Personal Data Protection Act or other applicable laws.

Wayi International Digital Entertainment Co., Ltd. Human Rights Policy
Article 1 (Purpose of Establishment)
	
To protect and secure basic human rights, and to recognize and support the human rights protection philosophy and basic principles disclosed by international human 
rights conventions of the  “United Nations Universal Declaration of Human Rights”, “United Nations Global Compact” and “International Labour Conventions”, the Company and  
subsidiaries (hereinafter collectively referred to as “the Company”) has established this policy to realize the responsibility for the respect and protection of human rights as well  
as to comply with the local labor related laws and regulations of the location where the Company is located.
Article 2 (Protection of Human Rights at Workplace)
	
The Company prohibits any conducts violating human rights including forced/compulsory labor, human trafficking, child labor and sexual harassment, etc., and any 
differential treatment or any form of discrimination of employees due to race, rank, language, thought, religion, political party, native place, birth place, gender, sexual orientation, 
age, marriage status, appearance, facial features, physical and mental disability, astrological sign, blood type or union membership identity, etc. in order to properly protect the 
human rights of employees at the workplace, and to provide a working environment of dignity and equality.
Article 3 (Implementation of Same Salary for Same Jobs)
	
For the payment of salaries to employees, the Company provides no differential treatment due to gender or sexual orientation difference. For employees of the same job or 
value, the salary is paid equally. However, for proper reasons of seniority, reward/punishment, performance or factors other than gender or sexual orientation, such restriction shall 
not be applied.
Article 4 (Provision of Healthy and Safe Workplace)
	
The Company complies with occupational safety and health related laws and cares for the physical and mental health of employees through the organization of safety and 
health works, education and training, employee health examination or organization of various health promotion activities, etc. in order to improve or enhance the safety and 
health conditions of the working environment, thereby reducing the risk of occurrence of occupational accidents and providing a working environment of safety, healthy and 
sanitation to employees.
Article 5 (Support of Assembly Freedom)
	
The Company respects employees’ right to assemble and join various types of clubs and organizations, and also provides proper and diverse activities, in order to improve 
the balance between work and living of employees.
Article 6 (Promotion of Labor-Management Harmony)
	
The Company manages topics related to the labor rights and the interests of employees, and also implements labor-management communication through the methods of 
collective bargaining, periodic convention of labor-management meetings, provision of employee complaint and advice channels, etc. in order to protect and improve the rights 
and interests of employees.
Article 7 (Personal Data Protection)
	
The Company complies with personal data protection related laws, and ensures that the collection, processing and use of personal data comply with regulatory requirements, 
and also maintains and protects the rights and interests of personal data.
Article 8 (Human Rights Policy Promotion)
	
In addition to the support and implementation of human rights protection, the Company also establishes the “Corporate Sustainability Report” and encourages  
cooperating partners, including suppliers, to care and value the management of relevant risks of human rights topics jointly.
Article 9 (Implementation)
	
This policy shall be implemented after the approval of the General Manager, and the same rules shall be applied to amendments thereof.

Remuneration System

actively review the market remuneration environment at all times and make adjustments timely, in order to attract outstanding employees to service the Company.
In addition, we also maintain high correlation between the business performance and employees’ remuneration. According to Article 25 of the “Articles of Incorporation” of the

In addition, we have established a comprehensive performance evaluation system and Work Rules, in order to specify remuneration and reward/punishment standards clearly. The

Retirement system 
Advance notice of operational changes
The retirement system and regulations for employees are established according to relevant regulations of the “Labor Standards Act” and “Labor Pension Act”. Employees may apply 
for retirement under the following conditions: (1) Having worked for more than 15 years and aged 55. (2) Having worked for more than 25 years. (3) Having worked for more than 10 years 
and aged 60.
The Company does not have any employees subject to the old system; all employees are subject to the retirement system of the Labor Pension Act (new retirement system), under 
which the Company allocates 6% of their monthly salary to their Individual Labor Pension Account. For an employee who voluntarily contributes to his/her pension, an amount is withheld 
from his/her monthly salary based on the voluntary contribution rate (up to 6%) and allocated to the Individual Labor Pension Account under the Bureau of Labor Insurance. In 2024, the 
Company made full allocations to the retirement reserve fund, and no pension payments were made during the year.
The Company provides advance notice of the contract termination date in accordance with the provisions of Article 16-1 of the Labor Standards Act. The following provisions are 
complied with:
1. Where a worker has worked continuously for more than three months but less than one year, the notice shall be given ten days in advance.
2. Where a worker has worked continuously for more than one year but less than three years, the notice shall be given twenty days in advance.
3. Where a worker has worked continuously for more than three years, the notice shall be given thirty days in advance.
4. After receiving the advance notice referred to in the preceding paragraph, a worker may, during hours of work, ask for leave of absence for the purpose of finding a new job. Such 
leave of absence may not exceed 2 work days per week. Wages shall be paid during such leave of absence.

4.5 Healthy Workplace
Engaged in the gaming industry, Wayi is a type III company with low risk under the “Regulations of Occupational Safety and Health Management”. “Occupational safety and health 
personnel” have been appointed to supervise the implementation of the occupational safety and health management plan. To establish a healthy and safe workplace and protect 
employees from occupational disasters, Wayi has formulated the “Wayi Safety and Health Code of Conduct” in accordance with Article 34, Paragraph 1 of the “Occupational Safety 
and Health Act”. After the code was approved by the labor and management meeting, it was promulgated for all employees to follow.
According to Article 2 of the “Wayi Safety and Health Code of Conduct”: Laborers refer to individuals who are employed by the organization to provide their services and receive 
compensation in return; workers refer to laborers, self-employed workers, and other personnel who work under the instruction or oversight of the individual in charge of the workplace.
Workplace safety measures implemented by the Company are described in the following:
	
■
Employees responsible for general affairs completed the occupational safety and health training courses and obtained the training certificates. In accordance with Article 19 of 
the Occupational Safety and Health Education and Training Regulations, they will complete the refresher training for on-the-job safety and health education in April 2025.
	
■
New employees are required to provide a health examination report upon onboarding. The Company also arranges regular health examinations for employees to care for their 
health status. Employees under the age of 65 undergo a health examination every three years, while those aged 65 and above receive annual health examinations in accordance 
with regulatory requirements. The Company’s health examination items exceed regulatory standards and comply with the Regulations of the Labor Health Protection and the 
Personal Data Protection Act. Health examination data for items not required by law are retained by employees themselves to ensure personal privacy and data security.
	
■
Protection measures at the workplace:
(1)	 The Company’s workplace complies with office building safety regulations, and a firefighting system has been installed in accordance with the Fire Services Act. Annual 
firefighting drills are conducted in collaboration with the fire department.
(2)	 The glass conference rooms of the Company are furnished with materials certified by the competent authorities as “Grade 1 flame-resistant,” which prevent the 
occurrence of fire and extend the time available for evacuation.
(3)	 The filter element of the water dispenser in the tea room is replaced every quarter, and the water tower is cleaned by the building management unit to ensure the quality 
of drinking water.
(4)	 The air conditioning system is serviced quarterly, and the air conditioning system is updated to ensure air quality at the workplace.
(5)	 The office area and toilets are cleaned by the building management unit to maintain the tidiness and comfort of the workplace.
	
■
Personal safety protection measures:
(1)	 The Company has installed a security surveillance system to provide colleagues with a safe workplace.
(2)	 Access control is implemented by the building management, which also conducts regular patrols and inspections on various floors.
(3)	 Access control is implemented by the building management according to the work needs of various employees.
(4)	 The content of Article 16 (2) of the labor contract of the Company specifies that in case where there is any immediate hazard at a workplace, the Company shall instruct 
employees to retreat to a safe place immediately.
	
■
Labor-management meetings are convened once every 3 months to discuss OSH-related issues. Extraordinary meetings are convened in case of emergencies. In 2024, 4 labor-
management meetings were held. In addition, no work-related injuries or occupational illnesses took place during the year.
	
■
Education and training related to health, fire protection, and first aid is regularly organized.

5.1 Environmental Protection
Unlike the conventional manufacturing industry, the 
Company is part of the gaming industry, hence no hazardous 
waste or air pollution is generated. In response to global climate 
change, Wayi and all of its employees strive to decrease energy 
consumption and waste generation. While pursuing operational

environment management and cooperate with the government’s 
policy to enhance environmental management performance.
Wayi’s operations are office-based; the main energy 
consumption of the Company refers to externally purchased 
electricity. The water resource usage is classified into the water 
source for the headquarters from East District Business Office of 
Taipei Water Department (Xindian River Qingtan Weir) and the 
4th Management Division Dali Service Office of Taiwan Water 
Corporation (Liyutan Reservoir) for Taichung Office, respectively.

the Company is situated in a “Low-Medium risk (1-2)” region, and 
its water withdrawal does not have a major impact on the local 
water source. Furthermore, wastewater from the headquarters is 
discharged through the sewer system to the Neihu Wastewater 
Treatment Plant, which, after treatment, is ultimately released 
into the Keelung River. Wastewater from the Taichung branch is 
discharged through the sewer system to the Futian Water Resource 
Recovery Center and then released into the Han River, a tributary 
of the Dali River in the Wu River system. None of these discharges 
have caused significant impacts on the ecological environment.
