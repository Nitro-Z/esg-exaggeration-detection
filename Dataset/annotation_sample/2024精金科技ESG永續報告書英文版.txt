Message from the Chairman – 
Sustainable Development Strategy
In 2024, the world faced drastic and rapid changes. The outbreak of 
regional wars, geopolitical tensions, persistent inﬂation concerns, and 
major power rivalries have presented multiple challenges to interna-
tional trade. In response, we have invested more resources in techno-
logical innovation and diversiﬁed transformation, actively recruiting 
talent while expanding new product lines in e-paper, thereby substan-
tially solidifying HannsTouch’s core competitiveness for the next ﬁve to 
ten years.
Since its inception, HannsTouch Holdings Company has embraced the 
vision of “becoming the best partner in human intelligent living and a 
world-class distinguished company.” Focusing on touch applications 
and TFT backplane solutions, we are a professional manufacturer of 
sensors for high-end AMOLED touch panels. We consider it our vital 
mission “to be a long-term and trustworthy partner in the global touch 
and e-paper industries.”
Through the eﬀorts of our entire staﬀ, HannsTouch Holdings Company 
was honored with the Ministry of Economic Aﬀairs’ Energy Saving 
Benchmark Award for meritorious mention in 2023, and in 2024, 
received the “Healthy Workplace Certiﬁcation” from the Health Promo-
tion Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, as well as the 
Visionox Quality Improvement Award and Outstanding Contribution 
Award. In our pursuit of optimal operational performance, we remain 
committed to the co-existence and mutual prosperity of corporate 
growth and the ecological environment.
Looking back, the mobile phone market is projected to grow in 2024, and 
businesses in 5G-related ﬁelds are expected to beneﬁt accordingly. 
However, the implementation of a “Reciprocal Tariﬀs” policy following 
the change in the U.S. presidency in 2025 will add an element of uncer-
tainty for businesses in the mobile phone and 5G-related sectors, leading 
to a consumer market characterized by more observation than purchas-
ing.
HannsTouch Holdings Company is focused on enhancing the value of its 
core business, continuously developing high-end HIAA and high 
screen-to-body ratio full-screen technologies. In addition to meeting the 
demands of various generation AMOLED fabs for high-end smartphones 
and wearable products, we are also dedicated to developing higher-level

diverse clientele through steady and solid eﬀorts.
Currently, our G5.5 production line, in line with the company’s long-term

e-paper backplane production. We have successively developed prod-
ucts such as under-display optical ﬁngerprint sensor thin-ﬁlm transistor

Simultaneously, in the area of new technology research and develop-

expansion plan for our e-paper backplane-related technologies, which 
we have cultivated for many years, in the ﬁrst quarter of 2022, doubling 
our production capacity. In 2024 and 2025, we continue to move towards 
diversiﬁed transformation, expanding the application scenarios of our 
products and advancing into high-value-added ﬁelds, striving to create 
maximum operational beneﬁts.
Furthermore, we closely monitor environmental sustainability issues, 
hoping to internalize ESG, GRI Standards, and the United Nations SDGs 
into our corporate culture and core values. Through investments in clean 
energy, we remain committed to energy conservation and carbon reduc-
tion, emphasizing issues such as labor rights, environmental protection, 
and health. This proactive momentum demonstrates that for our compa-
ny, implementing ESG sustainability work is no longer a matter of “when 
to do it,” but of “doing it now.” Therefore, we completed ESG vendor 
certiﬁcation in May 2023, continuously enhancing information transpar-
ency and fully disclosing our sustainability solutions to promote sustain-
able operations.
While the international situation in 2024 continues to face numerous 
challenges and uncertainties, HannsTouch Holdings Company will 
persist in providing diversiﬁed services and wholeheartedly creating 
greater value for our shareholders. We will, as always, regard our employ-
ees, customers, and business partners as our most important people, 
and are committed to sound corporate governance and fulﬁlling our 
social responsibilities as a corporate citizen. We thank all our sharehold-
ers for their trust and support in HannsTouch Holdings Company and 
look forward to advancing together in 2025 to create a long-term and 
prosperous future.

Report Profile and Scope
HannsTouch Holdings Company (hereinafter referred to as HannsTouch, 
the Company, or we), in pursuit of corporate sustainable operations and 
to enhance information transparency, has issued the 2024 Sustainability 
Report (hereinafter referred to as this Report). Through the issuance of 
this Report, we explain to stakeholders the measures and performance 
undertaken towards our sustainable operation goals, with a continued 
focus on corporate governance and integrity management, implementa-
tion of environmental protection, construction of a friendly workplace 
and occupational safety measures, as well as enhancement of employee 
capabilities and work performance, enabling sustained growth in opera-
tional performance and proﬁt.
HannsTouch Holdings Company hopes that stakeholders will continue 
to follow us and provide valuable suggestions, allowing the company to 
take great strides forward on the path of implementing corporate 
sustainable operations.
• Reporting Period
The year 2024 (from January 1, 2024, to December 31, 2024). This report 
discloses HannsTouch’s various ESG sustainability management 
policies, risk assessments, materiality issue prioritization, responses 
and list management, and ESG action performance information, while 
some content is included to enhance the reader’s comprehension of the 
report’s information.
For the full year of 2024, and additionally, to ensure the completeness of 
the disclosed data, any content that spans operational activities of 
diﬀerent years will be explained separately within the text of this report.
• ESG Information Disclosure Boundary
The information contained herein is primarily based on HannsTouch 
Holdings Company’s locations in Taiwan, including the Tainan head-
quarters and the Taipei oﬃce, with disclosure of relevant material 
topics oriented towards HannsTouch’s employees and external 
customers, suppliers, and investors.
Financial performance ﬁgures are calculated in New Taiwan Dollars 
(NTD) or thousands of NTD. The reliability of ﬁnancial data is publicly 
released after certiﬁcation by accountants and is presented using 
commonly accepted text and numerical descriptions.

1. Practicing Sustainable Management
1.1 Sustainable Development Committee Organi-
zation and Responsibilities
In response to global trends and government policies, the Board of 
Directors revised the Corporate Social Responsibility Best Practice 
Principles to Sustainable Development Best Practice Principles on 
December 21, 2021, and oﬃcially designated the Corporate Social 
Responsibility Report as the Sustainability Report. This facilitates the 
integration of ESG concepts with the company’s internal systems and 
communication with corresponding stakeholders. To this end, the CSR 
project organization was restructured into the Sustainable Development 
Project Team, led by the Chairman, with the President appointed as the 
project manager. “Environmental,” “Social Sustainability,” and “Corpo-
rate Governance” groups were established, with various functional units 
within the company responsible for speciﬁc ESG aspects, consolidating 
management goals and indicators for environment (E), social responsi-
bility (S), and corporate governance (G) related to ESG sustainable devel-
opment, and linking them with daily operations, integrating them into 
routine work.
The Sustainable Development Team periodically identiﬁes stakehold-
ers, collects and reviews issues of their concern to be presented together 
at the annual meeting, ensuring all material considerations have been 
covered. After conﬁrmation by the committee, action plans, perfor-
mance, and future strategies are developed and reported to the Chair-
man for presentation to the Board of Directors.

1.3 Materiality Analysis and Generation Process
To enable our company to better understand and respond to the issues of concern to our stakeholders, we primarily collect sustainability-related 
issues through multiple methods such as referencing Sustainable Development Goals, industry regulations and standards, and organizational 
annual objectives. In accordance with the principles of Inclusivity, Materiality, Responsiveness, and Impact from the AA1000 Accountability Princi-
ples (2018) and the SES Stakeholder Engagement Standard, we work with external experts to analyze issues of concern through the steps of identiﬁ-
cation, prioritization, conﬁrmation, and review. This comprehensively examines the sustainability performance, and we disclose the impact 
situations, management strategies, and practical conditions for each material topic one by one, ensuring HannsTouch’s sustainable development 
goals and strategies remain consistent with stakeholder expectations, and that our sustainability information disclosures meet the needs of 
stakeholders, thereby strengthening the eﬀectiveness of external communication. The process for generating material issues is as follows:

responses, a total of 63 valid questionnaires were collected, for a recovery 
rate of 31.5%, to understand the issues of primary concern to stakeholders.

Step 5: Prioritize Sustainability Issues
Based on a comprehensive analysis of the scores for concern and impact

Step 6: Adjust Material Issues

and Safety,’ and ‘Customer Relationship Management’ were included as the

2.1 Company Proﬁle
HannsTouch is a leading global supplier of AMOLED touch sensors and TFT 
thin-ﬁlm transistor driver backplanes. With high-end technology and 
complete solutions, we serve world-renowned brands and manufacturers, 
assisting customers in developing new products, creating new markets, 
and continuously expanding the diverse applications of touch and 
e-paper.
Founded in 1999, oﬃcially listed on the stock exchange in 2002 (Stock 
Code: 3049), and in June 2023, “HannsTouch Solution Incorporated” was 
oﬃcially renamed “HannsTouch Holdings Company.” In addition to the 
eﬃcient use of the G5.5 production line and the cutting-edge technology 
for direct investment on 0.25mm ultra-thin glass, HannsTouch is also 
committed to providing customers with value-added services, creating a 
new generation of touch and e-paper product applications and service 
business models.
HannsTouch’s touch solutions can be applied to diﬀerent end-product 
ﬁelds such as AMOLED and TFT-LCD displays. Product applications cover 
wearable devices, mobile phones, tablet computers, notebook computers, 
all-in-one computers, and industrial display products; TFT backplane 
solutions can be applied to products such as electronic shelf labels, 
e-books, and digital signage.

3.5 Customer Privacy
At the beginning of cooperation with customers, to ensure that the trade secrets disclosed by both parties are properly protected and 
utilized by the other party, HannsTouch requires the signing of a conﬁdentiality agreement with the customer. In view of this, the 
company requires all employees to sign conﬁdentiality documents upon employment, demanding that employees and cooperating 
customers and partners adhere to the ethical policy of “strictly safeguarding personal privacy and protecting the security of commercial 
information of customers and suppliers.”
Related measures include:
(1) From the moment a customer provides a design to HannsTouch, it is converted to an internal customer code to prevent leakage of 
direct customer information. After formal production begins, it is further converted internally to an internal part number for ease of 
communication.
(2) Each relevant unit within the factory has its own dedicated information area, and unauthorized access by other units is not permit-
ted.

To achieve responsible and sustainable procurement, HannsTouch Holdings Company has formulated a conﬂict minerals policy and commits to

5.6 Climate Change Strategy and Management
In recent years, the impacts of climate change have become apparent around the world. Abnormal weather such as storms, 
droughts, and global warming not only bring huge shocks to the world economy but also pose a threat to corporate operations and 
production. Faced with increasingly severe climate issues, governments, societies, and industries worldwide are actively discussing 
climate solutions. For businesses, it is even more necessary to face the risks and potential opportunities brought by climate change, 
enhance their resilience to climate risks, and strengthen corporate resilience to reduce the impacts and losses caused by climate 
change.
HannsTouch Holdings Company recognizes the necessity of facing climate change and is actively enhancing our company’s climate 
resilience. In addition to being committed to environmental protection and implementing energy conservation and carbon reduc-
tion strategies, we also refer to the “Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures” (TCFD) framework to initially establish our 
company’s climate risk management framework. Through the four core elements of governance, strategy, risk management, and 
metrics and targets, we identify potential climate-related risks and opportunities, assess their possible impacts, and reduce the 
harm brought by risks through proactive understanding.
TCFD Core Framework
• HannsTouch’s ESG promotion team convenes relevant members to discuss, on an ad-hoc basis, the potential 
internal and external impacts of various issues on the organization, including identifying and evaluating climate 
change risks and responding to climate impacts. After identifying climate-related impacts, meetings are held with 
senior executives (the President) to discuss climate-related risks and opportunities. Improvement suggestions and 
measures are proposed for the potential harm caused by risks to adapt to and mitigate climate financial risks, and 
to identify corresponding climate financial opportunities. 
• The ESG promotion team reports the full-year ESG performance to the President, and the President confirms the 
short, medium, and long-term goals, including climate change-related issues.
• Our company incorporates the potential impacts of climate change into our overall operational considerations. We 
estimate the probability and degree of impact of risks and formulate risk response and mitigation plans. We

6.3 Compensation and Beneﬁts
Material Issue→Compensation and Beneﬁts
• Policy: Competitive compensation and beneﬁts to attract and retain talent. 
• Goal: To balance industrial competitiveness, the overall economy, and the sustainable operation of the 
corporate culture, the company, based on its operational status and management objectives, has estab-
lished ‘Salary Management Regulations’ and ‘Work Rules’ - beneﬁts regulations, to provide competitive 
compensation and beneﬁts to attract and retain talent. 
• Commitment: Utilize due diligence and early warning communication methods to improve the 
employee compensation and beneﬁts system, and to allocate amounts for year-end bonuses, employee 
proﬁt-sharing, etc., based on operational proﬁt performance. 
• Management Measures: 
(1) Provide a stable employee compensation structure in accordance with the company’s remuneration 
management system. 
(2) Annually review market salary levels to maintain a position in the top 50% of the industry, and continu-
ously improve employee satisfaction. 
(3) Plan a mechanism to link the remuneration of the Board of Directors and senior executives with ESG 
performance. 
(4) Allocate employee welfare funds in accordance with the provisions of the ‘Employee Welfare Fund Act’ 
to organize welfare activities. 
(5) Conduct performance evaluations and interviews to improve work behavior, enhance performance, and 
cultivate and develop employees’ individual abilities.
Management
Approach
A sound human resource base is the key to the sustainable operation of HannsTouch Holdings Company. To attract outstanding talent, the company 
provides market-competitive compensation and beneﬁts, rewarding employees who create performance and make long-term contributions. The 
company has established “Salary Management Regulations” and, upholding the philosophy of treating employees equally, has planned a transpar-
ent evaluation and reward/punishment system. This fosters a positive atmosphere of fair competition among colleagues, making them willing to 
invest more eﬀort in their work performance, which in turn further shapes a corporate culture at HannsTouch Holdings Company that attracts and 
retains talent. At the same time, colleagues feel a stronger commitment to the company and actively participate in company aﬀairs.

Secondly, regarding promotion channels, when the company’s promotion information is transparent and open, employees with good work perfor-

6.6.2 Hazard Identiﬁcation, Risk Assessment, and Accident Investiga-
tion
The company has established an “EHS Risk and Opportunity Assessment 
Procedure,” whereby the EHS promotion committee member of each 
unit annually conducts safety and health hazard identiﬁcation, risk and 
opportunity assessment for all operations within the unit. This includes 
hazardous workplaces, chemical substances used in processes, or other 
work activity safety assessments. The assessment results are reported to 
the EHS management system’s management review meeting for review 
and reporting.
In 2024, a total of 1 opportunity for improvement on a risk hazard was 
identiﬁed:
Due to excessive pressure within the chemical supply pipeline, the 
hazard of pipe rupture during chemical ﬁlling increased, leading to an 
increased risk of personnel contact with hazardous substances. The 
SCUB 1F ﬂuorine system chemical supply pipeline improvement project 
has been completed, reducing the hazard risk of pipe rupture during 
ﬁlling to an acceptable risk level.
To prevent the use of hazardous substances from aﬀecting employee 
health, in addition to prioritizing the use of low-hazard chemicals and 
establishing local exhaust ventilation, if new chemicals need to be 
purchased or chemicals used in the process need to be changed, the 
factory’s EHS operational change management procedure must be 
followed. A hazard risk level assessment for the use of the relevant 
chemical must be conducted, and it can only be used after approval by 
the relevant units. In accordance with the chemical usage situation, 
personal respiratory protective equipment that meets regulatory 
requirements is provided. For processes with higher risks requiring 
respiratory protection, corresponding ﬁt testing is conducted to ensure 
its eﬀectiveness.
Based on the actual operational hazard conditions and regulatory 
requirements, a qualiﬁed monitoring institution is regularly commis-
sioned to conduct workplace environment monitoring to control the 
status of hazardous factors in the work environment. Based on the 
monitoring results, improvements are made to the worksite to reduce 
the occurrence of occupational diseases. The main types of chemical 
hazards are organic solvents and acidic chemical substances. Chemical 
factor monitoring items include ethanol, ethylene glycol, phosphoric

6.6.3 Worker Participation, Consultation, and Communication in Occupational Safety and Health
To protect the rights and interests of employees, our company, in accordance with the requirements of the “Occupational Safety and Health 
Management Regulations,” has established an OHS Committee. The Safety and Health Committee has a total of 20 members, including 1 chair-
man, 1 executive secretary, 2 occupational safety and health personnel, 8 committee members, and 8 labor representatives, with the proportion 
of labor representatives being greater than one-third.
The responsibility of the Safety and Health Committee is to improve the level of safety and health management and achieve safety management 
goals by planning, implementing, evaluating, and improving the OHS environment. The Occupational Safety and Health Committee meets 
regularly once every quarter. Topics include: how to identify and eliminate hazards, risk assessment, workplace environment monitoring 
measures, accident investigation and audits, health management, occupational disease prevention and health promotion matters, self-inspec-
tions and safety and health audit matters, safety and health education and training, and audit standards for contractors and suppliers.
Labor representatives (workers) participate in every meeting.

6.6.4 Worker Training in Occupational Safety 
and Health
To allow employees to work in a healthy and safe work environment, 
we require colleagues to be trained and obtain professional industri-
al safety licenses. General colleagues are required to obtain a 
six-hour course certiﬁcation. We regularly conduct industrial safety 
training for new employees, current employees, and contract 
workers, so that employees can learn the necessary safety knowl-
edge for their work.
To avoid the recurrence of similar accidents, our company also 
regularly conducts accident education and training. Through 
accident investigation and analysis methods, we help colleagues 
understand the root causes of accidents, reduce the occurrence of 
industrial safety incidents, and enhance employee health and safety.
By eﬀectively preventing and controlling risks, we regularly identify 
and evaluate potential emergency situations and events, and plan 
speciﬁc emergency plans and response procedures to minimize the 
impact of possible risks. This includes: emergency reporting, 
employee notiﬁcation and evacuation plans, and employee training 
and drills. Relevant drills must be conducted at least once a year or in 
accordance with relevant laws. This includes ﬁre detection and 
ﬁreﬁghting equipment, unobstructed emergency exits, adequate 
escape exit facilities, contact information for emergency personnel, 
and recovery plans.
In addition, we arrange external training courses for forklift opera-
tors, ﬁxed crane operators, high-pressure gas container operators, 
and high-pressure gas speciﬁc equipment operators. For chemi-
cal-using units, we conduct two sessions annually of training and 
awareness on the labeling and general knowledge of dangerous and 
hazardous materials, as well as the use of personal protective equip-
ment. We also hold ﬁre emergency response drills twice a year to 
enhance personnel safety awareness and improve the safety of the 
operating environment.
2. For occupational safety and health training and awareness courses, 
employees can apply to the Industrial Safety Oﬃce to participate 
freely, with all costs covered by the company.
• Conduct new employee safety and health education and training, 
and on-the-job education and training for employees; organize 
external training courses for special operation personnel to enhance 
employee safety awareness and ensure personnel operational safety.
• Require contractors to undergo industrial safety education and 
training, and contract personnel must regularly undergo on-the-job 
education and training to enhance personnel safety awareness.
• The annual occupational safety and health management plan 
includes an emergency response drill plan. Regular emergency 
accident response drills are conducted for each unit to enhance 
personnel’s emergency accident response capabilities.
• Regularly conduct safety and health awareness campaigns to 
increase employees’ safety and health concepts.
• Regularly send ﬁrst aid medical personnel or EHS dedicated person-
nel to participate in job-related education and training.

6.7.2 Donation Activities
In 2024, the Human Resources Department of HannsTouch Holdings Company initiated a long-term Red Envelope donation activity in cooperation 
with World Vision to assist families in special situations in Taiwan. The main support is for medical services, nutritional assistance, and education-
al assistance, providing medical expenses, living allowances, nutritional supplements, and health insurance installment subsidies. It provides 
basic daily necessities, basic meals, milk powder, oatmeal, etc., for impoverished children. Furthermore, NT$7,500 is subsidized per student each 
semester for tuition and miscellaneous fees, to cover after-school care/tutoring fees, as well as uniforms/sports uniforms/shoes/school applica-
