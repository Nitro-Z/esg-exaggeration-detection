About the Report
Scope and Boundary
DACIN Construction Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "DACIN" or "DACIN Construction") recognizes 
the importance of corporate sustainability and responds R. O. C. Government's anticipation for corporate 
social responsibility, in view of this, since 2017, initiated on the preparation of the first Sustainability Report 
(formerly known as the corporate social responsibility report ;CSR report) with the global information 
disclosure framework for sustainability voluntarily, which is the 5th report for the period from January 1 to 
December 31, 2021, only except that some of issues and information retrospect to ones over past years. 
The operation of DACIN (Taiwan) becomes the pivot, except 'financial performance' part is provided 
within the range of the incorporated entity including subsidiaries DACIN Development, DACIN Integration 
Technology and DACIN Asia; besides, the statistical results were mostly listed by historic changing trend to 
express achievements across CSR aspects completely.
Reporting Standard
For assurance of open, transparent and integral principle, with consideration of current condition of 
domestic CSR report preparation, DACIN selected GRI Sustainability Reporting Standards (GRI Standards) 
issued by the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) and followed core options therein as the disclosure principle 
for 2021 Sustainability Report, delivering CSR strategies, input and performance to stakeholders. 
All financial performance and other information presented in this report are subject to the consolidated 
financial statement that is issued in public with CPA signature; all financial figures are calculated in New 
Taiwan Dollar for preparation of financial statement pursuant to IFRSs (International Financial Reporting 
Standards) for listed companies.

About the Report

"2021 Sustainability Report of DACIN Construction Co., 
Ltd.", DACIN's 5th voluntary made sustainability report 
pursuant to GRI Guideline (Global Reporting Initiative), 
consisted of analysis on the issues that a wide range of 
stakeholders have concerned, addressing and balancing 
their needs, reviewing non-financial performance and 
combing behavior across realms in economy, society, 
environmental sustainability with an integrative course of 
making a report.
In early 2022, The Global Risk Report 2022 published 
by The World Economic Forum (WEF) pointed out that 
environment-related risks accounted for five of the top 
seven risks, including "failure of climate action" in the 
first place, "extreme weather" in the second place, 
"biodiversity loss" in the third place, "human-made 
environmental damage" in the seventh place, and "lack 
of natural resources" in the eighth place. Among them, 
"failure of climate action" has jumped to the top risk, 
showing that if the national government and enterprises 
do not actively focus on energy saving, carbon reduction 
and green energy technologies. In addition, the war in 
Russia and Ukraine has triggered the rise of energy, food 
and commodity prices, and Taipower has been unable 
to compete with the pressure of high international fuel 
costs. The Ministry of Economic Affairs' tariff review 
committee will temporarily approve an average tariff 
increase of 8.4% on June 27, mainly targeting high-
voltage and extra-high-voltage consumers with a 15% 
increase.  As a result, climate change adaptation and 
energy management will be extremely important to the 
sustainable operation of enterprises.
DACIN understands that solving the climate crisis 
and implementing sustainable development is a 
matter of great urgency. Therefore, the disclosure 
and management framework recommended by 
Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures 
(TCFD) will be integrated into the management 
foundation of the climate change issue in 2022, and 
education and training will also be held. In addition, 
we conducted training to raise awareness of climate 
change and ESG, identify potential operational risks 
and opportunities, and develop short-, medium-, and 
long-term management strategies and goals for climate 
change issues. For example, the Nangang Machinery 
Factory's public housing site obtained the international 
BS 8001 certification for environmental economy; the 
GLOW&GLORY case suggested the owner to adopt the 
aluminum formwork recycling method to reduce waste 
and mitigate the impact of labor shortage; and the 
demolition project of Fubon Insurance Building assisted 
the owner to implement UL2799 (zero waste to landﬁll) 
policy; the part of the TSMC plant adopts the precast 
modular method to reduce material loss and waste; the 
Taichung Commercial Bank Ofﬁce and Hotel construction 
base adopts permeable pavement and water ﬂooding to 
collect surface seepage water to improve the soil layer's 
ability to store rainwater; in the hope of moving the 
construction industry toward a green, environmentally 
friendly, and sustainable industry.

UL2799 Zero Waste Landfill Policy - Fubon Property & Casualty 
Insurance Co. Headquarters Building In-Situ Demolition Project
UL2799Zero Waste Landﬁll Introduction
All waste streams within the boundary must be managed, inspected and audited for compliance to conﬁrm that the 
waste has been properly recycled, reused and converted.
Demolition of old buildings using whole building cutting 
instead of traditional crushing and demolition method
In order to maintain a safe, low-noise, low-pollution, low-vibration demolition 
method and to avoid affecting the neighboring houses, the original demolition 
plan of this case adopts the whole building cutting method, with a tower crane 
set up on site to transport the cut concrete blocks out to a designated site for 
crushing, which can effectively reduce the impact on the surrounding environment 
and no free-falling slag, and enhance the recycling of waste. The possibility of 
recycling is also enhanced.

(1) UL's certification calculation condition is non-incineration non-landfill conversion rate > 90%; 
(2) Conversion rate of more than 80% of the conversion level (including waste to energy) is eligible to use the

1-1-2  Concerns and Impact Analysis
Dacin compiled its CSR report by considering the relevance of each GRI Standards topic to its own business, as 
well as international industry trends, domestic industry concerns, and major industry issues, and compiled 22 
sustainable issues related to Dacin's operations.
In order to obtain complete stakeholder feedback, Dacin sent a survey to all stakeholders that included 22 
sustainability issues. 230 valid questionnaires were collected to ensure that stakeholders' concerns about each 
sustainability issue were effectively collected; 12 valid questionnaires were also collected to gauge the views of 
senior executives on the impact of each sustainability issue on the company's operations. The opinions of external 
stakeholders and internal officials are compiled, tallied, and analyzed, and a matrix of major issues and their 
importance is drawn up to confirm the importance of each issue. After evaluation and discussion, Dacin selected 
the issues with high-higj, high-medium and medium-high impact on operations and the environment, economy 
and society, as well as stakeholders' concerns as the major issues for this year. Therefore, Dacin's major issues for 
2021 are: regulatory compliance, business performance, risk management, sustainability strategy and vision, 
quality and customer satisfaction management, brand value, innovation and technology development, supply 
chain management, employee health and safety, and pollution prevention, etc. These are the key issues revealed 
in this report.

2-1-3   Main Business
Dacin is mainly engaged in the construction of civil roads, bridges, harbors, buildings, painting, etc., waterproof 
grouting, pre-filled piles and drilling, pre-force and ready-mixed concrete, including public works, such as 
MRT projects, highways, civil bridge projects, etc., and commercial buildings and factories, such as hotels, 
shopping malls, hospitals, schools, factories, etc., and also assists in the implementation of new towns, new 
urban development, urban renewal and redevelopment, and maintenance of communities in accordance with 
the government's urban development plan. We also assist in the implementation of new towns, new urban

The DacinBoard of Directors is the highest level of corporate governance and represents the shareholders in 
making operational decisions. Its responsibilities include the appointment and removal of the chief executive 
officer, the formulation of business plans, the approval of important bylaws and contracts, the approval of

the Establishment and Compliance of Independent Directors of Public Companies. Each director receives regular

3-1  Innovative Technology Development
Throughout the 50 years since Dacin’s founding, it has undergone growth, reform, inheritance and development,

3-1-1  Technology and Patent Support
Dacin acknowledges the fact that construction is a labour-intensive and highly-hazardous industry, alongside 
with the country’s declining birthrate and aging population, there is severe shortage of construction labor. In 
recent years, Dacin has focused on technological research and development in hopes of using new technology 
to increase work efficiency and improve workplace safety. In response to the difficulties in the construction of 
large diameter piles in hard pebble layers, a series of geological and hydraulic tests have been completed at 
the Taichung site in 2021. After detailed analysis and discussion with professional consultants and experts,

quality, improve the work rate, reduce the wear and tear of machinery, and save energy and carbon in the future 
construction of large diameter complete pipe foundation piles in similar geological conditions.

required, assistance in calculations and analysis of construction problems, confirmation of respective deficiencies 
and enhancement methods, solving of challenging technical problems and errrors, producing high-quality and 
valuable engineering solutions, approximately 72 technical support cases were completed for ongoing and 
pitching projects in 2021.

Based on “Project Procurement Outsourcing Methods”, the budget control and procurement team are in charge of

Main Scope of work

Budget control team to outsource, evaluate materials, complete “Construction Budget Sheet”.

Outsourcing
Submit “Procurement Outsourcing Request form” to procurement team, after veriﬁcation of

Dacin constructs different structures, basic engineering and buildings. Its upstream businesses include basic 
engineering, raw materials, structural, renovation, mechanical, electrical and industrial engineering etc. Supply chain 
mainly manages suppliers (Materials, Equipment, Consultation, Design etc.) and labor contractors. 
Dacin manages suppliers based on “Subcontractor Management Operating Methods”, for suppliers, labor contractors of 
construction works, processes include subcontractor assessment and registration, bidding and contracting, evaluation and 
treatment. The company actively searches for new contractors and suppliers. An assessment of the new supplier has to be 
conducted before requesting for quotation. Assessment includes the company’s license, registration certiﬁcation, latest tax 
payment records, and account statements in recent year, portfolio of similar project nature etc. After assessment has been 
approved, log data into “List of qualiﬁed subcontractors” and proceed to negoatiate and compare prices.
Dacin conducts assessment for subcontractors with project value more than ﬁve hundred thousand. When project has 
accumulated to 50% of contract value or when project has been completed, personnel should evaluate subcontractor 
and report to supervisor for endorsement according to “Subcontractor Evaluation Form” ,the evaluation item includes 
aspects such as environmental protection, industrial safety and waste disposal. Suppliers with excelent or failed 
results will be reassessed by supervising president. 
Dacin will compile evaluation results of recent 3 years and forward to procurement team for reference. Suppliers 
rated excellent based on evaluation results can be prioritized as negotiating suppliers, reserved amount 
can abide by original bidding conditions and reduce by 50%. Should the negotiated amount be higher than other 
qualified contractors by 5%, project can be awarded to contractor after assessment.  
In order to reduce costs and increase efﬁciency, Dacin controls raw material price, 
adopts integrated procurement methods such as weekly monitoring of raw material 
price ﬂuctuations in the international market, evaluating the best purchasing time, or 
bulk purchasing for construction sites that are closely situated in order to minimize the 
transport costs for raw materials, and achieve the objective of joint pricing.
Dacin implements most procurement operations at location of construction site, local procurement proportion in recent 
three years has achieved 100%. Money spent for local purchase is 100% of total purchase amount in year 2021. In order 
to reduce price ﬂuctuations that affect costs, In addition, Dacin tracks and monitors price changes of signiﬁcant 
construction materials and mechanical equipment; controls contractor labor costs without compromising quality and 
duration of construction works. The development of Dacin and its upstream businesses are heavily influenced by the 
market, hence the correlating relationship between the company and its suppliers. It aims to strengthen the 
partnership between both parties to create a sustainable supply chain for the future.

4-1-3  Human Rights
Dacin’s working principles strictly adhere to government laws and regulations. Workplace regulations, Company 
and on-site attendance, employee leave, evaluation and promotion, remuerations and compensation policies are 
generated based on the Labor Law, Gender work equality law, Occupational Safety related laws and regulations 
to facilitate employes daily work. To avoid harassment due to gender differences, company enforces respective 
standards of behavior to manage inappropriate behavior.
Dacin adheres to “United Nations Universal Declaration of Human Rights”, “United Nations Guiding Principles 
on Business and Human Rights”, “United Nations Global Covenent” and “United Nationals International Labor 
Organization” principles, respects basic human rights that are acknowledged internationally and develops its 
human rights policies. Based on Employment Service Act Article 5, Dacin condones all discriminatory behavior 
inclusive of racial, class, language, ideology, religion, political party, nationality, place of birth, gender, sexual 
orientation, age, marriage, appearance, senses, physical and mental disabilities or discrimination against former 
trade union members. In 2021, company had educated and enforced its human rights policies upon newly 
recruited employees, with a total of 163 employees,82 hours. 
Hazard identiﬁcation and risk evaluation of workplace abuse prevention
In order to strengthen employee-management relations, enhance cooperation between both parties, increase 
work efficiency, Dacin organizes meetings to promote effective communication, sets up suggestion boxes for 
employees to submit feedback, express their opinions and provide opportunities for them to voice out. These 
motivate and unite employees. Four labor-management meetings have been held in 2021. The main contents of 
the communication include changing the application for printing to online application; opening up the labor and 
safety operations to personnel from other departments to solve the labor and safety manpower gap; initiating the 
installation of asset management software; agreeing to overtime work; night work for women; and extending the 
period of special leave.

4-3-1  Occupational Safety Maintenance
The occupational safety and health policy of Da Cin is “Work safety for all 
employees, zero accidents at work”, and make the 5 promises for achieving 
sustainable development and fulﬁll social responsibilities. Except for this policy, the 
company also actively cooperates with government regulations on occupational 
safety, health, and environmental protection; implement relevant policies and 
research the innovation of the safety, health, and environmental equipment for 
creating a more comfortable working environment that achieves the goals of zero 
harm on safety and health, zero pollution on environmental protection.
5 Promises on Occupational Safety and Health
Da Cin officially introduced OHSAS 18001 and TOSHMS occupational safety and health management system in 
2010. The system covers the head office and all employees and workers at the construction site. By assessing 
operational risks and PDCA implementation mode, establish occupational safety management concepts to 
prevent occupational disasters, and regularly accept SGS external audits to continuously maintain the validity of 
the certificate. In 2019, in line with the international trend and the policy of the Occupational Safety and Health 
Administration, it passed the ISO45001 international standard of occupational safety and health management 
system and the Taiwan Occupational Safety and Health Management System (TOSHMS) on November 20th of the 
same year and obtained certificates to meet the latest certification requirements.

To ensure the reliability of the operation of the security system, our company introduced the Labor Safety Quality 
E-based Audit System in 2013 to carry out the safety and health E-based audit on the construction site for 
improving the quality and efficiency of labor safety inspection and ISO45001&TOSHMS internal audit, ensuring 
immediate notification and follow-up improvement of the deficiency, and effectively grasping the labor safety 
management situation on the construction site. Feedback related units through Informatization, as a reference for 
manufacturer evaluation, for decision-making reference for strengthening the key management of security and 
health, and continuous improvement of the system
The occupational safety and health management structure of Da Cin is divided into two occupational safety 
management notification systems: engineering and labor safety with the chief executive as the chief officer: 
The engineering system is responsible for the promotion of site safety and health management affairs with the 
director of the engineering-related business department/supervisory assistant; the labor safety system takes the 
Occupational Safety and Health Management Committee as the supervision and evaluation unit, and the labor 
safety office performs the power and responsibility management work. The principal of the construction site 
needs to meet the management requirements that follow the engineering system and the labor safety system; 
carry out the on-site labor safety operation management, and supervise the safety and health duties of on-site 
supervisors and management personnel, and occupational safety and health personnel. If the worker finds that 
there is an immediate danger in performing the duties, he can stop the operation and retreat to a safe place 
on his own without endangering the safety of other workers, and immediately report to the direct supervisor. 
Following the Occupational Safety and Health Law, the company shall not dismiss the worker, transfer to another 
job, fail to pay wages during the period of suspension of operations, or other disadvantages.
To ensure the suitability and efficiency of the occupational safety and health management system and 
continuously improving, the chief executive officer is responsible to hold the annual management review meeting, 
and the labor safety office proposes the suitability of the management process of the occupational safety and 
health system, the execution results of various system goals and the performance analysis report of each main 
operation process. If necessary, put forward a plan for control measures for occupational safety and health risk 
opportunities (In connection with the 5 risk measures, when the level reaches 4 or 5, propose measures to reduce 
risks and evaluate the risk estimates after control; control measures to reduce risks and the risk is estimated after 
drawing and control) or the occupational safety and health management plan planning table to adjust or update 
the occupational safety and health system management process and various operating procedures, and to ensure 
that the planning results and continuous improvement goals are achieved.

operations and environmental protection may cooperate well.

be adjusted to comply with the construction safety and

5-1-3  Promotion of Circular Economy
The construction industry consumes most of the world's resources and creates a large amount of construction waste 
disposal problems after demolition. For this reason, we are the ﬁrst company to demonstrate a circular economy in 
the construction industry through the Taipei Nangang Depot Public Housing project and will obtain the international 
circular economy standard BS 8001 certiﬁcation in 2021, which is the ﬁrst construction project in China to obtain the 
circular economy certiﬁcation and implement the concept of circular economy. In addition to creating a livable city, 
we are also working towards global environmental sustainability. In the future, we will continue to introduce various 
new and innovative circular economy models and increase the proportion of circular economy beneﬁts year by year 
to encourage green procurement of recycled building materials and circular design, providing richer, more innovative 
and more economically efﬁcient elements of construction circular economy.
The concept of "Circular Economy" is the 5Rs: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Rethink, and Repair. Dacin took the 
Nangang Depot community houses as a demonstration base and combined with "recyclable and recycling 
building materials", "resource recycling", "waste recycling", "flexible modules" and other strategies to 
promote the recycling and sustainability of social housing resources. 
Policy objective: Starting from 2018 (base year), for the ratio of using environmentally 
friendly recycled paper certified by FSC/PEFC, the objective for the first phase is to 
increase by 8% per year until it reaches (2024) 50%.

NATURE AND SCOPE OF THE ASSURANCE/VERIFICATION 
SGS Taiwan Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as SGS) was commissioned by DACIN CONSTRUCTION CO., LTD. 
(hereinafter referred to as DACIN) to conduct an independent assurance of the Sustainability Report for 2021. 
The scope of the assurance, based on the SGS Sustainability Report Assurance methodology, included the 
sampled text, and data in accompanying tables, contained in the report presented during verification 
(2022/06/24~2022/07/14). SGS reserves the right to update the assurance statement from time to time depending 
on the level of report content discrepancy of the published version from the agreed standards requirements.   
 
INTENDED USERS OF THIS ASSURANCE STATEMENT 
This Assurance Statement is provided with the intention of informing all DACIN’s Stakeholders. 
 
RESPONSIBILITIES 
The information in the DACIN’s Sustainability Report of 2021 and its presentation are the responsibility of the 
directors or governing body (as applicable) and management of DACIN. SGS has not been involved in the 
preparation of any of the material included in the Report. 
 
Our responsibility is to express an opinion on the report content within the scope of verification with the intention 
to inform all DACIN’s stakeholders. 
 
ASSURANCE STANDARDS, TYPE AND LEVEL OF ASSURANCE 
 
The SGS ESG & Sustainability Report Assurance protocols used to conduct assurance are based upon 
internationally recognized assurance guidance, including the Principles contained within the Global Reporting 
Initiative Sustainability Reporting Standards (GRI Standards) 101: Foundation 2016 for report quality, and the 
guidance on levels of assurance contained within the AA1000 series of standards and guidance for Assurance 
Providers.

AA1000 ACCOUNTABILITY PRINCIPLES (2018) CONCLUSIONS, FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
 
Inclusivity 
DACIN has demonstrated a good commitment to stakeholder inclusivity and stakeholder engagement. A variety 
of engagement efforts such as survey and communication to employees, customers, investors, suppliers, 
sustainability experts, and other stakeholders are implemented to underpin the organization's understanding of 
stakeholder concerns. For future reporting, it is encouraged to involve stakeholders in developing and achieving 
an accountable and strategic response to sustainability performances. 
Materiality 
DACIN has established effective processes for determining issues that are material to the business. Formal 
review has identified stakeholders and those issues that are material to each group and the report addresses 
these at an appropriate level to reflect their importance and priority to these stakeholders. 
Responsiveness 
The report includes coverage given to stakeholder engagement and channels for stakeholder feedback. 
Impact 
DACIN has demonstrated a process on identify and fairly represented impacts that encompass a range of 
environmental, social and governance topics from wide range of sources, such as activities, policies, programs, 
decisions and products and services, as well as any related performance.  Measurement and evaluation of its 
impacts related to material topic were in place at target setting with combination of qualitative and quantitative 
measurements. 
 
 
GLOBAL REPORTING INITIATIVE REPORTING STANDARDS CONCLUSIONS, FINDINGS AND 
RECOMMENDATIONS 
 
The report, DACIN’s Sustainability Report of 2021, is adequately in line with the GRI Standards in accordance 
with Core Option. The material topics and their boundaries within and outside of the organization are properly 
defined in accordance with GRI’s Reporting Principles for Defining Report Content. Disclosures of identified 
material topics and boundaries, and stakeholder engagement, GRI 102-40 to GRI 102-47, are correctly located 
in content index and report. For future reporting, it is recommended to have more descriptions of DACIN’s 
involvement with the impacts for each material topic (GRI 103-1), and how efforts were given to mitigate the 
impacts. When reporting on goals and targets for each material topic, the expected results are suggested to be 
set, if applicable, with quantitative objectives (GRI 103-2). It is proposed to explain the mechanisms for evaluating 
the effectiveness of the management approach specifically in the future (GRI 103-3).
