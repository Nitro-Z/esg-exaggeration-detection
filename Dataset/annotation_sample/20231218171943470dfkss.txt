Calculation Basis

Filing of Sustainability Reports by TWSE Listed Companies” of 
the Taiwan Stock Exchange, the “Code of Practice on Sustainable 
Development of Listed Companies and Over-the-counter 
Companies” and the sustainable indicators of the Sustainability 
Accounting Standards Board (SASB).
The financial data in the report is calculated in New Taiwan dollars 
in accordance with the International Financial Reporting Standards 
(IFRS), and audited and certified by Deloitte & Touche LLP.

Internal Audit

report is reviewed internally in two stages. In the first stage,

being confirmed by various departments, which will be publicly 
disclosed.

Chairman’s Statement
Kerry TJ is a leader in the logistics industry in Taiwan. It has a 
transportation network covering 368 administrative regions, serving 
a wide range of industries including government units, high-tech 
industries, manufacturing, medical institutes, department store 
channels and a wide range of end consumers. It provides customers 
with comprehensive logistics solutions. With the persistence in 
the ‘customer first’ rule and emphasis on humanity, the Company 
strives to promote corporate sustainability and makes various 
improvements in the operating environment, social participation, 
corporate governance, employee relationship, etc. With the 
expectations of making employees and customers feel secured, 
and keeping shareholders satisfying, the Company takes this 
three-pronged approach as the core, and then goes deep into the 
community’s services.
Promotion and Implementation of Carbon 
Reduction Goal
As one of the most important logistics enterprises in Taiwan, 
vehicles are the most important operating resource of Kerry TJ 
besides caring about humanity, including a variety of trucks for 
distribution, large trucks and trailers. Under the profound experience 
of global warming and extreme climate crisis, the Company 
attaches great importance to the “environment” and makes 
significant capital expenditures every year. In line with the policy 
of net zero carbon emission, the Company strengthens carbon 
emission and prevents air pollution according to the vehicle service 
life, mileage, maintenance records, etc., and replaces old fleets every 
year. In 2012, the Company began to research and develop and test 
electric trucks with vehicle manufacturers, taking the lead in all 
industry peers; recently, the replacement of old vehicles with new 
ones has been accelerated, and the application of environmentally 
friendly vehicles, hybrid vehicles and electric tricycles in the new 
stage has been introduced.
Apart from vehicle replacement or electric vehicle use, to take 
action on carbon reduction, the Company makes efforts in 
numerous aspects such as designing green buildings for the

Implementation of Safe Workplace and 
Protection of the Disadvantaged Groups
Kerry TJ firmly believes that it is more than ever to work hard than

transportation connections in remote areas and supporting farmers

not require returns. While giving back to society, the enterprise can 
also gather the cohesion of employees and inject a good force into the 
overall corporate culture.

About Kerry TJ
Corporate Profile
Kerry TJ is a member of Hong Kong Kerry Holdings Limited, 
headquartered in Taipei. Kerry TJ has a full-service base and a fleet 
of vehicles, extending to the outlying islands such as Penghu and 
Kinmen. Currently, it has more than 150 operating bases across the 
platform, including Logistics Centers, Dispatch Centers, Depots, and 
Pickup Terminals. The complete transportation network provides 
a solid foundation for providing professional customer services. 
Currently, Kerry TJ has over 4,000 employees and over 3,000 vehicles, 
providing professional logistics services including integrated logistics, 
international logistics, and supply chain solutions. It is a company 
listed on the Taiwan Stock Exchange with stock code 2608.
Kerry TJ, formerly known as T. Join Transportation Company Limited, 
has been established for nearly 70 years, with the provision of 
domestic cargo route transportation services as its primary core 
business. Through the design of regular delivery vehicles, it accurately 
plans long-distance shuttle buses between regions, takes the lead 
in constructing the backbone of the cargo transportation network 
through the Hub-and-Spoke Network, and effectively and timely 
completes the cargo transportation tasks through the distribution 
points of satellite depot in various regions, so as to meet the services 
of Business-to-Business(B2B) and Business-to-Consumer (B2C), and 
is one of the major transportation and logistics service providers 
in Taiwan. Cold chain logistics and ambient and low-temperature 
warehouse services are another professional core business of Kerry 
TJ, providing customers with other add-on services such as multi-
temperature layer, devanning, warehousing, tallying, delivery, 
scrapping, bonded and customized value-added services. Kerry TJ 
provides different logistics services to meet the different business 
needs of customers, and has introduced a professional automatic 
storage equipment system to build an efficient multi-temperature 
logistics center. In respect of low-temperature warehouses and 
the related delivery, Kerry TJ provides professional and customized 
warehouse and delivery services for different temperature layers, such 
as refrigeration (0~7˚C) and freezer (-18~-25˚C).

Operating Performance
Financial Performance
Looking back on 2022, due to the impact of the pandemic and the 
Russia-Ukraine war, the global economy was still dominated by a highly 
uncertain economic environment, coupled with factors such as high-
throughput expansion, shortage of labor and decline in consumption 
power, which have a negative impact on the logistics industry and 
its supply chain. In 2022, Kerry TJ faced an overall severe and slow 
economic environment. Its operating performance declined for the first 
time in the past five years. Revenue amounted to NT$10,075,000,000, 
representing a decrease of 4.6% over the previous year; net  profit after 
tax decreased by 11.1% from the previous year to NT$1,240,000,000.
Explanation of the difference in financial performance in 2021 and 2022:
1. In the post-pandemic era, the decrease in the volume of goods 
transported has led to a decline in revenue, resulting in a decrease in 
gross profit and operating profit.
2. The increase in net non-operating income was mainly due to the increase 
in equity method investment income from recognized investment and 
the increase in income from government entity's grants.
3. Net income before tax and net income after tax decreased due to the 
decrease in operating income.
Since 2022, the price of oil and electricity has increased while the 
volume of goods has decreased by about 10% due to inflation. As the 
logistics industry is a franchise industry, it is unable to raise the tariff by 
itself. Starting from July 1, 2022, Kerry TJ has been pushing forward the 
reform of its business strategy. Apart from collecting discounts through 
bargaining with customers, we charge fees based on customized services 
to ease part of the cost pressure; furthermore, Kerry TJ has implemented 
a comprehensive standardization of delivery of goods to optimize 
the quality of operations, coupled with the investment in automated 
equipment to improve operational efficiency, reduce the dependence on 
labor and improve service quality. After time verification, the Company 
has gradually gained recognition from the market and its service quality 
has also been resonated and recognized by customers. Taking the first 
step from difficult times and finding there is a way out, the Company 
will verify the correctness of the policy reform direction of the dual 
products (brand and quality). Moreover, in the face of severe challenges, 
Kerry TJ still maintained double-digit growth in revenue and profit 
from specialty logistics, including the continuous excellent growth of 
Science Park Logistics in High-tech Logistics such as bonded business 
and chemical warehouses. During the pandemic period, Pharmaceutical 
Logistics was entrusted by the Ministry of Health and Welfare to 
implement the storage and distribution of anti-pandemic materials, 
and the measures adopted to give back to society were also recognized 
by many parties; cold-chain storage and distribution are committed to 
adjusting the operational structure and continuously providing food 
safety control for the public.

Business Development Overview and Future Prospects
Kerry TJ’s operational strategy is focused on enhancing profitability. Its main business has developed from early-stage ambient 
temperature delivery (accounting for more than 90% of overall revenue) to 12 core projects to date. Through the integration 
of the internal resources, the Company is able to achieve resource sharing benefit and improve overall operational efficiency, 
and reduces costs.
Modern logistics and information technology are inseparable. Kerry TJ strengthens the investment in information software and 
hardware. It is committed to recruiting information talents, gradually replacing the existing information system, developing 
and introducing professional transportation and storage systems with more comprehensive functions, and moving toward the 
direction of intelligent logistics.
Kerry TJ’s business strategy is to maintain flexibility and strengthen brand and quality. The Company is committed to bolstering 
its distribution and service capabilities through a self-owned fleet, with a primary focus on ensuring quality, standardizing the 
delivery of goods, and improving the working conditions of its drivers. These efforts are instrumental in paving the way for a 
more automated future operational system. Kerry TJ's strategic priorities include specializing in "Pharma Logistics," "Cold Chain 
Services," "High-Tech Logistics," and "LTL and Express Services" as it works towards its mission of becoming Taiwan's premier 
integrated logistics service provider.

Management Structure
Sustainability Steering Committee
In order to implement corporate governance and continuously 
promote sustainable development goals, Kerry TJ established 
the Sustainability Steering Committee in 2022, which is a 
dedicated unit for the sustainable development of the Company.

convene a meeting at least once a year to review the Company’s 
sustainable development policies, strategies, and management 
policies; it also supervises the Company’s promotion of 
sustainable development-related matters, implementation plans 
and reports to the Board of Directors on the implementation 
results of sustainable development.
We have established a “Sustainable Development Team” 
within the Company and set up four functional teams of 
Corporate Governance, Green Logistics, Employee Care and 
Social Participation and Customer Services which are specific 
to governance, environment and social (including human rights) 
impact issues, respectively. And the director above the division 
level serves as the convener and has the function of cross-
department communication and resource coordination through 
upstream and downstream integration and horizontal linkage; 
furthermore, we have set up the posts of chief convener and 
executive secretary. Through clear organization and division of 
labor, we convene meetings from time to time to track work 
progress and implementation results to connect the sustainable 
development issues of various departments, and ensure that the 
sustainable development strategy is implemented in the daily 
operation of Kerry TJ. The chief convener of the Sustainable 
Development Team shall report to the Sustainability Steering 
Committee at least once a year about the implementation 
results and future work plans of sustainable development, 
and then report to the Board of Directors to supervise the 
promotion of sustainable development.

Kerry TJ referred to the five major orientations of AA1000 Stakeholder Engagement Standard (AA1000 SES) 2015, including Dependency, 
Responsibility, Tension, Influence, Diverse perspectives, and identified the degree of stakeholder relationship through a sampling 
questionnaire of 16 department heads and different groups. The Sustainability Steering Committee then conducted a stakeholder 
discussion assessment to confirm the importance of stakeholders to Kerry TJ. The top seven stakeholders after assessment are 
government agencies, customers, investors (including banks), employees, suppliers, media and community and society.

Negative Impacts

The Management of Water Resources
Water used at Kerry TJ’s office in Taiwan is tap water. Its 
wastewater is divided into domestic wastewater and car wash 
wastewater. Domestic wastewater is discharged from toilet 
and washing tank sewage pipelines. The car wash wastewater 
is discharged from the interception ditch of car wash area. The 
wastewater treatment method is to concentrate the wastewater 
into the water collection well, which is directly connected to the 
sewage well through an independent pipe and then discharged 
into the sewage sewer. Kerry TJ’s water intake and discharge in

The Management of Waste
Kerry TJ generates wastes from its operations, which are classified 
into general domestic wastes, waste plastic films, waste pallets 
and septic tanks. For general domestic waste treatment, the local 
offices enter into contracts with the local legal waste collection 
and disposal companies. The waste collection and disposal 
companies are required to follow the provisions of “Method 
and Standard of Facilities for Storage and Removal of Industrial 
Waste” and relevant regulations. The cleaning vehicles are 
required to acquire removal permission issued by the competent 
environmental protection authority. Waste plastic films and waste 
pallets are disposed by waste collection and disposal companies, 
but currently, there is no data available for collection.
The current impact of waste treatment is mainly the treatment 
cost, which is entrusted to external professional waste collection 
and disposal companies for transportation and finally disposed 
to the incinerator. In recent years, in addition to the year-on-
year increase in overall environmental waste, and the rising 
environmental awareness have led to increased restrictions 
on waste disposal and incineration, the cost of waste removal 
has also increased year-on-year. If incinerators in each city are 
suspended for operation due to annual renovation, the handlers 
will be required to transfer to other cities for incineration, and the 
treatment efficiency of each furnace is limited. Finally, the cost 
will be transferred to the units entrusted for cleaning. According 
to the statistics, the total amount of waste generated in 2022 in

Board of Directors Performance Evaluation
To fulfill the corporate governance and continue to enhance the functions of the Board of Directors, and to establish performance targets to strengthen 
the operational efficiency of the Board, Kerry TJ formulated the Board of Directors Performance Evaluation Regulations in 2020. The Board of Directors 
shall conduct the Board of Directors performance evaluation annually in accordance with the evaluation procedures and indicators of the Regulations, 
and the evaluation shall be conducted by an external professional independent institution or a team of external experts and scholars at least once every 
three years. The scope of the evaluation includes the overall Board of Directors, individual Board of Directors members and functional committees, and 
the evaluation methods include Board of Directors and its member self-evaluation or peer evaluation. The results of the Board of Directors’ performance 
evaluation shall be completed by the end of the first quarter of the following year and submitted to the Board of Directors and reported to the 
competent authorities for disclosure.

Mitigation of Conflicts of Interest Between 
the Board of Directors and Management
Due to the needs of the Company’s operation, seeking to fully 
communicate the operation overview and strategic goals of the 
Company directly with directors, and discussing with General Managers 
in respect of the actual situation when making decisions. The chief 
executive officer of Kerry TJ was assumed by the Chairman. In order to 
ensure the independence of directors and avoid conflicts of interest, 
none of the elected independent directors have served for more than 
9 consecutive years. All directors are also in compliance with the 
Exchange Act to regulate the independence of the Board of Directors, 
and have no spouse or second kin relationship with other directors, 
which can effectively perform their supervisory functions. Moreover, 
according to the “Rules of Procedure for the Board of Directors 
Meetings” of the Company, directors who are involved in interests 
in meeting matters with themselves or their representatives shall 
state the material contents, and shall abstain from discussion and 
voting (The avoidance of interests of the Board of Directors in 2022 
is disclosed in P.36 “Corporate Governance Operations” in the 2022 
annual report of the Company), and shall not exercise their voting rights 
on behalf of other directors. Both functional committees of the Board 
of Directors fulfilled the functions of the committees, fully discussed 
various issues relating to the management of the Company and made 
recommendations for the reference of the Board of Directors in a timely 
manner at each meeting, which followed relevant laws and regulations 
and strengthened corporate governance.
Mechanisms and Regulations relating to 
Conflict of Interest Avoidance
Kerry TJ has established a “Code of Ethics”, which sets out the 
relevant practices to prevent conflicts of interest. Directors and 
managers should not place their personal gains and interests above 
corporate interests. Moreover, they shall not, by virtue of their 
positions in the Company, allow themselves, their spouse, parents, 
children or second kin relationship to obtain improper benefits.
Remuneration Policy and Salary Distribution
Kerry TJ has formulated the “Performance Management Measures” and the “Remuneration Policy and System for Employees and Managers” to provide 
objective and appropriate assessments on the performance and performance of senior managers and employees, respectively, as a reference for position 
adjustment, training and development, rewards and punishments, and salary adjustment. Moreover, according to Article 7 of the “Organizational Rules 
of the Remuneration Committee”, the “Remuneration Committee”, which is a functional committee of the Board of Directors, regularly reviews the 
performance evaluation and remuneration of directors and managers on an annual basis. The performance evaluation and remuneration shall be paid with 
reference to the general level of the industry, taking into account the time invested by the individual, the responsibilities, the achievement of individual 
objectives, the performance in other positions, the remuneration given by the Company to the same position in recent years, and the reasonableness 
of the relationship between the individual performance and the Company’s operating performance and future risks, such as the achievement of the 
Company’s short-term and long-term business objectives and financial position. The Directors may receive traveling expenses and Directors’ emoluments, 
and not more than 3% of the profits after the finalization of the annual accounts shall be set aside as Directors’ emoluments. The Company determines 
the remuneration of directors and managers after considering the scale of operation and industrial attributes and with reference to the general salary of 
the market, and submits the proposal to the Board for discussion after the Remuneration Committee considering and approving the proposal.

3.3 Risk Management
Risk management and response are critical parts of the 
sustainable operation of the enterprise. It is necessary to 
clearly recognize the actual or potential risks and formulate 
countermeasures and corresponding measures, so that the 
enterprise can respond to the risks in a timely and correct 
manner when it faces risks. Kerry TJ holds monthly operation 
and management meetings and is responsible for the review, 
supervision and coordination of various risk management 
businesses. “Sustainable operation, corporate governance, 
developing green logistics, creating a happy workplace and 
building an inclusive society” is the identification indicator of 
Kerry TJ in achieving the future of “sustainable operation”. How 
to transform from a change in the macro environment, practice 
social responsibility and deepen corporate operation and 
management are the goals of Kerry TJ.
Implementation of Risk Management
Risks of changes in routine operations represent conducting 
risk management through routine management activities, and 
the risk is assessed and the countermeasures are proposed 
through meetings and reported to the senior management. 
Risk control for material changes is proposed to the Board 
for resolution. Regular meetings include operation meetings, 
transportation meetings, departmental meetings, business 
reports, pension supervision committee, occupational safety and 
health committee, contractor safety and health coordination 
meeting, management review meeting and safety promotion 
meeting to cope with and reduce the operational risks arising 
from changes in internal and external operating environment. 
The Company has established the “Operational Measures for 
Pre-organizational and Post-organizational Steps and Risk 
Management” and “Management of Material Information 
Processing and Prevention of Insider Transactions”, and set up a 
corresponding notification system. In the event of a risk event, 
each unit or subsidiary shall immediately notify the Company’s 
risk management office and internal audit, and regularly track 
the implementation of risk events and improvement schemes.

3.4 Legal Compliance
Kerry TJ operates in compliance with the regulations and conducts 
regulatory identification on relevant regulations relating to operations, 
environmental protection and transportation services at least twice a 
year in accordance with internal standards. At the same time, online and 
physical promotions are implemented, and education and training are 
conducted to ensure that the Company is in compliance with relevant 
regulations. For each violation event, Kerry TJ pays the same attention 
and reviews for improvement afterwards and proposes improvement 
plans to prevent recurrence. If a violation event has significant (the 
definition of “material”: senior management comprehensively reviews 
the amount of fines, impacts the operation, goodwill impairment, the 
internal control systems of the Company, etc. for the violation event) 
impacts on the operation of the Company, it will be judged as a major 
violation.
Summary and Improvement of Violation Events
Material Violation Events
Kerry TJ determines whether the violation events are material (definition 
of material: senior executives comprehensively review the amount 
of fines, the impacts on operations, goodwill and the internal control 
system of the Company for the violation events) violation events which 
have impacts on the operations of the Company on a case-by-case 
basis. There were no material violation events in 2022.
Compliance with Corporate Governance Regulations
During 2022, Kerry TJ was not subject to monetary or other non-
monetary penalties in respect of compliance with corporate governance 
regulations.
Compliance with Social and Economic Regulations
During 2022, Kerry TJ was not subject to monetary penalties or 
other non-monetary penalties in respect of compliance with social 
and economic regulations, including anti-competitive behavior, anti-
trust behavior, monopolistic behavior, marketing communications, 
information and labeling of goods and services, and health or safety-
related regulations relating to the services or goods provided by the 
Company.
Compliance with Occupational Safety and Health 
Regulations
During 2022, Kerry TJ was not subject to monetary or other non-
monetary penalties in respect of compliance with occupational safety 
and health regulations.
Compliance with Basic Labor Regulations
During 2022, Kerry TJ was fined a total of NT$750,000 in 3 cases of 
sanctions and penalties related to the basic labor regulations, mainly 
due to the extension of overtime working hours. In order to improve this 
situation, in 2022, Kerry TJ improved the cases of labor inspection and 
punishment, strengthened the promotion of labor laws to its business 
partners within the Company, effectively allocated manpower needs 
and improved transportation equipment, and carried out multi-faceted 
recruitment channels to fill the manpower gap, thereby balancing driver 
management measures and actively improving labor conditions. Kerry 
TJ is also committed to maintaining a harmonious relationship between 
the employer and the employees and understands that decent working 
conditions can bring stable business development and harmonious 
relationship between the employer and the employees.
Compliance with Regulations related to Environmental 
Protection and the Environment
In 2022, Kerry TJ had 21 violations of the environmental laws, with 
a total penalty of NT$147,500. The penalties were mainly due to 
the emission of air pollutants from mobile sources (transportation 
vehicles), which did not comply with the emission standards and 
violated the provisions of the Air Pollution Control Act. Such violations 
and penalties did not cause significant pollution that would have an 
impact on the environment, and did not have any significant impact 
on the Company’s operations. For the penalty cases of the relevant 
Environmental Protection Bureau in each county or city in 2022, 
Kerry TJ has immediately arranged for the vehicles to return to the 
maintenance plant for adjustment and re-inspection of the exhaust 
gas, and all the vehicles are in compliance with the emission standard 
of air pollutants from mobile sources; besides, Kerry TJ has participated 
in an environmental seminar for one hour in accordance with the 
requirements of the laws. Kerry TJ’s review of the reasons for the 
failure to pass the exhaust test found that the generation of “carbon 
accumulation” resulted in the poor function of the machine parts and 
the inability to truly inhibit the generation of exhaust gas. Kerry TJ will 
strengthen its efforts to promote the right driving habits and establish 
a vehicle repair and maintenance program to ensure that vehicles 
maintain excellent efficiency and reduce the impacts of exhaust 
emissions on the environment.

Information System Transfer and Development
Electronic Signature System
The COVID-19 pandemic in the past three years has created the 
demand for electronic signature system, with an aim to reduce the 
direct contact between drivers and recipients and the risk of infection 
between employees and customers. Kerry TJ introduced an electronic 
signature system to enable recipients to scan the QR code generated by 
the drivers’ mobile devices for electronic signature, which avoided the 
traditional practice of signing by pens and submitting paper bills. While 
saving receipts which were equivalent to more than 12,500,000 pieces 
of A4 paper, it also reduced the opportunities for people to contact 
during the pandemic. More importantly, the confirmation of receipt can 
be directly synchronized to the Company’s information system. It does 
not require the traditional steps of manual confirmation after scanning 
the receipt number through drivers, which eliminates the dispute on 
whether the receipt is confirmed and improves the efficiency of business 
practices.
Currently, Kerry TJ is planning to introduce the electronic signature 
system to other business units, and to fulfill corporate social 
responsibility for environmental protection and occupational safety.
Situation Room Project
In order to help Kerry TJ efficiently analyze its operational information 
from the vast amount of business data accumulated to keep abreast of 
the operating conditions and enable the senior management to make 
better management decisions, Kerry TJ has planned the situation room 
project. The purpose of the project is to integrate the operational data 
of different business units within the Group into a single platform, 
and convert the information into graphics and forms, so that users 
can better understand the data and trends. Through real-time data 
aggregation, it can give feedback on the operating conditions and 
improve site leaders’ speed of response. Kerry TJ will continue data 
mining, and conduct more statistical analysis explore potential data 
relationships, and provide more diversified decision-making data so that 
it can improve the efficiency and competitiveness of the company.

Employee Rights and Interests
In addition to the protection of salary and benefits, including benefits such as labor, health, group insurance, 
retirement allowance, annual leave, parental leave and paternity leave for male employees, Kerry TJ is also 
committed to providing a dignified and safe working environment for its employees and ensuring that its 
employees are not subject to discrimination, harassment or unequal treatment due to any other conditions 
protected by applicable laws and regulations. In order to protect the rights and interests of employees at work, 
each employee of Kerry TJ has formally entered into an Employment Contract and complied with relevant laws 
such as the Labor Standards Act.
Retiring with Peace of Mind
Kerry TJ has set up the “Labor Pension Fund Supervision Committee” to supervise payment and collection of 
various pensions. The Labor Pension Fund Supervision Committee composes of a chairman, a vice chairman and 
seven members. The committee holds a meeting every quarter with 9 attendees. The main topics of the meeting 
include the explanation of the account payment of the meeting report, the review of the preferential retirees 
and the explanations of the amendments of the management measures for employee retirement.
Defined Contribution Plans
The pension system of the “Labor Pension Act” applicable to Kerry TJ is a state-managed defined contribution 
plan. The pension of the defined contribution plan is recognized as an expense in the period in which an 
employee has rendered service to the plan and 6% of the employee’s monthly salary is allocated to the special 
individual account of the Bureau of Labor Insurance.
Defined Benefit Plans
Kerry TJ allocates 10% of the total salary to the retirement fund on a monthly basis for the employees under 
the old system. Under the new retirement system, the Company determines the benefit cost (including service 
cost, net interest and re-measurement amount) based on the defined benefit plans using the projected unit 
credit method. Service cost (including the service cost for the current year) and net interest on the net defined 
benefit liability (asset) are recognized as employee benefit expense when incurred. Furthermore, in accordance 
with the relevant laws, 6% of the insured salary is allocated to the special individual pension account of the 
Bureau of Labor Insurance on a monthly basis. The employees who meet the retirement conditions regulated 
by the Labor Standards Act may apply to the Company 30 days before retirement. Moreover, in consideration of 
the contribution of long-term employees to the Company and the sustainable operation, the Company has also 
formulated preferential retirement measures for those who have retired under the old retirement system.
Minimum Notice Periods Regarding Operational Changes
For major operational changes that affect the rights and interests of employees and relevant corresponding 
measures, the Company will communicate and coordinate through labor communication meetings or 
extraordinary meetings, and notify the affected employees in advance in accordance with the law. Besides, the 
Company also provides relevant supporting measures, such as assisting in transferring to other internal units, 
providing the employees with relevant conditions to assist in applying for government grants. According to the 
Labor Standards Act, the Company makes a 10 to 30 days’ advance notice based on the seniority of employees.

Employee Health Care
Employees are not only important assets of Kerry TJ, but also partners who work together to create high-quality 
services. As such, Kerry TJ has set up a “Health Management Office” to coordinate and plan the Company’s health 
management, and arranged workers to station in the three districts including North District, Central District and South 
District to strengthen the health care of employees. Through the PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) Cyclic Management 
Model, the Company has formulated employee health care policies in accordance with the four major aspects of health 
management, occupational disease prevention, infectious disease prevention and health promotion. With both regulatory 
compliance and practical needs, Kerry TJ continues to create a friendly and healthy workplace, with the goal of preventing 
occupational injuries and diseases and protecting safety and health of the employees.
Health Risk Management for Drivers
Health Management
 Annual health check and health check of meal offering which are better than legal items are provided to the employees who have been on the job for one year 
or more to ensure that they meet the food hygiene level. The number of employees who have been implemented health check in 2022 was 3,864.
 Based on the severity of abnormal health check reports, Kerry TJ has established a three-level grading standards of low level, medium level and severe level, and 
provided individual health guidance and tracking for the employees with moderate and severe abnormalities, and arranged on-site health service doctors or 
occupational doctors for one-on-one consultation when necessary.
 For new employees, middle-aged and senior-aged employees, and low-temperature warehouse employees, relevant matching suggestions are provided for their 
abnormal health check reports.
 Each business premise is equipped with blood pressure monitors to encourage the employees to manage their own health, and to establish health tools for 
employees to take care of each other.
Occupational Diseases Prevention
In order to effectively reduce the risk of employees being exposed to hazards and suffering from occupational diseases, the Company has formulated the “Human Hazard 
Prevention Plan”, “Prevention Plan for Diseases Arising from Abnormal Work Load”, “Prevention Plan for Illegal Infringements” and “Mother Protection Plan” in accordance with 
the relevant guidelines of the Occupational Safety and Health Law, and implemented the following measures:
 During the annual health check, relevant questionnaires on the items such as skeletal muscles, overloading and illegal infringements have been completed.
 High-risk groups are identified based on the results of questionnaires on skeletal muscles, overloading and illegal infringement.
 Kerry TJ has cooperated with occupational safety and health personnel in the “Work Safety Management Office”, such as inspecting the working environment on site, 
conducting relevant environmental risk assessment and improvement.
 Kerry TJ has cooperated with doctors from Chang Gung Memorial Hospital and Chien-Yu Hospital to regularly perform on-site services at each work sites in accordance with 
regulations, and provide personal health guidance to protect the health of colleagues. In 2022, a total of 173 on-site services have been performed by executive doctors and 
nursing staff, and a total of 802 employees have received consultation services.
 The daily morning health exercise allows colleagues to start reducing occupational diseases associated with transporting goods by stretching muscles and bones.
 Kerry TJ has provided matching and reinstatement services for the colleagues 
returning to work.
 Upgrading the “Blessing Gifts for Pregnancy” to encourage childbirth and 
improve the recognition of pregnant colleagues in the workplace; adding a 
“Postnatal Gifts” to welcome female colleagues to return to work and to 
encourage working mothers to continue breastfeeding. For pregnant women, 
in accordance with the Measures for the Implementation of Mother’s Health 
Protection, relevant sanitary education information and work precautions 
during and after pregnancy are provided. In 2022, a total of 17 female 
employees have received mother health protection, and the medical workers 
have completed mother protection measures such as hazard identification and 
risk assessment for 61 person-times.
Prevention and Treatment of Infectious Diseases
In recent years, due to the changes in environment and pathogenesis, various 
infectious diseases have continued to occur. Failure to implement pandemic 
prevention measures may affect the health of employees when serious infection 
occurs, which may have an impact on the Company’s operations. Therefore, 
relevant measures are provided for the prevention and treatment of infectious 
diseases.
 In line with the relevant government policies, Kerry TJ will assess the 
development of the pandemic and potential risks to the workplace at any time 
and adjust corresponding measures in a timely manner.
 Transmitting new medical knowledge from time to time, paying attention to 
health issues at any time, and providing employees with health knowledge, 
information and pandemic prevention measures.
 Kerry TJ has improved employee care, planned relevant pandemic prevention 
leave and education and training, and provided sufficient protective equipment, 
such as goggles, masks, medical masks, gloves, discarded protective clothing, 
alcohol bottles and partitions.
 Kerry TJ has regularly monitored the progress of the employees’ vaccination and 
provided relevant assistance in a timely manner. The Company’s vaccination 
rate for the third dose of COVID-19 vaccine was 84.3%.
 Each workplace has set up a quarantine station to implement personnel 
access management, and regularly arrange disinfection in the workplace to 
ensure a safe and hygienic working environment for employees, customers and 
contractors.
 In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, Kerry TJ has provided on-site 
colleagues of business units with pandemic prevention propaganda and 
pandemic prevention materials to protect the health and safety of employees 
in the workplace.
Health Promotion
Based on the results of health checks, Kerry TJ has conducted analysis and 
statistics on ten major abnormalities as the planning basis for the annual 
internal health promotion activities, such as cigarettes and betel nuts

5.1 Social Welfare
Kerry TJ always believes that in order to achieve sustainable operation 
and development, apart from pursuing revenue, profit and shareholder’s

with gratitude, and adheres to the three major missions of “caring 
for employees”, “caring for the disadvantaged” and “operation with 
integrity” in its daily operation on the basis of sustainable operation. 
Guided by the philosophy of “taking from society and giving back to 
society”, Kerry TJ integrates the Group’s resources, provides assistance 
to disadvantaged groups or natural disasters, implements social care and 
transmits positive forces to bring positive momentum to society, and 
create more positive values.
Kerry TJ firmly believes that corporate social responsibility is not 
only a way to benefit others, but also a mutually beneficial practice. 
The Company always attaches great importance to combining its 
own strengths with its own talents while giving back to society 
through traditional charitable donations, giving full play to the core 
capability of enterprise, and joining the circular of goods (food) bank

the cornerstone of the sustainable development of the Company. In

society widens and the allocation of resources is uneven. According to

to living. Given the public credibility of the government, it serves as an

5.3 Pandemic Response
Since the beginning of 2020, under the shadow of the COVID-19 pandemic, many factories around the world have been suspended, industries such 
as tourism, aviation and catering have been severely impacted, and the economy has also been affected. The pandemic has also accelerated the 
transformation of many industries and has changed people’s consumption patterns and lifestyle. 
At the first time of the outbreak, Kerry TJ held emergency meetings to discuss the emergency response and assessed the impact of the pandemic to 
create a healthy environment. Kerry TJ set up pandemic prevention stations in various workplaces to measure body temperature, implemented alcohol 
disinfection, planned for off-site/regional offices, cancelled large-scale gatherings, held large-scale conference via video, and distributed masks to all 
employees. In the face of the pandemic, Kerry TJ continued to adapt and learn to jointly build a safe society and environment. With the outbreak of the 
pandemic and the upgrading of the government’s pandemic prevention policy, Kerry TJ distributed medical masks, gloves, goggles, disinfectant alcohol 
and other personal protective equipment to frontline delivery colleagues. For those delivered in high-risk areas, enhanced equipment such as protective 
masks and clothing were provided, and contact-free electronic signing were used to reduce the risk of infection.
Pandemic Cold Chain Command Center
In May 2021, the COVID-19 pandemic in Taiwan deteriorated, which 
caused a sudden burst of business for the logistics industry in Taiwan, 
especially for the cold chain logistics and distribution business with 
an increase by approximately 200% as a whole. As a result of the 
lack of preparation, most of counterparts suspended the cold chain 
business in early June to ease the stockpile of goods. Facing such 
challenges, Kerry TJ set up the “Pandemic Cold Chain Command 
Center” at the headquarters to direct 500 self-owned cold chain 
fleets with 300 dedicated cold chain alliance fleets, monitored the 
business volume of each low-temperature distribution station on 
a daily basis, made immediate adjustments to the transportation 
capacity of long-distance vehicle and tracked the daily distribution 
of each low-temperature distribution station. In terms of operation, 
the direct vehicle model was adjusted according to the quantity, so 
that the hypermarkets of the bulk business could replenish the stock 
smoothly. At the same time, the Company immediately dispatched materials to support the northern region from the central and southern districts, 
where the pandemic was less severe, to allocate resources to maximize the overall strength.
Through the continuous efforts and deployment, Kerry TJ was the only logistics company that cooperated with the cold chain service policy by Ministry 
of Transportation and Communications during the pandemic. In addition, Kerry TJ continued to provide reliable cold chain services to the public, 
hypermarkets and operators, allowing the public to continue to purchase safe fresh fruit and food conveniently during the pandemic, providing support 
for people’s lives.
Enhanced Health Management Policy
With the escalation of the COVID-19 pandemic in April 2022, Kerry 
TJ strengthened its employee health management policy to help 
employees implement self-health protection in the face of the spread 
of the pandemic and reduce their anxiety and panic about the spread 
of COVID-19 and provided relevant hygiene education and training 
to ensure the health and safety of employees and their families. The 
Company had also reviewed the current group insurance coverage and 
considered staying in a quarantine hotel or quarantine office due to a 
definite diagnosis of COVID-19 as hospitalisation, or hospitalisation 
due to a definite diagnosis of COVID-19 can be regarded as the general 
illness and be covered by the group hospitalisation medical insurance.
Ensuring Operational Sustainability Plan
Kerry TJ had adjusted and organized human resources to support the 
needs of business development in an effective manner to ensure its 
operational sustainability and promote separation mechanism during 
working hours to minimize the risk of spread of the pandemic. With the 
rapid outbreak, Kerry TJ activated its internally operational sustainability 
plan and crisis response plan to ensure the health and safety of its 
employees and reduce the impact of COVID-19 on the Company’s 
operations and development.
Work from Home or Remote Working 
In May, the Company launched work from home or remote working 
measures to provide continuous customer service while providing 
care and security for employees. The Company also regularly 
published the latest information on COVID-19 prevention and 
informed all employees through emails to ensure that employees 
receive accurate and complete information, fulfilling the 
responsibilities of the Company as a legal employer and the relevant 
social responsibilities and obligations that should be undertaken, and 
protecting the health and safety of employees.
