About This Report
Editorial Principles
The 2023 ESG Report (hereinafter referred to as "this report") is the third ESG report issued by Billion Electric Co., Ltd. 
(hereinafter referred to as "Billion", "Billion Electric", "Billion Group", "the Company" or "the Group"). The content structure 
of this report is prepared with reference to the GRI Sustainability Reporting Standards issued by the Global Sustainability 
Standards Board (GSSB), following the eight reporting principles of GRI 2021: accuracy, balance, clarity, comparability, 
completeness, sustainability context, timeliness and verifiability. It also corresponds to the HARDWARE industry in the 
Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB) for compilation. At the same time, it echoes the "Corporate Social 
Responsibility Best Practice Principles for TWSE/TPEx Listed Companies" and the United Nations Sustainable Development 
Goals (SDGs).
The information disclosed in this report covers 
Billion Electric (including headquarters, Yilan Plant 
and Hsinchu laboratory) and two subsidiaries,Billion 
Watts Technologies and VGwatt Energy (hereinafter 
referred to as "Billion Watts" and "VGwatt"), for the year 
ended December 31, 2023. It includes the economic, 
environmental, social and corporate governance 
performance of BEC Technology Inc. (Subsidiary in USA). 
The financial data is consistent with the scope and time 
of the consolidated financial report. All financial figures 
in the report are calculated in New Taiwan dollars (NTD). 
If the content information covers related information 
outside this scope, it will be explained in the report.  The 
report is compiled by the personnel of each unit of the 
Group, and is reviewed and revised by the Sustainable 
Development Committee. After being approved by the 
General Manager and Chairman in accordance with 
relevant approval authority, it is submitted to the Board 
of Directors for review and issuance.

subsidiary "Billion Sunpower Co., Ltd." was disposed of, so it is not included in the 2023 
sustainability report. The scope newly includes "VGwatt Energy Co., Ltd.". The 2023 material topic 
targets focus on 9 topics, so 3 topics were reduced. The detailed contents are as follows. There 
are no other major information restatements. Only minor data are restated for more accurate 
presentation, and the content and reasons for information restatement are noted in each section.
Before issuing the report, the senior executive unit implements the ESG (environmental, 
social and governance) performance and strategic goals to ensure continuous progress and realize 
sustainability concepts. At the same time, the Sustainable Development Committee and the head 
of each unit review the correctness of the content and related information of each chapter of this 
report. The ESG report is reported to the Board of Directors every quarter on the progress, and 
finally issued after passing external assurance.
In May 2024, this report passed the verification of SGS Taiwan Ltd., a third-party impartial 
unit, and completed moderate assurance of Type 1, meeting the requirements of GRI 2021 
standards.  Through external third-party verification and strict internal control and audit 
mechanisms, the accuracy of financial, environmental and social information and data in this 
report is ensured.  At the same time, this report is published on the ESG Corporate Sustainability 
Section of Billion Electric's official website for users to download.
This is the third ESG report prepared by Billion Electric Co., Ltd. The Company will regularly issue 
the "ESG Report" every year.

Chairman's Message
The long-term emissions of massive amounts of 
greenhouse gases have monumental impacts on the climate, 
raising global temperatures and triggering frequent extreme 
weather occurrences. Facing company transformations 
amidst the climate crisis, we witnessed the entrance of the 
Billion Group into the solar energy field over a decade ago, 
cooperating with the government’s green energy policies 
and striving for the future of planet earth through Group 
transformation. Currently, 70% of company revenue is derived 
from solar energy and energy storage, while both the fields 
of communication and power supply products continue to 
thrive. Active transition towards 5G and private networks is 
also being carried out in the communication industry. Active 
investing in charging pile developments by the subsidiary 
Billion EV Charging was carried out this year, and the planning 
for overseas market expansion in the coming year. All these 
initiatives have brought the challenge of generating NTD 10 
billion in Group revenue, as we march towards the Group’s 
60th anniversary.  The Group now employs over 200 people 
in a variety of firms both domestically and internationally, 
with total revenue exceeding NTD 2 billion. In addition to the 
support of shareholders, I would like to acknowledge the 
efforts of our team and colleagues.  All of these components 
are essential for growth. Industrialization has brought 
civilization, development, and convenience to the planet, 
but it has also introduced carbon emissions. The longer 
it accumulates, the greater the impact on our planet and 
living environment. Therefore, we attach great importance 
to the ESG management philosophy and incorporates 
environment, society and corporate governance into our 
market management strategies. Through clarifying brand 
value, improving employee well-being, emphasizing social 
responsibility, and investing in innovation and technology, 
we hope that while the Company profits and shareholders 
will receive reasonable remuneration, the Company may also 
fulfill its corporate social responsibility, creating a high-quality 
innovative environment for joint growth with all colleagues, 
establishing an excellent corporate image, improving market 
competitiveness, and optimizing the Company's operating 
performance. The results are then given back to shareholders 
and society to achieve a win-win cycle of management and lay 
a solid foundation for the Group's sustainable development.
In terms of the environment, in addition to the original 
ISO 14001 environmental management system and QC 080000 
hazardous substance management system, we actively 
introduced ISO 14064:2018 GHG inventory in 2023, and 
obtained the ISO 27001 certificate for information security 
to continue to emphasize environment and information 
security. The Company philosophy incorporates a sustainable 
mentality, adhering to an integrity and transparency focused 
corporate governance culture. As an leading international 
brand in network communication and energy storage, it is 
Billion Group's mission and responsibility to prevent further 
degradation of climate change. The Group aims to join the 
target of achieving net zero emissions by 2050. The Company 
shall lead by example, implementing carbon emissions control 
across the whole supply chain. As a global citizen, ensuring 
compliance with carbon reduction and emission requirements 
where customers are situated is also the critical to the Group's 
competitiveness and sustainable growth. Our objective 
is to minimize carbon emissions by utilizing innovative 
technologies. To establish a sustainable future, all members 
of the Group continue to innovate and breakthrough to realize 
green products, care for the environment and give back to 
society to create a sustainable future.  At the same time, the 
Company will prioritize increasing employees’ environmental 
knowledge and involvements, promoting the harmonious 
development of enterprises and the environment. 
In the aspect of social values, the Company is committed 
to achieving shared and mutually beneficial outcomes.  We 
provide customers with reassuring and satisfactory products, 
while also caring about the daily lives of our employees. In 
addition, in terms of employee well-being, Billion Group 
implements code of conduct and regulations to ensure 
employees' rights and interests.  A systematic set of human 
resources regulations, processes and policies have been 
developed, addressing aspects such as compensation, human 
rights, employment, training, and promotion.  Through policies 
and systems, the Group demonstrates concern and attention 
to colleagues in terms of talent cultivation and retention.  To 
attract and retain outstanding talents, the Company not only 
focuses on employees’ career development, but also on their 
work-life balance, and physical and mental health, providing 
the necessary support and assistance. To boost society’s

Implementing Sustainable Management
1.1 Sustainability Commitment
To implement sustainable management, Billion has established the "Code of Business Ethics and Conduct", "Integrity Management Code of Conduct", "Integrity Management Implementation 
Procedures and Code of Conduct", and "Management and Operations Human Rights Plan". We insist on developing and designing green and sustainable products. Facing the impact of climate change 
on the earth, we actively take relevant countermeasures to fulfill our commitment to environmental policies and strive to realize our responsibilities to society and the environment while supporting the 
best interests of our stakeholders. The Company's R&D of green products can meet the requirements of EU RoHS, China RoHS directives and customer HSF requirements. We also encourage suppliers 
and employees to engage in similar activities. The goal of all these is to fulfill the role of a responsible corporate citizen.  We fully implement ISO 9001 Quality Management System and ISO 14001 
Environmental Management System. The policies are as follows:
1.	Quality Policy - Quality with Care, Meeting Customer and Legal Requirements, Responding to Green Product Consensus, Establishing a Green Supply Chain
Led by the Quality Assurance unit, each unit cooperates in implementation and regularly promotes policies and goals internally.
2.	Environmental Policy - Encourage Full Participation, Effectively Save Energy, Continue Pollution Prevention, Commit to Continuous Improvement
Led by the Administration Department, each unit cooperates in implementation and regularly promotes policies and implementation status internally.
3.	Specific management plans for human rights policies include assessments and cooperation with the above-mentioned human rights policies in aspects such as a safe and healthy work 
environment, an equal and non-discriminatory workplace, freedom of work, working hours, wages, bonuses and benefits, prohibition of child labor, business ethics and morality. In line with 
this, the Group has established relevant human resources rules and systems, such as the “Sexual Harassment Prevention and Handling Measures” and the “Measures for Preventing Unlawful 
Infringement when Performing Duties”, and has set up a dedicated phone line and email for reporting to provide employees with a work environment free from discrimination and harassment.

1.3 Identification of Material Topics
Billion Electric introduced materiality analysis in the process of preparing the sustainability report. Following the Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB) standards for the 
HARDWARE industry and with reference to industry peers, a total of 33 topics were selected. External experts scored these topics based on their actual or potential negative impacts and 
positive contributions to the economy, environment, and people (including human rights). They also considered the requirements and concerns of international sustainability advocacy 
organizations and potential investors regarding corporate sustainability issues. After internal discussions with the Company's executives and colleagues, the topics that have a significant 
impact on the economic, environmental and social aspects of the company's sustainable management are used as the basis for the material topics and information disclosure in the 2023 
report. The detailed identification process is as follows:

1.3.1 Identification Boundary of Material Topics
Based on the scoring results of external experts, 13 issues were selected: economic performance, customer privacy/information security, customer health and safety, supplier social 
assessment, supplier environmental assessment, employee diversity and equal opportunity, innovative product R&D and marketing, occupational health and safety, risk management,

management, customer relationship management, and innovative product R&D and marketing, a total of 9 sustainability issues. These are listed as items for continuous attention and 
promotion, as well as the basis for future ESG report disclosure. Compared with 2022, after discussions with senior management and colleagues, "Innovative Products and Technologies"

and services, not related to the production of renewable energy within the Company, and because the Company's production lines do not generate significant hazardous and large 
amounts of waste, the 2023 material topics focus on 9 material topics, so the three topics of "Energy", "Renewable Energy" and "Waste" were reduced.  The content will be compiled by the 
Sustainable Development Committee, reported to the Board of Directors for confirmation, and then issued.

Material Topics:
Economic Performance
Materiality
Economic performance is key to a company’s survival. Stable profitability and continuous growth are the main factors that enable sustainable development and 
fulfillment of social responsibilities, and are also issues of concern to stakeholders.
Impacts
Actual Positive:
Positive growth in economic performance increases investors’ investment in Billion.  There is strong market demand for high-quality products and services, and 
significant results have been achieved in the development and investment in green energy and energy storage.
Potential Positive:
With the global net-zero emission target by 2050 and government emphasis on low-carbon investment, the Group’s green energy industry layout will generate 
new operational momentum.
Potential Negative:
Rapid changes in industry trends and market conditions, and numerous competitors may cause oversupply.
Policies/Strategies
Adopt a stable management strategy and maintain profitability to maximize company value and protect shareholders’ interests while fulfilling social 
responsibilities. For currency positions generated from operations, first adopt natural hedging, and then use exchange rate hedging instruments to reduce or avoid 
possible economic losses.
•	Continue to invest in technology research and development, and seek new product and market opportunities, which is crucial for maintaining the Company’s 
growth momentum.
•	Regularly hold investor conferences and publish financial reports and operating results for investors, shareholders, and all stakeholders to understand the oper-
ating results.
•	Obtain ISO 9001 certification to enable the Company to respond to market trends, gain customer trust, and connect with the world.
•	Analyze technology and industry market dynamics to help the Group identify business opportunities.
•	Plan and track the implementation of the Group’s long-term and short-term strategies.
•	Evaluate potential strategic investment, acquisition targets, and cooperation opportunities.
•	Overseas operational deployment to diversify business risks and reduce operating costs.
•	Conduct capital management to ensure the ability to continue operation, and optimize debt and equity balances to maximize shareholders’ compensation.
•	The main management regularly reviews the capital structure based on economic environment and business considerations. Following the recommendations of 
senior management and in compliance with legal regulations, balance the overall capital structure through dividend payments, share buybacks, and financing.

Material Topics: Risk Management
Materiality
•		With the successive signing and implementation of regional trade agreements and the influence of strong country competition, the global business and trade environment 
has changed dramatically in recent years, with multinational companies being particularly affected. Looking back at the 2008 financial crisis, the 2019 US-China trade 
war, the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, the logistics problems caused by COVID-19 in 2021, and the Russia-Ukraine war in 2022, which caused global inflation to intensify, 
businesses have faced considerable impacts and huge challenges.  Therefore, risk identification, management, and mitigation are aspects that the Group must pay attention 
to.
•		Risks that affect the Company's normal operations in the production and operation process, such as sales/procurement concentration, supply chain management, 
corporate image building and maintenance, etc.
Impacts
•		Potential Positive Impact: For a company to operate sustainably, it must consider various possible risks and minimize them or even turn them into opportunities.
•		Potential Negative Impact: The Russia-Ukraine war may lead to increased raw material and transportation costs, while energy storage projects demand substantial funds. 
Additionally, when energy-related regulations change or risks accelerate too quickly, it will lead to increased capital costs.
Policies/Strategies
Based on the overall operational strategy and business environment, with the goal of complying with laws and regulations, promoting and implementing the 
Company's overall risk management, clearly understanding the risks faced by the Company's operations, and ensuring the effectiveness of risk management, 
establish "Risk Management Policies and Procedures" as the highest guiding principles.
Financial Risks
A. Some contracts adopt US dollar sales, and related purchases are paid in US dollars, adopting a natural hedging method to avoid the exchange rate fluctuation 
risk of these receivables and payables.
B. For export quotations, consider the fluctuations in exchange rates and timely reflect the costs to adjust the selling price.
Procurement concentration risk
The Company and its subsidiaries continue to actively develop other suppliers, in order to avoid the supply shortages or interruptions.
Operational Risks
•		Analyze the likelihood of various risks, plan preventive measures, and formulate emergency response processes.
•		Quality Policy - Quality with Care, Meeting Customer and Legal Requirements, Responding to Green Product Consensus, Establishing a Green Supply Chain
•		Environmental Policy - Encourage Full Participation, Effectively Save Energy, Continue Pollution Prevention, Commit to Continuous Improvement
•	Bad Debts: To avoid the risk of bad debts from accounts receivable, most customers' transaction terms are shipment after receiving full payment; for customers with O/A 
(Open Account) needs, this payment term is provided only after obtaining insurance bank coverage.
Climate Risks
Climate change poses an escalating global threat that demands urgent action from everyone. No one can escape its far-reaching consequences.  Recognizing the 
gravity of this crisis, Billion Group closely monitors climate trends and regulatory developments, both globally and in Taiwan. We are committed to doing our 
utmost to combat this threat, taking proactive measures to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate our environmental impact.

2.2.1 Board of Directors
Chart of Board
The Company shall have seven to nine directors, with the term of three years, and all Directors shall be eligible for re-election. The directors shall be elected from among persons of legal capacity 
by the shareholders' meeting. The Company employs a candidate nomination system for the election of directors, and shareholders are required to elect from the list of nominated director candidates. 
Each share has the same voting rights equal to the number of Directors to be elected, and a shareholder may cast all his/her voting rights to one candidate or among several candidates; those candidates 
receiving more voting rights shall be elected as Directors. The chairman is elected by and from among the directors. The chairman represents the Company externally and manages all important 
company affairs. The Company has independent directors, and the number of independent directors shall not be less than three. Their professional qualifications, shareholding, restrictions on 
concurrent positions, nomination and election methods, and other matters to be complied with shall be handled in accordance with the relevant regulations of the securities competent authority. The 
total number of registered shares held by all directors shall comply with the relevant regulations.
The current Board of Directors of Billion Electric has nine seats, including four independent directors, accounting for nearly 50%. All directors are elected by shareholders' votes, with no gender 
differences or restrictions. Their responsibilities include supervising, appointing, and directing the Company's management, and are responsible for the Company's overall operating conditions, 
dedicated to maximizing shareholders' rights and interests.
The Company's Board of Directors emphasizes high efficiency, transparency, diversity, and professionalism to strengthen corporate governance. The Board of Directors considers professional 
capabilities in executing business such as operational judgment, accounting and financial analysis, business management, crisis management, industry knowledge, global market perspective, 
leadership, and decision-making to avoid decision-making blind spots.

Remuneration Setting Process
The Remuneration Committee aims to assist the Board of Directors in executing and evaluating the Company's overall remuneration and benefit policies. To regularly evaluate the remuneration 
of directors and managers, the Company has established the "Board of Directors Performance Evaluation Measures" and "Employee Performance Management Measures". Every year, based on the 
results of performance evaluations, it serves as a reference for setting individual remuneration. Considering the current trends in corporate governance, reasonable remuneration is given to balance the 
Company's sustainable operation and risk control. The relevant performance appraisal and remuneration reasonableness are reviewed by the Nomination Committee and the Remuneration Committee 
before being submitted to the Board of Directors for approval.
The performance evaluation of managers, in addition to referring to individual performance achievement rates and contributions to the Company, also considers the Company's overall operating 
performance, future industry risks and development trends, and reviews the remuneration system in a timely manner according to actual operating conditions and relevant laws and regulations.  The 
performance assessment of the Board members is based on their grasp of Company goals and tasks, the awareness on Directors' responsibilities, participation in the Company's operations, internal 
relationship management and communication, Directors' professional and continuing education, and internal control. The Company's policy for paying directors' remuneration is based on the 
provisions of the Company Act and the Company's Articles of Association, which stipulate that no more than 3% of the current year's profit can be used as Directors' remuneration.  The remuneration of 
Directors and managers at the level of associate and above is first sent to the Remuneration Committee, then submitted to the Board of Directors for resolution before payment.
For new employees, salary is given based on education, past experience, professional certificates, language skills, and other conditions. For current employees, regular performance evaluations are 
conducted, considering seniority, performance, and contribution as the basis for salary adjustments.

The Company's dedicated personnel for integrity management is the Legal Affairs Department, which is fully responsible for formulating and supervising the implementation of integrity 
management policies and prevention programs. The work content includes regularly reviewing and revising the Company's relevant integrity regulations and submitting them to the Board of Directors 
for approval, integrity law education and advocacy, signing of integrity management declarations and employee integrity commitment letters, etc.  The Legal Affairs dedicated personnel regularly

•	For whistleblowing matters involving general employees
1.	For matters involving money or benefits that can be converted into money, they 
should be directly reported to the General Manager.
2.	For matters not related to money or benefits, in addition to reporting to the General 
Manager, the department head should also be notified.
•	For whistleblowing matters involving the Chairman, Directors or senior executives, they 
should be reported to the Independent Directors or the Audit Committee.
•		General employees
1.	Money cases are handled by Human Resources.
2.	Non-money cases are handled by Human Resources together with the department 
head, and if necessary, should be handled together with Legal Affairs.
•		For cases involving senior executives, whether involving money or not, they are all 
handled by Human Resources and Legal Affairs.  The General Manager may adjust the 
above handling procedures according to the actual situation.
•	If it is verified that the reported person has indeed violated relevant laws and regulations or the Company's Codes of Ethical Conduct and Integrity Code of Conduct, the reported

•	The acceptance, investigation process, and investigation results of the whistleblowing should all be retained as written documents and kept for 5 years.  They may be kept 
electronically. If litigation related to the content of the whistleblowing occurs before the expiration of the retention period, the relevant information should continue to be retained 
until 2 years after the end of the litigation.
•	For whistleblowing matters that have been verified to be true, the relevant units of the company should be instructed to review the relevant internal control systems and 
operating procedures, and propose concrete improvement measures to prevent the same behavior from occurring again.
•	The Company's responsible unit should report the whistleblowing matters, their handling methods, and subsequent review and improvement measures to the Board of Directors.

Materiality
Selecting suppliers with social responsibility and environmental management is one of the important keys to sustainable operations. If suppliers fail to meet requirements, not only 
will the enterprise lose competitiveness, but it may also face relevant regulatory penalties in the future, increasing cost risks. Establishing stable, trusting, and long-term cooperative

Impacts
Positive Impact: Suppliers with social responsibility comply with ethical norms and take environmental responsibility, making the environment more friendly and mitigating global 
pollution. Enterprises can also enhance market competitiveness and increase profits.
Negative Impact: If existing suppliers cannot meet social responsibility and environmental requirements and cannot improve in the short term, it may lead to supply problems and 
cause negative corporate image and impact on business performance due to delayed delivery.
Policies/
Strategies
•	Social responsibility ethical norms and environmental management have been included in supplier evaluations to confirm that suppliers meet requirements. Regular sampling

working hours comply with local legal requirements, no illegal improper benefits, ensure products do not use conflict minerals from Congo and surrounding areas, etc.
•	Environmental policy aspects: When issuing purchase orders, advocate the company's environmental policy to suppliers: Encourage full participation, effectively save energy, 
continue pollution prevention, commit to continuous improvement.

Information Security
With the popularization of the internet, business models have changed, but it has also brought about cybercrime and information security issues. Therefore, maintaining internal information 
security has become an urgent matter. To maintain corporate governance, reduce operational and information security risks, and provide customers with high-quality services, in addition to 
implementing necessary measures such as computer room and network management, it is necessary to formulate an information security management system to protect the Company's important 
information assets and achieve sustainable operation goals.
The Company's various information security management regulations comply with relevant government regulations, such as:
Information Security Management Act, Criminal Law, National Secrets Protection Act, Patent Act, Trademark Act, Copyright Act, Personal Data Protection Act, etc., and irregularly implements 
information security education and training, promotes information security policies and related implementation regulations, and has formulated information security system-related measures:
1.		All company personnel are responsible for maintaining information security and should comply with the Company's relevant information security management regulations.
2.	Conduct information asset inventory and risk assessment regularly every year, effectively implement risk control, and continuously improve to prevent threats from information security 
incidents.
3.		Regulate the application and change management of information system accounts, passwords, and access rights to ensure that any access behavior is properly authorized and managed, prevent 
improper information access and avoid leakage of important data.
4.		Plan appropriate access control, environmental monitoring, equipment maintenance, auditing, abnormal handling and other security control measures for physical and environmental security 
to prevent unauthorized physical and environmental access and ensure normal operation of the Company's various operating spaces.
5.		Before new systems and equipment are put into operation, risk and safety factors must be considered to prevent situations that endanger information security.
6.		Formulate an internal audit plan for the information security management system, regularly review the usage of all personnel and equipment within the scope of the information security 
management system, and formulate and implement corrective and preventive measures according to the audit report.
7.		Collect, screen and compare information security threat intelligence. Implement detection and protection measures based on this intelligence. Complete a comprehensive inventory and control 
system to ensure that identified threats do not affect the Organization.

5.4 Health and Safety Protection
Although Billion Group has not introduced ISO 
45001 and does not have an Occupational Safety and 
Health Committee, it has established relevant processes 
based on the risk assessment technical guidelines of the 
Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) 
to identify, evaluate, and control workplace environment 
and operational hazards. However, because Billion's 
headquarters has over 100 people, it needs to establish 
a Occupational Safety and Health Committee, which is 
currently being planned. Employees and supplier partners 
are important assets in the sustainable development 
of enterprises. Therefore, in addition to complying 
with Occupational Safety and Health Act. and related 
requirements, the Company's operational activities 
and service processes should continuously improve 
occupational safety and health, avoid unsafe behaviors, 
environments or equipment, and take relevant measures 
to prevent occupational hazards and protect the safety 
and health of employees and supplier partners.
Currently, the Yilan plant has less than thirty 
employees and only has a Type A occupational safety 
and health business supervisor. Irregular meetings are 
held to address deficiencies in occupational health and 
safety at the Yilan plant and related suggestions, which 
are incorporated into the safety and health management 
policy for continuous improvement of occupational safety 
and health performance.  This effectively controls hazard 
factors generated during operations, enhances factory 
safety, implements self-management, and aims to reduce 
occupational hazards.
Billion Group's occupational safety-related regulations apply to all 
employees, as follows:
•	 Set up necessary prevention equipment or measures 
in accordance with the law to prevent employees from 
occupational hazards.
•	 Conduct internal occupational safety and health education and 
training for at least three hours every three years in accordance 
with the law, strengthening related advocacy.
•	 Set up occupational safety and health signs in accordance with 
the law.
•	 Provide appropriate lighting or illumination for workplace 
entrances and exits, stairs, passages, emergency exits, safety 
ladders, etc.
•	 Set up first aid medicines and equipment.
•	 For hazardous chemicals, provide labeling, prepare lists, 
and display safety data sheets, and take necessary general 
knowledge measures.
•	 Occupational safety personnel regularly review the safety data 
sheets of various hazardous chemicals, and the procurement 
unit or user unit obtains new data from suppliers before the 
expiration date.
•	 Regularly outsource work environment monitoring every six 
months and announce the monitoring results.
•	 The Company arranges general health check-ups and special 
health check-ups annually, with contracted medical personnel 
handling health management, occupational disease prevention, 
and health promotion for worker health protection.
•	 Billion headquarters provides special health check-ups for 
employees working with XRF testing (ionizing radiation risk).
•	 Implement legal requirements to ensure the work safety and 
physical and mental health of female workers.
•	 Regularly promote occupational safety and health regulations, 
conduct safety and health education and training, etc., with 
employee participation, consultation, and communication.
•	 Arrange self-defense fire drills (disaster emergency response 
and evacuation courses in public places) every six months.
•	 Formulate safety and health work rules and report to the Labor 
Standards Inspection Office New Taipei City Government for 
reference.
Billion Watts's employees are important assets in 
enterprise development. Occupational health and safety 
are regularly promoted at weekly energy storage meetings 
to ensure on-site safety at various sites and promote 
the Company's operational activities. In addition to 
complying with occupational safety and health regulations 
and related requirements, the service process should 
continuously improve occupational safety and health, 
avoid unsafe behaviors, environments or equipment, and 
take relevant measures to prevent occupational hazards. 
Related opinions are incorporated into the safety and 
health management policy to protect employee safety and 
health.
VGwatt Energy's employees are important assets 
in enterprise development. Irregular meetings are held 
to promote occupational health and safety, advocating 
that the Company's operational activities and service 
processes, in addition to complying with occupational

should continuously improve occupational safety 
and health, avoid unsafe behaviors, environments or 
equipment, and take relevant measures to prevent 
occupational hazards. Related opinions are incorporated 
into the safety and health management policy to protect 
employee safety and health.

safety and health for continuous improvement.
Based on the level of risk, high-risk operations or hazards are prioritized for handling, and improvement deadlines and responsible personnel are set. Appropriate control measures are taken to 
reduce or eliminate risks. The selection of control measures should follow these principles:
•		Eliminate hazards from the source whenever possible, such as changing operating methods, replacing with safer substances or equipment, etc.
•	If hazards cannot be eliminated, reduce the probability or degree of impact of hazards, such as installing protective devices, strengthening management controls, providing personal protective 
equipment, etc.
•	Adopt multiple control measures to enhance control effectiveness.
After implementing control measures, reassess the risk level and record it in the risk assessment form. Continuously monitor and inspect the implementation of control measures, and regularly 
review the results of risk assessments to ensure their effectiveness.

Appendix 4: Climate-related Financial Disclosures for TWSE/TPEx Listed Companies
1.	
Implementation Status of Climate-related Information
Item
Implementation Status
1.Describe the supervision and 
governance of climate-related risks 
and opportunities by the Board and 
management.
The Company regularly convenes board meetings, and the Sustainable Development Committee reports the implementation status of ESG and the 
achievement of greenhouse gas inventory targets to the board of directors quarterly as per the FSC's regulations. Under the Sustainability Committee, there 
are six sub-groups for corporate governance, environmental protection, social responsibility, risk management, information security and ethical management. 
Members from each sub-group form an executive team to promote related matters.
2.Describe how the identified 
climate risks and opportunities 
impact the Company's business, 
strategy and finances planning 
(short, medium and long term).
The Sustainable Development Committee discusses and identifies climate risks and opportunities that have an impact on the Company at the beginning of 
each year, and scores them with external experts based on probability of occurrence and financial impact to screen out significant transition climate risks, 
physical climate risks, and transition climate opportunities. The risk intervals are defined as follows:
•	Short-term: 1-3 years
•	Mid-term: 4-10 years
•	Long-term: Over 10 years
·	Policy and regulatory risks (energy tax-related regulations):
1. Participate in carbon trading/renewable energy markets: Include carbon emission intensity performance in assessments, strengthen the core photovoltaic 
and energy storage businesses, establish carbon neutrality goals
2. Renewable energy regulations and procurement to expand new energy businesses: Actively develop new energy sources such as solar, energy storage, 
and solar-storage integration
·	Market risks (changes in customer behavior): Begin to require greenhouse gas
  inventory and formulate carbon reduction policies
·	Physical risks (climate-related impact):
1.Heavy rainfall/strong typhoons and floods leading to increased transportation and equipment repair costs, strengthen operational management resilience: 
Real-time monitoring of rainfall, water levels, and water conditions, establish emergency response coordination mechanisms for equipment.
Drought causing increased transportation costs, war room management: Adopt optimized inventory and flexible transportation scheduling
3.Describe the financial impact of 
extreme weather events and actions 
for transformation.

4.Describe how climate risk 
identification, assessment and 
management processes are 
integrated into the overall risk 
management system.
The Sustainability Development Committee established six cross-departmental working groups, which identify and assess climate risks and opportunities 
every 2-3 years, and review annually whether adjustments are needed based on the actual operating environment. Regular risk management is carried out 
by each business unit in accordance with internal control procedures, and each working group regularly reports its progress and results to the Sustainability 
Development Committee.
To reduce the impact on production capacity and on-time delivery caused by typhoon-related work stoppages, Billion arranges advance production or overtime 
to meet shipping requirements. At the same time, considering the risk impact of climate change on the Company, a risk assessment form has been developed 
to conduct risk assessment surveys on key operational activities of each department. By considering factors such as threats, impacts, and degree of influence, 
the Company identifies the key risks it currently faces and formulates corresponding risk control strategies to reduce risks. To ensure the effectiveness of 
risk assessments, it is required that risk assessments be reviewed at least every three years in conjunction with adjustments to the Company's business 
and personnel. Additionally, emergency response measures have been formulated. When risk disasters occur, the responsible department head executes 
notification work according to the site's disaster emergency response measures, and the crisis event management team coordinates crisis handling and 
subsequent recovery work to maintain the Company's continuous operations.
