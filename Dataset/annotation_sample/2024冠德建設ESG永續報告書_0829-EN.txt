Cover Concept
In a softly lit corner of the city, a building stands quietly in the city. It doesn’t clamor for attention or deliberately 
stand out, but rises naturally from the greenery, as if a work of art co-existing with the city and the nature 
themselves.
Under the shade of trees, people are reading and others are playing and running with their children. In the 
distance, a cyclist rides through the morning mist. These slices of life intertwine, giving the city a natural rhythm 
and providing a glimpse of beautiful and joyful living.
The cover is conceived as a starting point for a vision. 
It reminds us that cities can co-exist with nature, spaces can provide warmth to people, and the essence of 
architecture lies not only in providing shelter, but also in fostering a connection between people, the environment, 
and generations to come.
This piece of work is Kindom’s response to "How to Build the Future". 
We believe that every detail can be the starting point for change, from environmental protection (E), social 
participation (S) to governance integrity (G). This is not a slogan, but a choice realized in every brick, every 
window, and every green leaf.
What you see is not just a picture – it’s an attitude, a vision for a sustainable life, and an ongoing commitment.

Internal control
　The Company has established the sustainable development management procedures. The 
sustainability manager is responsible for the overall planning and communication integration of 
the annual ESG report. Each year, the five major sustainability functional teams and respective 
responsible departments are responsible for compiling the information required by the report 
and writing the report content. After the preparation of the annual ESG report, it is submitted to 
the Sustainability Task Force for preliminary review, and the Administrative Management 
Division is responsible for preparing the report. The Division performs an internal audit of the 
report, and the report is finalized by the Sustainable Development Committee after approval.
External Assurance  GRI 2-5 all

Standard v3, Type 1 moderate assurance. Please see refer to Appendix for details on the

Development’s 2024 Sustainability Report, the British Standards Institution has no financial 
relationship with the Company.

Kindom Development’s short-, medium- and long-term planning
Facing the global sustainability trend and Taiwan’s national path to net-zero emissions by 2050, Kindom Development promotes its sustainability blueprint with systematic 
planning, and clearly outlines three phases focused on establishing robust systems, creating value, and aligning with international standards.
From 2021 to 2050, Kindom Estate is focusing on "Legal Compliance and Benchmark Establishment" by achieving environmental management systems such as ISO 14001 
and ISO 14064-1, and introducing SBTi carbon reduction goals and TCFD climate-related financial disclosures, to build a strong management foundation for low-carbon 
transition. For the social aspect, we are promoting sustainable shared value through initiatives focused on employee wellbeing and public benefit. With regard to the 
governance aspect, we are actively aligning with GRESB and other assessment systems to enhance governance transparency and risk management, and progressively 
establish a foundation for sustainable operations.
By 2026, we will enter the "Sustainability Transition, Value Creation" phase. Kindom will focus on innovative low-carbon buildings, strengthening community services, and 
the introduction of green energy, in order to move towards RE100 and a 42% carbon reduction goal. Simultaneously, we will systematize and digitize our charitable work and 
corporate social engagement results, expanding our positive impact on internal and external stakeholders, and building transformation momentum via the three aspects of 
products, services, and capital.
From 2028 to 2030, we will fully promote "Going Global, Partnering To Reach Near Zero". Kindom Development focuses on aligning with the SBTi’s carbon reduction 
pathway and achieving near-zero building standards, strengthening its implementation of the SDGs and continuously developing an internationally competitive ESG building 
brand. On the governance front, we are actively benchmarking against DJSI global assessment indicators, in order to comprehensively enhance our sustainability visibility and 
influence both domestically and internationally.

Governance｜Ethical enterprise & joint innovation
1. Ranked at "21% to 35%" in the Corporate Governance Evaluation for TWSE- and 
TPEx-listed companies, the Company firmly secures a leading position among domestic 
construction firms.
2. Completed the establishment of the ISO 14001 Environmental Management System for 
developed land and buildings to align with climate and natural risk management and 
international governance principles.
3. Reinforced the information security system and obtained ISO 27001 certification 
4. The Company’s disclosure and transparency of sustainability information have earned it 
numerous international ESG reporting awards.
Environment｜Environmental sustainability
1. In 2024, cumulative carbon reduction reached 13.88% compared to the base year, putting the 
Company on track to meet its SBTi 2030 goal of a 42% reduction.
2. The "One Tree per Household" program, a green living initiative, engaged residents in the joint 
adoption of 925 native trees. This effort is estimated to sequester approximately 111 tCO₂e.
3. The Company promotes the use of low-carbon building materials by performing seismic tests on 
low-carbon concrete in collaboration with construction sites and academic institutions. In 
particular, it encourages the use of materials such as PLC low-carbon concrete, electric arc 
furnace rebar, and aluminum formwork, with an expected maximum carbon reduction potential 
of up to 14.42%.
Social｜Happy society
1. In 2024, 306 internal training courses were held, totaling 2,441.4 hours of training and an 
average of 23.7 hours per person.
2. The Company's "Veteran Employee Support Program" was awarded the Age-Friendly 
Employer Certification and the Taiwan iSports Certification.
3. A total donation of NT$6.968 million was raised to support education, medical care, and 
underprivileged group-related lectures.
4. In partnership with the Foundation, the Company promoted a reading program by opening 
libraries in all public facilities within its residential projects through a charity book drive that 
collected 4,700 books. The initiative reached over 90,000 people and benefited more than 
300 children and their families, advancing rural education.
Product & supply chain｜Smart cities 
1. In response to the GRESB guidelines for developing low-carbon buildings, the Company 
has formulated standardized ESG policies for its construction projects.
2. All new projects will be equipped with solar power. The Company is promoting 
energy-saving buildings with an EUI of 110 kWh/m²/year and plans to collaborate with 
tenants in the future to purchase green energy.
3. Through sustainable supply chain initiatives focusing on "green procurement, regulatory 
agreements, and advocacy," the Company is vigorously promoting low-carbon management 
and fostering sustainable collaboration across the entire supply chain.
4. To comply with FSC’s Reference Guide for Sustainable Economic Activities, future building 
projects will target at least a Silver rating for green building certification. The Company will 
also pursue additional certifications for smart, healthy, and seismic-resistant building 
features.

Culture of common good, making sustainability part of daily lives
　Sustainability is also realized through culture. Based on the philosophy of "Sharing and 
Mutual Benefits ", we promote community common reading, forest conservation and tree 
planting, and various social initiatives, in order to establish connections between people and the 
land and to drive positive virtuous cycle in the society. The Kindom Yushan Education 
Foundation promotes environmental protection through reading and aesthetics in living, with the 
goal of making sustainability a part of everyday life for future generations. At the workplace, we 
create a diverse and inclusive atmosphere, and promote camaraderie through daily interactions, 
team-building activities, sports days and employee clubs. We also strengthen employee 
engagement with comprehensive remuneration and benefits, in order to establish the 
cornerstone of a happy and successful company.
Architecture is not merely a space, but a vessel for hope
　From a single brick to a building, a city block, and a new district, Kindom has always believed 
that architecture is more than just a space but a vessel for hope with value. The 2024 
milestones are just the beginning. We will continue to collaborate with our customers, 
employees, communities, and supply chain partners to implement ESG principles throughout 
the city, creating innovative urban lifestyles and leading the way towards a more sustainable 
future, conveying happiness and showcasing the beauty of Taiwan to the world.
Message from the Management
Plan for the city of the future, achieve mutual benefits, and sustainable living
　The global economic and policy environments continue to impose various challenges. Kindom 
Development leverages its 46 years of accumulated expertise and keen understanding on 
urban dynamics to demonstrate exceptional resilience in the face of such challenges. In 2024, 
Kindom Development’s consolidated revenue reached NT$28.678 billion, an annual increase of 
47%; net profit after tax reached NT$5.55 billion, an annual increase of 132%; and earnings per 
share reached NT$10.25. The successful acquisition of projects of Y16 Banqiao Station and 
Children Welfare projects further demonstrate Kindom Development’s ability to seize 
opportunities in times of uncertainty, and to proactively plan diverse products, in order to inject 
lasting sustainable momentum into the city.
Transforming from building developer to urban manager
　Upon entering the "Kindom 3.0" phase, based on the core of KINDOM LIVING, we promote 
urban upgrading to transform buildings from mere spatial carriers into spaces capable of 
integrating life, ecology, and culture. For the large complex development projects of the 
Taipower Nangang Northern Storage Project, the Taiwan Railways Taipei Station E1E2 Project, 
and Taipei Xing-Er-Xing-San Project, Kindom plans to introduce AIoT, digital operations, 
precast modules, and low-carbon building materials. In addition, with the international urban 
renewal experience, we aim to deeply integrate public space design, art and cultural facilities, 
and neighborhood life into the local community, in order to construct a foundation extending 
from "service", thereby establishing a platform for living and learning, providing residents with a 
comfortable neighborhood experience, and transforming "urban governance" from a slogan into 
a visible and tangible part of daily life.
Sustainability commitment and co-existence with the environment
　Sustainability is at the core of Kindom’s DNA. We adopt a whole-lifecycle approach and 
comprehensively implement our "Building Product Development With ESG Policy" to reduce 
carbon emissions throughout the entire process from design and construction to the usage 
phase, including the use of electric arc furnace steel rebar, precast components, smart energy, 
and renewable energy, in order to allow buildings to co-exist with the climate. In addition, we 
also collaborate with supply chain partners to establish carbon reduction standards and to 
establish industry competitiveness jointly. Accordingly, we have successively obtained 
numerous awards, including the Five-Star GRESB rating, the TIBA Smart Building Platinum 
Award for Taipower Nangang North Storage Project, the UK International Property Award for 
Kindom Minquan Building. All of these awards affirm our commitment and dedication to 
sustainability.

Material Topic Management
Material topic assessment process   GRI 2-14, 3-1, 3-2
　To ensure the sustainability report accurately responsd to stakeholders’ concerns and reflects 
the Company’s substantial impacts on the economy, environment, and society, Kindom 
Development continuously improves its materiality assessment process and disclosure 
procedures in alignment with the GRI Standards and international disclosure trends. In 2024, 
the Company continued to adopt the material topics identified in 2023. After comprehensive 
assessment by the Sustainable Management Committee, it was concluded that a full 
assessment every two years can achieve a better balance between the rate of topic change and 
the Company’s internal resource allocation. The current material topics remain highly consistent 
with the operational strategy and continuity and substantial correlation still exist. Therefore, 
although new material topic identification has not been performed in 2024, the overall 
identification process still remains consistent with the following standard procedures and is used 
as the basis for the materiality management framework.
Step 1. Identify key topics
　(1) Summarize the industry attributes: Inventory the Company's business items, business 
models, products or services, industry types, workers, etc., and analyze all the industry 
attributes related to the Company.
　(2) Identify key topics: In addition to the actual occurrence of major positive and negative 
events in the past, the Company, through stakeholder communication and feedback, global 
norms and standards (GRI industry standards, SDGs, TCFD, CDP, and SASB, etc.), also 
considers potential risks or opportunities, industry norms and standards, and industry 
benchmark companies, etc. In doing this, we are able to take a complete inventory of key 
sustainability topics related to the Company.
Step 2. Determine material topics
　(1) Stakeholder feedback: We engage the Company's top-level managers, experts, and 
external stakeholders through a combination of interviews, focus meetings, and questionnaires 
to comprehensively rate the two major indicators of positive and negative impacts of each key 
topic: "level of impact" and "probability of occurrence".
　(2) Assess the level of impact: Different calculation standards are set according to the nature 
of the key issues. Negative impact includes the severity, scope of impact, and reversibility of the 
event, while positive impact includes the level of impact and scope of impact.
　(3) Assess the probability of occurrence: Calculated based on the probability of occurrence of 
the impact.
　
　(4) Ranking and determining material topics: After calculating the degree of impact and 
probability of occurrence of each issue, the Sustainability Task Force sets materiality threshold 
scores for the two major indicators, selects current material topics, and reports to the chairman. 
The identification results of material topics are discussed and resolved.
Step 3. Material topic information reporting
　The Sustainability Task Force will determine the international standards corresponding to 
material topics, review the management policies and objectives of material topics, collect annual 
data, ensure that important sustainability information has been fully disclosed in the Report and 
fully respond to stakeholder issues of concern. After the completion of the annual ESG Report, 
the chairman reexamines the contents of the report again, and forwards it to the Sustainability 
Management Committee and the Board of Directors for review and resolution. This is to ensure 
that there are no doubts of inappropriate or misrepresented information disclosed.
Step 4. Continuous review
　The Company conducts self-assessment on the material topic disclosure content and 
implementation status annually, and compares year-over-year trends to determine whether the 
identification strategy and response mechanism need to be further adjusted. Although new topic 
identification was not performed in 2024, the annual review operation was still conducted to 
verify the management approach, goal setting, and actual performance for the material topics 
identified in the previous year, in order to be used as a basis for the topic identification and 
strategy optimization of the next period.

Material Topic Management Strategy
The Board of Directors of the Company is the highest decision-making and supervisory unit for the 
management of material issues. The Sustainability Task Force is designated to be responsible for 
the management items of material issues, including taking stock of and reviewing the 
management approaches for material issues, making recommendations for optimization and 
improvement, and establishing diverse stakeholder communication channels. On a regular basis, 
the suggestions of stakeholders are integrated to determine the type and impact of their opinions, 
with countermeasures or response policies formulated to be reported to the Board of Directors. 
The Board of Directors and the Sustainable Development Committee convene meetings with the 
Sustainability Task Force at least once semi-annually to discuss the management of material 
topics and formulate the direction of sustainable development and strategic objectives for the 
coming year.

1.1.3 Tax Policy  Tax Approach  GRI 207-1 i, ii
　Kindom Development adheres to the local tax regulations of each operating location. In 
addition, according to the international tax guidelines, we have established tax strategies 
complying with local laws and the Group’s operation model. For different tax jurisdictions, the 
Company establishes reasonable transfer pricing principles based on functions, risks, and 
resource allocation, in order to implement tax compliance and to fulfill the tax responsibilities.
　To manage tax risks, the Company has established a tax governance related unit to be responsible 
for the continuous tracking, assessment, and management of possible impacts of any tax related 
regulatory changes on the Company's operations. The unit is also responsible for internal 
communications and management mechanisms, in order to ensure that the operational decisions are 
consistent with the tax strategies, and to promptly identify and handle potential risks. The Company’s 
overall tax management is based on the principles of legality, transparency and stability. In addition to 
striving to reduce audit and dispute risks, we also prioritize tax responsibility in each operating region, 
and appropriately retain profits where economic value is actually generated. Kindom Development will 
continue to enhance tax information transparency, in order to use it as an important basis for 
communication with stakeholders.
Kindom Development's tax strategy and management approach
Tax Risk Management GRI 207-2 a
　Changes in tax laws and regulations may increase the Company's effective tax rate, increase 
the Company's tax burden, and affect the Company's net profit. In order to effectively manage tax 
risks, the tax enforcement unit regularly reviews the trends of tax laws and regulations in each 
country of the operating site, identifies potential tax risks, and reports to the Board of Directors to 
discuss and formulate countermeasures. When new operating activities or transactions are 
generated, the potential tax impacts are assessed in advance to formulate the optimal tax strategy.
Whistleblowing Mechanism (GRI 207-2 b)
　In addition, the Company has established an independent complaint channel. If employees or 
external stakeholders discover unethical or illegal tax practices, or mechanisms that may 
jeopardize the Company's tax integrity, they can file a complaint, which will be handled by the tax 
governance unit. No unethical or illegal tax practices occurred during the year.
Stakeholder Communication (GRI 207-3)
　The Company formulates tax strategies with reference to laws and regulations, letters of 
interpretation, or news announced by the competent authorities. In addition, the Company regularly 
participates in tax seminars or courses organized by the tax authorities in various regions to update 
the latest regulatory information, and periodically discusses with accountants the possible impact of 
future tax laws and regulations on the Company. For tax policies or tax incentives that are required 
to be reviewed by the competent authorities in advance, the Company obtains approval from the 
competent authorities in accordance with the regulatory requirements. The Company's income tax 
filings have been audited and certified by an external accounting firm to ensure that the Company 
pays reasonable taxes in the local area. At Kindom Development, we cooperate with tax

1.2.1 Governance Structure  GRI 2-9
　The Board of Directors is the Company’s highest governing body, and is composed of 
directors elected by all shareholders in accordance with applicable laws and the Company’s 
Articles of Association. The Board of Directors is responsible for the Company’s business 
decisions and oversight. Three functional committees, the Remuneration Committee, Audit 
Committee and Sustainable Development Committee, are established under the Board of 
Directors. These Committees provide professional review and supervision of directors’ 
remuneration policies, ensure the authenticity and accuracy of financial reports, and manage 
the development and implementation of key ESG performance goals.
In addition, the Internal Audit Office is established as an independent audit unit to ensure the 
effectiveness of internal control systems according to the annual audit plan, in order to 
strengthen operational risk management and legal compliance. Each functional committee must 
report its operational results and key resolutions to the Board of Directors on a regular basis, in 
order to ensure the transparency and effectiveness of the governance mechanisms and to 
protect the overall interests of the Company and its stakeholders.
　The Board of Directors forms an important bridge between the management team and the 
Companyy and is responsible for exercising supervisory and decision-making power as 
authorized by the shareholders, while also considering the interests of all stakeholders and 
establishing operating principles and sustainability strategies. The Board of Directors is also 
responsible for appointing the management team to supervise the Company’s overall 
operations, and monitoring the decision-making and execution of sustainability strategies.
　The Company’s Board of Directors is elected via the candidate nomination system and are 
elected by the shareholders’ meeting from the candidate roster. Their term of office is 3 years 
and they are eligible for reelection. Nomination and election standards refer to the evaluation of 
the candidates’ educational background, experience, professional expertise, integrity and 
correlation to the Company’s operations and growth. The Board must include at least one 
member of each gender and the diversity policy is established based on the Company’s 
operations, business type, and development needs. The current Board of Directors consists of 9 
directors (including 3 independent directors), and the male to female directors ratio is 8:1. The 
Chairman serves as the chair of the Company’s highest governance body and does not 
concurrently hold the position of President, in order to ensure an appropriate division of 
responsibilities and uphold a balance between decision-making supervision and operational 
execution.
　The Board of Directors convenes meetings once quarterly in principle. A total of 8 Board 
meetings were convened in 2024, with an average actual attendance rate of 87.5%, 
demonstrating the Board’s active fulfillment of its function and the responsibility of its 
decision-making supervision. For the attendance of each director and the content of the 
proposals, please refer to p.15 of the 2024 Annual Report of Kindom Development. Link

1.2.1 Governance Structure  GRI 2-9
Remuneration structure of directors and top-level managers
　The Company’s director remuneration system is reviewed by the Remuneration Committee and 
then submitted to the Board of Directors for approval, in accordance with applicable laws and 
regulations and the Company’s Articles of Association. In addition, remuneration adjustments are 
made based on a comprehensive consideration of the common level adopted in the same 
industry, individual director’s responsibilities and performance. The Board of Directors’ 
remuneration structure includes cash compensation (fixed), director's compensation (variable), 
business execution fees and various allowances. In addition to fixed salary and pension, 
performance bonuses are calculated based on the achievement status of individuals and overall 
company performance indicators, in order to strengthen the correlation between director’s 
responsibilities and operation results. Please refer to p.8 of Kindom Development 2024 Annual 
Report for details on directors’ remuneration, the remuneration bracket table and distribution 
standards. 
　The remuneration system for the senior management is also considered by the Remuneration 
Committee and then submitted to the Board of Directors for approval. The remuneration content 
includes fixed salary and pension, as well as performance-based bonuses calculated according to 
the business performance indicators, in order to encourage the management to focus on 
achieving the Company’s long-term operational goals. The retirement system applies to senior 
management on the same terms as other employees. For the 2024 senior management salary 
and range table, please refer to p.11 of the 2024 Annual Report of Kindom Development.
Board of Directors and Senior Management Resignation and Retirement Policy
　The notice period for directors and senior management of Kindom Development is determined 
in accordance with local government regulations. The number of days of notice and the calculation 
of severance pay are the same as those for other employees. In addition to severance, no other 
benefits in money or in kind will be paid to departing directors and senior management. For 
details, please refer to p.62 of the 2024 Annual Report of Kindom Development. 
Correlation Between Remunerations of Directors and Senior Management and ESG Performance
　To align director performance with the Company’s sustainability vision and short, medium and 
long-term goals, Kindom Development has established the senior management remuneration 
system approved by the Remuneration Committee and the Board of Directors in accordance with 
the "Director and Manager Performance Evaluation and Remuneration Management Procedures", 
in order to evaluate the core competencies of directors, to strengthen directors’ accountability and 
to link performance to remuneration. The system specifies that sustainability-related indicators 
shall be included with the business performance. As of 2022, an incentive mechanism linked to 
ESG performance has been formally introduced.
The ESG-related indicators accounts for 30% of the President’s performance evaluation items, 
including achievement rate (20%) for the five main sustainability actions (smart carbon reduction, 
pollution reduction, circular economy, green energy, and healthy living) and corporate governance 
assessment results (10%). The remaining evaluation items are related to the business 
performance (50%), talent cultivation (10%) and internal process optimization (10%), etc.
Senior managers are evaluated based on their departmental operation goals, and the evaluation 
items of sustainability performance (10%) and talent cultivation (5%) are also included. The 
sustainability aspect includes the implementation results related to expansion of carbon reduction 
applications, supply chain collaboration, strengthening of information disclosure and 
cross-departmental resource integration. The aforementioned indicators are used as an important 
basis for short-term and long-term incentives and remunerations.
Recovery mechanism
　To ensure the implementation of the Company’s sustainable operation and principle of integrity, 
the Company has explicitly stipulated that if a material risk event occurs that may damage the 
Company’s reputation, or if significant internal management failure occurs, for the director or 
employee verified to be involved in misconduct after investigation,  the originally approved 
remuneration or incentive is to be revoked in accordance with the Company’s relevant regulations, 
and bonuses previously issued will be recovered, in order to ensure that the remuneration system 
aligns with action liability.
Board of Directors Performance Evaluation  GRI 2-18
　The Company has established a board performance evaluation system, conducting an annual 
internal self-assessment of the board and individual directors. In addition, it has been decided that 
evaluation is conducted by an external professional independent organization or a team of 
external expert scholars at least once every three years starting from 2022, and the evaluation 
shall be completed by the end of the first quarter of the following year, and the results are reported 
to the Board of Directors for disclosure in the Company's annual report and website. The 2024 
Board of Directors performance evaluation results were positive. Please refer to page 16 of the 
Kindom Development 2024 Annual Report for details on the performance evaluation criteria. 
Conflicts of Interest Management  GRI 2-15
　Kindom Development has clearly specified that directors must comply with the principle of 
recusal for conflicts of interest in the Articles of Association, Audit Committee Charter, 
Compensation Committee Charter, and Sustainability Committee Charter. When a Board meeting 
proposal involves the interests of a director, his/her spouse, or a relative within the second degree 
of kinship, or a company that the director controls or with which it is affiliated, the director must 
proactively disclose the nature of the relationship during the meeting. If the matter may cause 
damage to the Company’s interests, the director must not participate in the discussion or voting on 
the proposal, and must recuse himself/herself from such discussion or voting, and he/she shall not 
vote on behalf of other directors. The names of relevant directors, description of the conflict of 
interest and recusal status shall be recorded in the meeting minutes properly. In addition, directors 
and managers are required to annually complete related-party transaction declarations and submit 
the results.

Climate Change Management Framework
Climate change 
governance
The Sustainable Development Committee established by the Board of Directors serves as the highest decision-making unit for opportunities and risk management of climate change. 
The Sustainability Task Force will act as the implementation unit for managing and identifying climate change risks and opportunities, and regularly report relevant risks to the 
Sustainable Development Committee. The relevant procedures are assessed and managed according to the "External Assessment Operation Procedure" established by the 
Company. Finally, the relevant assessment procedures are reviewed by the internal auditor and the Finance Department will make recommendations on the financial impact.
Climate change 
strategy
The Company operates in the real estate industry. Opportunities and risks related to climate change can be categorized as physical, transitional, and opportunities. The Governance 
Committee has formulated relevant strategies for the following different aspects:
[Physical]: The main risks are "immediate and long-term". For high-risk fields, the possible forms and probability of risks are inventoried, and commercial insurance or preventive 
measures are evaluated to reduce the financial impact.
[Transition]: The main risks are "policies and regulations, technology, market, and reputation". For related risks, the Sustainable Development Executive Committee develops 
corresponding strategies and management policies for these risks, and establishes indicators for regular tracking.
[Opportunity]: The main opportunities are "resource utilization efficiency, renewable energy development, innovative products and services, market, and operational flexibility". 
The Sustainable Development Executive Committee develops strategies and management approaches for expanding competitive advantage based on opportunities, and 
establishes indicators for regular tracking.
The Sustainable Development Executive Committee, in collaboration with relevant units, identifies short-, medium- and long-term climate risks and opportunities for different 
businesses, formulates reasonable scenario assumptions, assesses the degree of impact of climate risks on related businesses, and formulates corresponding management 
actions or response strategies. When necessary, management indicators and targets will be set to strengthen the Group's control over specific climate risks and grasp the 
opportunities. For the risk and opportunity indicators and targets that have been set, please refer to the climate change indicators and targets in this chapter for details.
Climate risk 
management
In accordance with ISO31000 risk management principles and guidelines, the introduction of the risk system management of the Company has been planned. The identification and 
assessment of internal risk issues has also been completed according to the scope of Kindom Development's risk management policies and procedures, with the mitigation measures 
of risk issues implemented.

• The Company considers environmental laws and regulations, customer requirements and stakeholder expectations, and external initiatives. Top-level managers review the

Impact scenario:
　In order to achieve the goal of net-zero emissions by 2050, the National Development

manpower arrangements. Suspension of construction will lead to backward project 
progress and affect revenue. High temperatures will increase the risk of health hazards 
and even death of personnel. The above risks will lead to service interruption of 
enterprises, indirectly resulting in reduced revenue and increased expenses.

Zero net policy and regulatory response strategies:
1. Constructing green buildings: Kindom is committed to improving the green energy efficiency of its buildings, with the goal of achieving green building and low-carbon building dual certifications for all new construc-
tion projects starting in 2025. We will also re-evaluate and adjust the product structure, and invest funds for additional research and development to adapt to the changes brought about by the use of low-carbon raw 
materials, including structural seismic resistance and safety tests. This way, we are able to ensure that our products will still be able to meet the requirements under the new standards.
2. Reduce product carbon emissions: Kindom implements the carbon reduction plan for buildings, and it is expected to reduce carbon emissions by 20% per unit area by 2030. Since 2023, Kindom has adopted 
precast construction methods and used rubble concrete and low-carbon cement in selected projects, in order to replace traditional cement with low-carbon cement.
3. Reduce the company's own carbon emissions: Kindom Development will implement carbon reduction according to the SBTi goals. With 2022 being the baseline year, we are committed to reducing emissions 
by 42% by 2030 and achieve net zero by 2050.
4. Sustainable supply chain management: Manage suppliers by levels, and prioritize the procurement of raw materials and products with low carbon emissions, request suppliers to provide information on raw 
materials on a case-by-case basis, and plan carbon reduction 
5. Low-carbon sales centers: Starting in 2025, Kindom will transform all its sales centers to low-carbon sales centers through implementation measures of such as, LED lighting, solar panel installation and 
energy-efficient equipment. Accordingly, it is able to lower carbon emissions from sales centers and convey ESG principles to potential customers, thereby improving the brand image.
Strategies for increasing procurement costs and demand for low-carbon raw materials:
1. Increased cost transferred to sales prices: Due to the increased costs associated with the purchase of low-carbon raw materials, Kindom plans to transfer these costs to sales prices 
starting in 2024; however, it will be implemented through a staged transfer method on an annual basis.
2. Price advantage of low-carbon supply chain: The low-carbon supplier platform has been established in 
2024 in order to obtain lower unit prices through long-term cooperation.
3 . Reducing construction material waste: Since 2024, Kindom has collaborated with a low-carbon building design 
alliance to find the best approaches for low-carbon construction.
4 . Increasing construction projects’ use of green energy: Since 2024, Kindom Development has implemented the solar panel additional installation plan, and the total installed capacity is expected to 
reach 12 kW. The increase of use ratio of renewable energy is beneficial to obtaining energy efficiency marks.
Strategies to changes in rainfall patterns and extreme changes in climate patterns:
1. Enhancing building’s risk avoidance design: In response to the potential impacts of climate change, Kindom has strengthened the building’s risk avoidance design since 2024 by improving seismic performance 
and installing energy and water storage facilities. The upstream design end proposes the concept of risk aversion, and the downstream construction side proposes specific disaster prevention plans. At the same 
time, the natural disaster assessment system is strengthened to reduce the probability of the construction project encountering climate impacts in the future.
2. Strengthening of typhoon prevention measures: To prevent injuries to employees and damage to the construction site caused by typhoons, we will strengthen the typhoon prevention measures and conduct 
construction site inspections. During a typhoon, we will closely and immediately observe the current site conditions and reduce the number of essential personnel. We also take out insurance against related 
hazards in order to minimize the related risks.

072
　Kindom Development has adopted low-carbon sustainability as a long-term 
development strategy, and actively responds to global energy conservation and carbon 
reduction challenges. We integrate principles of health, sustainability, and energy 
efficiency into the planning and design of all our construction projects, with the goal of 
providing residents with buildings that are both environmentally sustainable and 
comfortable to live in.
Source design for energy efficiency
　Kindom Development incorporates comprehensive green building design requirements 
from the planning stage. By employing passive energy-saving strategies, we are able to 
effectively minimize building energy consumption at the source, including limiting building 
depth to 14 meters or less, in order to ensure indoor spaces benefit from natural 
ventilation during cooler months, thereby significantly reducing the need for air 
conditioning. Furthermore, Kindom has adopted strict design standards for building 
exteriors, such as a maximum window-to-wall ratio of 35% for office buildings and below 
25% for residential buildings, in order to minimize energy consumption. Window areas 
are also carefully planned to be equipped with external shading or balcony structures to 
minimize east and west sun exposure, in order to promote natural cooling and insulation. 
Additionally, the exterior design of full glass is prevented to further reduce the building's 
overall energy consumption.
Efficient air conditioning and ventilation management
　In the design and planning of air conditioning systems, Kindom Development follows 
strict control principles, and prevents excessive installation of equipment. In addition, air 
conditioning capacity is appropriately designed to meet actual space needs and to 
minimize energy waste. The Company selects high-efficiency chiller units and air 
conditioners, and utilizes variable frequency control technology to save energy. 
Furthermore, we also promote the use of total heat exchangers and CO₂ 
concentration-based outdoor air control systems, in order to achieve the goals of indoor 
air quality and energy saving at the same time. Moreover, during the cooler seasons of 
autumn and winter, the interior design allows all windows to be opened, enabling natural 
ventilation to replace air conditioning and to further minimize energy consumption.
Smart Lighting and Planning of Lighting
　In terms of energy-saving lighting, Kindom focuses on incorporating natural light into its 
building designs, in order to prevent energy waste from excessive use of indoor artificial 
lighting. In the architectural design process, it is recommended to use high-efficiency 
lighting, such as fluorescent lamps with electronic ballasts, metal halide lamps, and 
sodium lamps, all of which offer substantial energy savings. Furthermore, we have 
carefully considered the practical needs of indoor lighting design, rationally planned 
zoning switch installations, and integrated automatic dimming and daylight sensing 
control systems, in order to ensure that residents are able to enjoy a comfortable visual 
environment while achieving energy-saving goals at the same time.
The Kindom Mingquan Commercial Building is designed and built based on low-carbon 
principles, which is aligned with international trends and standards.

073
Water-saving design and water resource recycling and reuse
　Kindom Development emphasizes the sustainable management of water resources. To 
effectively save and manage water resources and water consumption, all construction projects 
incorporate specific water-saving measures customized to their individual conditions and needs. 
First, for domestic water consumption, Kindom insists on the full installation of water-efficient 
equipment with water-saving labels, including dual-flush toilets, water-saving showerheads, and 
water-saving faucets, in order to actively lower water consumption in the daily living environment. 
Secondly, Kindom also actively introduces a rainwater retention system and water reclamation 
system. Through careful planning of collection and purification of rainwater and domestic sewage, 
the reclaimed water is reused for non-drinking purposes, such as landscape irrigation, toilets, or car 
washing, in order to significantly reduce the need for tap water. Furthermore, in terms of the 
landscape planning, Kindom prioritizes drought-resistant native plants and permeable paving 
techniques, in order to effectively reduce landscape irrigation needs and to further improve water 
efficiency and sustainability on the property site.
Performance management during the building operational stage
　Kindom Development implements sustainable principles in both architectural planning and 
hardware design aspects, and actively ensures high performance and quality throughout building’s 
entire lifecycle with effective management and maintenance processes. To achieve such goal, 
Kindom Development is promoting the implementation of Building Energy Management Systems 
(BEMS). This system is able to precisely monitor energy consumption during building operations 
and to adjust electricity strategies and equipment configurations, in order to optimize comfort while 
minimizing energy consumption at the same time.
Continuous Verification and Strategy Adjustment Mechanism
　To ensure living spaces meet operational expectations from the start, Kindom Development has 
developed a comprehensive function verification program. All newly constructed buildings are 
rigorously tested before residents move-in, in order to guarantee that equipment is functioning 
properly and system performance meets design specifications. Furthermore, Kindom Development 
has established a robust information monitoring mechanism, regularly reviewing the effectiveness 
of its energy and water resource policies and continuously improving and adjusting its strategies to 
ensure these policies remain current and effective.

Smart building implementation cases
Kindom Development × Taipower Nangang Commercial Project 
Lead sustainable future office model with smart technologies
　The Taipower Commercial Building, located in Nangang District, Taipei City, is constructed by Kindom 
Development. It is a 21-story building with 4 basement floors, featuring a composite steel and reinforced 
concrete structure with a floor area reaching 95,504.19 square meters. This project has achieved the 
golden grade certification in both EEWH green building standards and IB intelligent building standards, 
and its design has been planned in accordance with the LEED system. We aim to balance energy savings, 
carbon reduction, health and comfort, and operational efficiency, in order to construct a smart building that 
is both forward-looking and practical.
　The design vision centers on "Buildings as platforms, IoT as the core" to emphasize the dual integration 
of smart and green technologies. By introducing innovative materials and intelligent systems, we aim to 
establish a healthy, safe, convenient, and low-carbon office environment, serving as a demonstration site 
for urban renewal and sustainable development.
Smart Technology × Sustainability Strategy
This project incorporates multiple digital and energy technologies, prioritizing overall building performance 
and resource management starting from the design stage.
　1. Energy efficiency management
　• Energy Use Intensity (EUI) is 110 kWh/m²/year, which is superior than most commercial building 
standards, resulting in annual carbon emissions of 4,421,258.98 kg CO₂e.
　• Installation of a 255-panel rooftop solar system with a total capacity of 98.17 kWp, and an estimated 
annual power generation capacity of 93,513–102,660 kWh, with annual carbon reduction reaching 
85.75 tonnes of CO₂e.
　• Introduction of three high-efficiency centrifugal 800 USRT chillers, along with IE3 motors and 
BAC-certified cooling towers, in order to reduce energy consumption by more than 12%.
　2. Water resources and indoor environmental quality
　• The rainwater reclamation rate of this project reaches 12.6%, and the annual water reclamation is 
87,962 m³.
　• Indoor air quality is maintained at good levels, with CO₂ concentrations of 650 ppm and TVOC 
levels of 560 ppb, both superior than common standards.
　3. Smart system integration
　• Energy Management System (EMS) supports peak electricity adjustment, visual energy 
consumption analysis and fault diagnosis, and smart electricity, water, and gas meters are also 
integrated in the system.
　• A central monitoring system covers lighting, air conditioning, water supply and drainage, fire 
protection, surveillance, and access control, and includes an uninterrupted power supply to ensure 
stable operation.
　• BIM integration platform assists the building operations and maintenance decision-making, in order 
to reduce equipment maintenance costs by 12% and to improve management transparency and 
efficiency.
Multifaceted contributions of sustainability value
Environmental aspect
　Through systematic integration and energy-efficient design, we can effectively lower overall building 
energy consumption. In addition, with the installation rooftop solar power supplying 100% green electricity, 
practical climate action is further enhanced. Implementation of smart lighting and air conditioning control 
technologies are able to reduce carbon emissions while ensuring comfort.
Social aspect
　Focusing on user health and work experience, we have installed the UNO real-time air quality 
monitoring system, a smart lighting system simulating natural light changes, and have also introduced 
antibacterial sensor lamps to effectively inhibit common airborne pathogens and enhance indoor hygiene 
protection, thereby meeting expectations for healthy office spaces in the post-pandemic era.
Economic aspect
　The rent performance ratio reaches 1.28, indicating a significant competitive edge for this project in the 
area.
Annual operating cost is approximately 12% lower than those of traditional buildings, and it requires less 
staff per unit area, effectively demonstrating the economic benefits of smart buildings in administrative 
management.
Meaning of smart buildings for sustainability
　From planning to implementation, the challenges in this project are beyond technical integration and 
also include user behavior, cross-system data consistency, and continuity of subsequent operations. The 
implementation of smart buildings requires coordination in all stages, starting with the design phase, and 
involving architecture, mechanical and electrical systems, information and management units, to create an 
efficient and functional system.
　The value of smart buildings is not determined by how much technologies are applied, but whether they 
meet user needs, improve energy efficiency, and maintain consistent operational performance through 
daily management. Kindom Development will continue to leverage the experience gained from this project 
and implement similar smart and low-carbon strategies in future developments, in order to progressively 
improve resource efficiency in construction and operations  and to respond to the challenges of urban 
transformation and climate change.
The "Taipower Nangang Commercial Project" is located at the Eastern District 
connecting to the Xinyi Planning District, and is expected to drive mutual growth 
and development in Nangang and to become a new landmark in Taipei City.

Turning discarded building materials into "another table"
Kindom Development’s circular furniture practice
　In daily construction processes, waste concrete, tiles, sand and gravel are often treated as 
unusable construction wastes and such wastes are typically handled through removal and landfill 
disposal or stockpiling. However, Kindom Development sees the potential for circular economy, 
and reintroduces these seemingly insignificant offcuts into the design and manufacturing 
processes to create aesthetically pleasing and functional conference tables, thereby implementing 
the transformation process from construction waste to sustainable furniture.
　This small project, named "Circular Furniture", has started with a simple question: "Could these 
leftover building materials have a second life, beyond just being removed and disposed?" We 
recycle materials like concrete, sand, and tiles from construction sites. These materials are then 
crushed, selected, and mixed before being transported to our partner factory in Longtan, Taoyuan 
for prototyping and pressing into large-size quartz slabs. Each large slab's dimension is 320cm x 
160cm and weighs 250kg, which can be ultimately cut into 8 circular tabletops of 80cm in diameter.
These recycled boards are not only simple and durable in appearance, but also contain 
approximately 20% of recycled material, made up of a blend of natural quartz sand and artificial 
resin. After design optimization and being fitted with metal table legs, the finished table is suitable for 
use in residential common areas, lobbies, or office recreation areas. These tables are of practicality 
and aesthetics, and they also represent a starting point for material circularity and value creation.
This furniture is more than one single design experiment, but demonstrates Kindom 
Development’s first step in establishing a building resource reuse system. We are progressively 
establishing internal recycling, transportation, and reprocessing procedures (SOPs), and we aim 
to further expand its implementation in the future to transform construction waste into functional 
recycled products with compelling stories.
　This table might just be the beginning. We wonder: "Other than a table, what else could it be?" 
In the future, it might become a coffee table, a bar counter, or even be displayed in an art 
exhibition, eventually finding its way into people's everyday lives. To become an object that not 
only showcases clever design but also promotes sustainable practices, thereby continuously 
expanding the benefits of circular economy.

Building carbon footprint application and implementation mechanism
　To implement carbon reduction management across the entire building lifecycle, Kindom 
Development has been actively adopting a building carbon footprint assessment system since 
2022, which aligns with international GRESB standards, the SBTi carbon reduction initiative, and 
Taiwan’s LCBA/LEBR low-carbon building certification mechanisms. Concurrently, a third-party 
LCA model has also been integrated to establish a systematic and scientific process for carbon 
emission disclosure and application, in order to establish the foundation for achieving a near-zero 
building goal in the future.
I. Application Strategy and Preliminary Preparation
　The application process is managed and coordinated by the Sustainable Development Project 
Team, integrating carbon management principles during the early stage of building development to 
ensure that environmental sustainability is implemented during the design and planning phases. 
The team performs inventory of the design documentation and ensures stable construction plans, 
and a full BOM list based on annual development progress, in order to use such documents and 
information as the subjects for the introduction of carbon footprint assessments in priority. 
Furthermore, the trend in carbon management indicators for the same year is also considered, and 
a preliminary carbon sensitivity analysis is conducted to evaluate the difference in carbon 
emissions performance between the proposed design and the baseline building, serving as a key 
basis for determining whether to proceed to the formal application process.
II. Scope of Investigation and Document Preparation
　The assessment of a building’s carbon footprint complies with the ISO 14040/14044 and the 
guidelines announced by the Environmental Protection Administration, Executive Yuan, covering 
the entire lifecycle of a building from material acquisition, manufacturing, transportation, 
construction to future demolition. Inventory procedures require the collection of the following 
essential documents and data:
　• Design drawings and structural configuration (such as SRC/RC structures)
　• Quantities of various building materials, unit weight, source distance, and transportation method
　• Mechanical and electrical equipment specifications and energy consumption data
　• Estimated operational lifespan and energy consumption pattern
After all documents and data are summarized, a third-party LCA consultant then inputs them into 
an international building carbon footprint assessment model to calculate the carbon emission 
difference between the baseline scenario (traditional design) and the proposed design (actual 
solution), in order to obtain the carbon emission factors per unit area (kgCO₂e/m²·yr) and total 
carbon emission data.
III. Internal Collaboration and Process Management
　The carbon footprint application process requires collaboration between the design unit, 
engineering unit, procurement unit and the sustainability team. The design department provides 
precise material specifications and drawings, while the engineering department assists in 
comparing them to actual construction methods. The procurement department tracks the source 
and transportation distance of building materials, and the ESG team is responsible for integrating 
the data and ensuring its consistency. Prior to submission, the documents and data must be 
reviewed by the internal audit unit and a supervisor to ensure that the calculations comply with 
GRESB and LCBA review requirements.
　To streamline processes, Kindom Development has established a "Building Material Carbon 
Emission Database" that compiles carbon emission factors and sourcing distances for frequently 
used materials, in order to provide a quick reference for estimations during the design phase. In 
addition, we have also established the "Building Carbon Footprint Diagnosis Table" to assist the 
design team to assess whether window area, thermal insulation, lighting and equipment energy 
efficiency are optimized for carbon emissions.
IV. Result Certification and Future Planning
　As of 2024, Kindom Development has completed carbon footprint applications for its Songchang 
Section Residential project and its G5 Station Commercial Project, achieving carbon emission 
reductions of 11.3% and 14.42% respectively with the acquisition of the 
　LEBR official certification mark. Looking into the future, Kindom Development will continue to 
expand its carbon footprint application coverage, with a goal of achieving LCA certification for at 
least 30% of all development projects by 2025 and full implementation for all projects by 2030. We 
will also expand our modular carbon inventory tool, cultivate internal Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) 
capabilities, and collaborate with upstream material suppliers to establish transparent carbon 
emission data standards, in order to progressively achieve standardization of carbon management 
during the development phase and to promote the transformation of the building industry to move 
towards net zero.

Relevant natural risk assessment of land 
development:
Application of TNFD Disclosure Framework
　Facing the challenges of global natural capital loss and the climate risk of rising 
temperature, Kindom actively responds to the TNFD (Task Force on Nature-related 
Financial Disclosures) disclosure framework by integrating nature-related risks and 
opportunities into the land development and operation management processes. The 
Company not only conducts environmental impact assessments in compliance with 
regulations, but also further introduces natural topic management mechanismsinto the 
development processes, in order to construct a prospective and integrated system in 
response to natural risks from the processes of strategic planning and governance to 
on-site execution.
I. Governance Aspect
　To effectively govern natural risks and impacts, Kindom Development has 
established "Land Development Management Procedures" to clearly define the 
operation process and review mechanisms for the development stages of "Cultivation 
Project", "Execution Project", and "Project Land Delivery Project". The Development 
Investment Division acts as the responsible unit in charge of consolidating regulatory 
analysis, conducting site environmental surveys, performing market assessments, and 
evaluating investments, and then submitting relevant documents and data to the 
President and Chairman for approval on the execution according to the Company’s 
governance structure. In terms of governance of nature related topics, Kindom not only 
complies with regulations by conducting environmental impact assessments, but also 
proactively integrates projects such as forest carbon sink, biodiversity protection, and 
ESG information disclosure through GRESB strategies, ISO 14001 system and 
supervision of the Sustainable Development Committee, in order to ensure that natural 
capital is adequately considered from the initial stage of development decision-making 
and to implement the risk management principle of prevention in priority.
II. Strategy Focus
　In terms of the project development strategy, Kindom actively complies with tree 
protection requirements from the National Housing and Urban Regeneration Center 
and local authorities, and plans for the preservation, relocation, and increased green 
coverage of existing large trees on construction sites. Green building standards with 
greening indicators, ecological water cycle design, and green space preservation are 
integrated into the development design, in order to effectively preserve and enhance 
the site’s natural ecological functions. In addition, "Kindom In terms of the project 
development strategy, Kindom actively complies with tree protection requirements 
from the National Housing and Urban Regeneration Center and local authorities, and 
plans for the preservation, relocation, and increased green coverage of existing large 
trees on construction sites. Green building standards with greening indicators, 
ecological water cycle design, and green space preservation are integrated into the 
development design, in order to effectively preserve and enhance the site’s natural 
ecological functions. The "Kindom Dazhizhan" project specifically preserves over 300 
pings of "Carbon Footprint Plaza" as green open space within the urban renewal area. 
This space serves not only as a cooling area and carbon sink for the community, but 
also further provides public ecological benefits. In addition, we are collaborating with 
the Forestry and Nature Conservation Agency to promote forest carbon sink projects, 
and we expect to plant 5,000 trees with the carbon sink benefits of 31.5 tons, in order 
to achieve the value of carbon reduction and biodiversity at the same time.
III. Risk and Impact Management
　Environmental impact assessments are conducted at the beginning of development 
or urban renewal projects, and a survey of the surrounding area’s existing conditions 
is also performed, in order to conduct inventory, simulation and analysis of potential 
natural risks, such as tree health, geological and hydrological factors, and impacts on 
ecological habitats. The design and planning phase also includes integrated planning 
on the flood retention capacity under extreme weather conditions, mitigation of the 
urban heat island effect, and long-term maintenance of community green spaces, 
thereby reducing financial impacts and the potential for operational disruptions due to 
natural capital risks.
IV. Metrics and Targets
　The Company has established quantitative indicators and long-term goals related to 
natural capital:
　1. All new projects are established with the targets of green building certification 
above silver grade and energy efficiency labeling. In addition, smart building 
technologies and green building materials are also introduced depending upon each 
individual project's condition.
　2. A site’s natural capital inventory data is integrated into full-cycle development 
control, in order to track green coverage area, mature tree retention rate, and 
ecological conservation effectiveness.

Urban development strategy and results in practice
　Kindom Development is dedicated to become a promoter for urban revitalization, and is committed to 
participating in government-led public urban renewal projects for the long term. With our professional 
development expertise and integrated capabilities, we are able to improve urban functionality, enhance 
public facilities, and ultimately enhance overall urban welfare. We believe that the core value of urban 
renewal lies in public benefits, which includes both physical improvements (such as pedestrian areas, parks, 
and public amenities), and urban revitalization and increased land value of the economic aspect.

initial decision-making, comprehensively considering factors such as the ecological environment, geological 
conditions, climate risks, and the local economy, in order to ensure that development projects are aligned 
with regional coordination and long-term sustainability. To minimize the impact on the environment and 
cultural assets, development site selection focuses on existing developed areas, and locations with 
convenient access to diverse public transportation are considered in priority. In addition, we also strive to 
preserve local historical relics and cultural heritage at the same time.

the planning phase, such as reserving rooftop space for potential solar panel installations or 
pre-provisioning electrical capacity and power distribution interfaces to facilitate future renewable energy 
equipment installation and grid connection. All of these efforts demonstrate our commitment to the goals of 
net-zero emissions and sustainable urban development.

4.2.1 Guarantee for Building Safety and Sustainable 
Quality
                                    —Seismic Certification Mark 
　Taiwan is located in the Circum-Pacific Seismic Zone, an area with frequent 
earthquakes, and building safety has long been a major public concern. Earthquake 
damage over the past years has demonstrated that inadequate structural design and 
poor construction quality can significantly increase the risk of disaster. To strengthen 
the structural resilience of buildings, the Architecture and Building Research Institute, 
Ministry of the Interior has launched the "Seismic Certification Mark" system since 
2003, and the Taiwan Architecture & Building Center has been entrusted to establish 
a complete process for verifying seismic quality and certifying the mark.
　The seismic certification system is divided into two phases: Phase 1 refers to the 
"Seismic Design Certification Mark", and Phase 2 refers to the "Seismic Certification 
Mark", covering the entire quality control process of building construction from design 
drawings and structural planning to on-site construction and acceptance inspection. 
This system is based on an application filing system, and applicants must submit an 
application for the certification. After qualifying the third-party professional verification, 
the certification mark can then be obtained.
I. Design Phase: Focus on both structural reasonableness and 
construction feasibility
　During the design phase, applicant is required to submit structural design plans, 
drawings, analysis reports and constructability details for review, and the Taiwan 
Architecture & Building Center assembles a verification team to perform document 
and drawing review. Review focus includes:
　• Whether the building complies with seismic design regulations;
　• Whether the earthquake isolation, energy dissipation and other advanced 
technologies are used;
　• Whether the structural configuration is reasonable and has adequate strength and 
resilience;
　• Check for components that are difficult to construct and for those where 
construction quality cannot be controlled easily. 
The verification team provides correction suggestions according to the actual 
condition, and once corrections are confirmed,  the "Seismic Design Certification 
Mark" is issued as the first stage of quality certification.
II. Construction Phase: Introduction of special supervision and 
third-party inspection
　After the design certification is obtained, the building project still requires a series of 
on-site inspections by third parties during construction before the "Seismic 
Certification" mark can be obtained. The focuses for the system at this stage are as 
follows:
　• Third-party verification: A verification agency assigns qualified personnel to 
regularly inspect the site to determine whether construction aligns with the design, 
including critical construction processes of foundation work, column and beam 
structures, steel rebar tying, and concrete pouring, etc.
　• Special supervision system: For specific seismic designs or construction type, the 
project owner must additionally hire a structural professional supervisor with at least 
three years of experience to perform on-site supervision, including full-time or 
periodic inspections of floor areas and sectioned construction, etc.
　• Verification of as-built drawings and construction work: Inspectors verify that the 
on-site construction outcome aligns with the design drawings and project plans. Any 
construction changes are re-inspected and assessed to ensure consistent quality 
throughout the project.
　• Verification results and handling: When the inspection is determined to be 
qualified, an official "Seismic Certification" mark and a compliance report are then 
issued. In addition, the mark must be displayed publicly at the building entrance and 
construction site for consumers and stakeholders to easily identify the mark.
III. System Characteristics and Building Quality Guarantee
　The purpose of the seismic certification system is not for "legal compliance" only, 
but to further establish a quality assurance system with substantial effect, and its core 
characteristics include:
　• Structural safety is controlled from both the "source of design" and "on-site 
construction";
　• Design documents, construction details and quality records must be verified by a 
third party;
　• Implementation of stationed supervision system to compensate the issue of 
uneven distribution of traditional construction supervision resources;
　• All process documents must be traceable to enhance the public transparency and 
corporate self-discipline.

4.2.2 Application Outcomes of LEED Certification 
System and Minquan East Project 
LEED System Introduction
　LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) is an international green building rating system 
developed by the U.S. Green Building Council (USGBC). It provides a comprehensive assessment of 
buildings based on standardized indicators, itemized scoring and third-party review mechanism, covering 
aspects of water resources, energy efficiency, material use, indoor environmental quality, job site selection 
and transportation, etc.
　LEED emphasizes management throughout the entire building lifecycle, covering design, construction, 
operation and user behavior while promoting high-performance, low-impact, and traceable building practices, 
making LEED one of the most representative green building standards internationally.
Certification framework and evaluation method
　LEED offers various evaluation versions based on the building’s purpose. The Minquan East Project adopts 
the "BD+C: Core and Shell" version, applicable to commercial building projects where the developer controls 
the external structure and public facilities. The total score is 110 points, with ranking of Certified Grade (40–49 
points), Silver Grade, Golden Grade and Platinum Grade (80 points and above).
Scoring categories include the following eight items:
　　• Site selection and transportation 
• Materials and resources
　　• Sustainable site 
• Indoor environmental quality
　　• Water consumption efficiency 
• Innovative design
　　• Energy and atmosphere 
• Regional priorities
Case example: Kindom Minquan East Project obtained the platinum grade certification
　Kindom Development’s Minquan East Building project, with its application submitted in 2023, is a complex 
commercial building with 15 ground floors and 4 basement floors, and the project has successfully obtained 
the LEED v4 "Core and Shell" platinum grade certification with a total score of 83 points. The project 
integrates energy conservation, carbon reduction and green design strategies, demonstrating the Company’s 
commitment to sustainable building practices.
Highlights of this project which helped it receive its high evaluation score are described in the following:
Site selection and transportation planning
　The site is located in a high-density area, adjacent to numerous bus and MRT lines, and various functional 
living facilities are available within a distance of 800 meters. In addition, bicycle parking spots,  shower rooms 
and electric vehicle charging stations are also provided to promote low-carbon transportation.
Site planning and landscape design
　Rainwater reclamation basin and flood retention facilities are planned to store 90% of rainfall; 
high-reflectivity pavement is used for the ground, and outdoor lighting is designed to minimize light pollution. 
Comprehensive planning includes more than 25% of green space and open areas, in order to enhance the 
site’s friendly relationship with its surroundings.
Water meters are installed to monitor various water usage systems, in order to facilitate subsequent operation 
management and continuous optimization.
Energy efficiency management
　Implement VRV water-cooled air conditioning and LED lighting, and integrate with light sensors and 
energy monitoring systems, in order to improve building energy efficiency. In addition, separate meters 
are installed and green lease clauses are specified, in order to guide users to participate in sustainable 
operations jointly.
Water resource consumption efficiency
　The drip irrigation method is adopted for the building's exterior landscaping, and 100% of the irrigation 
water comes from reclaimed rainwater. For the interior, equipment with water-saving labels are selected 
for use, and submeters are also installed to monitor various water usage systems, in order to facilitate 
subsequent operation management and continuous optimization.
Material selection for use and Waste disposal
　LCA building material carbon emissions analysis is introduced, and eco-friendly building materials with 
certifications of EPD and HPD are selected for use. In addition, a waste management plan to recycle 
more than 75% of waste during the construction stage is adopted, in order to implement the building 
material recycle and reuse principle.
Indoor environmental quality management
　MERV13 filters and air quality sensors are used to control dust and pollutant spread during the 
construction period, and the ventilation and airflow design are also enhanced. In addition, natural lighting 
and visual transparency are adopted to improve user comfort.
　The LEED system is used as an internationally recognized green building evaluation tool, in order to 
assist companies in systematically considering strategies for energy, water, and material use during the 
design phase and to also ensure implementation quality through third-party verification. Kindom 
Development has introduced the LEED Platinum Grade Standards to the Minquan East Project, 
demonstrating our dedication to both building quality and energy efficiency, and also reflecting the 
Company’s gradual shift from building-oriented to operation-oriented sustainable governance.

construction and prior to occupancy. Upon completion of the construction project

Joint sustainable practices with residents
Kindom Tianying Community’s low-carbon transformation promotion
　Kindom Development has always believed that the Company should contribute more than constructing and 
delivering buildings and should continuously respond to the evolving needs of occupants and the environmental 
responsibilities. While upholding the philosophy of community common good, we actively help established 
communities to participate in local government low-carbon subsidy programs. In 2024, we collaborated with the 
"Kindom Tianying Community" to successfully launch a range of tangible environmental improvements and 
educational programs, demonstrating our dedication to continuous environmental responsibility even after the 
sale of house units.
New Taipei City’s "Low-Carbon Community Renovation Subsidy" system offers resources to support community 
residents and management organizations in promoting low-carbon initiatives, including energy generation, 
resource recycling, ecological pest control, and low-carbon transportation. Communities must submit project 
proposals in the prescribed format, and after the proposals are reviewed, improvement works are performed, 
followed by submitting progress reports to apply for subsidies. This system emphasizes a governance structure 
co-established by the three parties of "local autonomy, government support, and industry collaboration", in order 
to effectively promote local environmental awareness and practical implementation.
　Kindom Development assisted the "Kordis Tianying Community" in linking residents, homeowners, and the 
management committee, allowing them to participate in collaborative design of viable improvement plans and to 
implement a range of sustainable activities and hardware upgrades. With regard to resource recycling, Tianying 
Community has implemented food waste initiatives and a fallen leaf composting system, waste cooking oil 
recycling, and resource reuse education signage installation, etc., in order to increase residents’ participation in 
reducing and recycling household waste.
　In addition, the community also focuses on promoting environmental knowledge and developing climate 
awareness. Through net-zero carbon emission seminars, plant-based air purification courses, and educational 
activities on biological control and ecological disease prevention, we aim to establish residents’ basic 
awareness on air quality, ecological conservation, and climate change, and to further transform their awareness 
into daily practices. It is particularly worth noting that Kindom Tianying Community has also organized a 
hands-on solar power workshop for families, in order to promote sustainability through daily practices and 
intergenerational learning, as well as strengthen community bonds and shared values.
　Furthermore, regarding the low-carbon transportation aspect, the community encourages carpooling and 
green commuting, putting up posters to advocate for residents to reduce their carbon footprint by using shared 
transportation vehicles or public transportation. In addition, living topics of concern to residents are also 
included, such as the introduction of energy-saving equipment and discussions on electricity management, in 
order to continuously strengthen their participation and support for these initiatives.
　Kindom Development understands that the value of a good building lies not only in its construction, but also in 
the community and quality of life. We will continue to act as a "Promoter" in the stage after house delivery, in 
order to support communities in exploring and implementing a wider range of environmental improvement 
initiatives. We aim to make each construction project a sustainable, self-evolving community, extending 
sustainability from buildings to lifestyles and fulfilling our long-term commitment to the environment and society.

　According to the Ministry of Labor (MOL)’s statistics, the occurrence of

occupational safety. To ensure the safety and health of the Company’s 
personnel (including employees, customers, and contractors), Kindom complies 
with existing occupational health and safety regulations in Taiwan as well as 
other workplace safety and health regulations and laws in a bid to create a 
high-quality, healthy, and safe work environment. We rigorously demand our 
employees and contractors to abide by occupational safety regulations, and in 
the spirit of total participation, we have reinforced our employees’ education and

oversight, demanding our contractors to implement occupational safety

shortcomings, in the hope of embedding the concept of safety first in our 
corporate culture.

training-related regulations; we also regularly host relevant safety and health 
education and training to strengthen our employees’ safety awareness. The

　• Fire drill: Emergency fire drills and evacuation training allow our employees

IV. Reinterpretion of Architecture – Kindom Development Salon 
Lectures
　In an era of rapid changes, architecture is no longer simply about physical construction; it 
acts a carrier for the rhythm of society, cultural memory, and human empathy. Facing 
technological innovation, climate change, urban transformation, and evolving lifestyles, the 
Kindom Yushan Education Foundation continues to organize the "Kindom Development 
Salon Lectures" and "Architecture International Lectures" series of events. During these 
events, leading thinkers and practitioners from around the world are invited to engage in 
cross-disciplinary discussions, responding to the challenges and responsibilities of 
contemporary architecture and fostering forward-thinking ideas for architectural education, 
industrial transformation, and sustainable urban development.
　The 2024 Architecture Salon Lecture focused on the topic of "Reinterpretation: Redefining 
Architectural Spatial Elements", established based on the core theme of the previous year. 
The event took place at Songshan Cultural and Creative Park—a former tobacco factory built 
in 1937 that has been reimagined as a new space for art, design, and cultural 
experimentation. The present lecture was co-organized and hosted by Chih-Yu Lin, Vice 
President of the School of Design and Associate Professor in the Department of Architecture 
of Ming Chuan University, and Yen-Lin Chen from Yen Partnership Architects. Through the 
interpretation of both spatial concepts and relevant issues, the lecture aimed to guide the 
architectural community through a process of rethinking and re-evaluating its values.

the presidents of five major architecture-related organizations – including the Taiwan 
Institute of Architects, the American Institute of Architects Taipei (AIA Taipei), Women in 
Architecture Taiwan (WAT), the Chinese Society of Interior Designers (CSID), and the CIAA 
Association – to participate in a special dialogue series called "Interviews with Presidents", 
which analyzes architecture’s response and innovation in the face of new challenges from 
multiple perspectives. Speakers explored how architecture can address issues of 
sustainability, digitalization, culture, and gender, demonstrating the industry’s collective 
intelligence through shared insights and thoughtful deliberation.

I. Sponsorship for Rural Education
1. Grass Book House Sanxia Headquarters and Daxi Branch, and Grass Life Skills Academy
　The Grass Book House is a community support system built by the Sustainable Urban and 
Rural Care Association and the Culture Art and Nature for disadvantaged children aged 6 to 
18 in Sanxia and Daxi. The Grass Book House links schools, communities, and families to 
care for children from high-concern and high-risk families to build up their self-confidence 
and have the power to make a difference in the future through courses such as character 
education, emotional counseling, and multi-functionality. To care for disadvantaged groups in 
remote townships and bridge the rural-urban gap, the Foundation has supported the 
operating expenses of Grass Book House Sanxia Headquarters for seven consecutive years 
and Daxi Branch for three consecutive years, demonstrating its support by taking action. In 
turn, we can endow youths with the confidence and ability to embrace the future.
　On the other hand, in order to enable teenagers to continue their studies, the Sustainable 
Urban and Rural Care Association has further established the Grass Life Skills Academy, 
offering multi-functional education, through baking, woodcarving, carpentry, coffee, beauty 
courses. Career counselors are on-hand to provide career guidance, and school teachers 
provide emotional education; the three parties work together to turn around the future for 
vulnerable children and ensure these children no longer lost and find self-worth. The 
foundation has supported the Grass Life Skills Academy diverse occupational training 
program for the sixth year, in the hope of helping more disadvantaged youths to cultivate 
their craft skills, learn, and pursue their dreams in a stable learning environment.
　These children resemble grasses. To accompany the growth of grasses and provide them 
with proper irrigation and nutrients, they are guaranteed an opportunity to grow into beautiful 
flowers and even trees that provide shade for others. In 2024, the Foundation donated NT$2 
million, benefiting 22 students and 20 families at the Grass Book House; 15 students and 12 
families at the Daxi Branch; and 18 students and 16 families at the Grass Life Skills 
Academy.

Conclusions

Environment, Social and Governance (ESG) in this report, so that appropriate planning and target-setting can be supported.  
In our professional opinion the report covers the Kindom’s inclusivity issues.   
Materiality

performance of Kindom and its stakeholders.  The sustainability information disclosed enables its stakeholders to make 
informed judgements about the Kindom's management and performance.  In our professional opinion the report covers 
the Kindom’s material issues.   
Responsiveness

Policy for Kindom is developed and continually provides the opportunity to further enhance Kindom’s responsiveness to 
stakeholder concerns.  Topics that stakeholder concern about have been responded timely.  In our professional opinion 
the report covers the Kindom’s responsiveness issues.   
Impact 
Kindom has identified and fairly represented impacts that were measured and disclosed in probably balanced and effective 
way.  Kindom has established processes to monitor, measure, evaluate, and manage impacts that lead to more effective 
decision-making and results-based management within the organization.  In our professional opinion the report covers the 
Kindom’s impact issues.

disclosures are reported, partially reported, or omitted.  In our professional opinion the self-declaration covers the 
Kindom’s sustainability topics.
