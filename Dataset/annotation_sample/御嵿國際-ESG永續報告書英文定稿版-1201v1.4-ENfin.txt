Message from the Chairman 
Building a Sustainable Vision, Realizing Corporate Value 
Since its inception, Toplus Global has been a leader in providing "integrated services that 
merge exquisite dining experiences with lifestyle aesthetics." We are dedicated to 
transforming the restaurant industry, shifting the focus from singular consumption moments 
to comprehensive, aesthetically rich lifestyle scenarios. Guided by our philosophy of 
"Innovation, Excellence, and Shared Prosperity," we firmly believe that sustainable corporate 
growth must balance operational achievements with social responsibility and environmental 
stewardship. Recognizing the urgency of global climate change, Toplus Global has integrated 
ESG governance into its core business strategy. We have set a clear, ambitious goal to 
achieve net-zero emissions across the entire group by 2030, demonstrating our generation's 
commitment through decisive action. 
 
In the first phase of our sustainability transformation, we focused on "establishing 
foundations and mastering data." By 2024, Toplus Global had fully completed ISO 14064-1 
greenhouse gas inventory verification across the entire group, successfully obtaining 
certification from the international third-party organization DNV. Concurrently, we 
implemented energy efficiency enhancements and carbon reduction measures. For instance, 
the Amazing Hall Taoyuan Flagship Venue pioneered the upgrade of its central air 
conditioning chiller system, significantly reducing operational energy consumption and 
laying a solid foundation for future carbon neutrality. Building on these Phase One 
achievements, we have initiated Phase Two's in-depth transformation. Toplus Global is 
concurrently applying for carbon credits issued by the Environmental Protection 
Administration and pursuing Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) certification to ensure 
future carbon reduction actions align with international standards. Regarding products and 
supply chains, we actively promote seasonal local ingredients, fully support locally sourced 
poultry eggs and fish-power co-generation agricultural products to reduce transportation 
carbon footprints. Through innovative menu design, we minimize overall food waste, 
deepening our low-carbon business model. 
 
Toplus Global consistently centers its corporate culture around a " People-centered " 
philosophy, prioritizing employee health, safety, and career development. All employees 
undergo systematic ESG training, extending sustainability awareness from senior 
management to every team member. The group actively participates in social welfare 
initiatives, promotes a Diversity & Inclusion workplace culture, and implements a mutually 
beneficial cycle with suppliers and consumers through restaurant reservation systems and 

 
8 
precision procurement models, demonstrating its profound commitment to society and the 
environment. 
 
Toplus Global has set a clear sustainability vision toward 2030, underpinned by four key 
drivers of progress: Operational Net-Zero Transition, Sustainable Supply Chain Upgrades, 
Robust Governance and Transparent Disclosure, and Deepening Social Inclusion Impact. We 
are committed to leading with decisive action and strategic innovation. By joining hands with 
our employees, partners, and the broader community, we will move progressively toward a 
zero-carbon future and establish Toplus Global as a sustainability leader in the Taiwanese 
and Asian restaurant sectors. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Sun Jeng-chyang, Chairman of Toplus Global

Background 
This report is one of the diverse communication channels established by Toplus Global Co., 
Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as Toplus Global or the company) to review the effectiveness of 
its sustainable business practices. It addresses the material issues prioritized by 
stakeholders and short-, medium-, and long-term plans, articulating current strategies and 
actual performance. Upholding transparency, integrity, and objectivity, we are fully 
committed to pursuing the vision of sustainable corporate development. This report aims 
to position Toplus Global as a practitioner of sustainable development and aspires to 
become a benchmark for sustainability within the hospitality industry. 
Report Publication 
This report is the "Sustainability Report" for the preceding fiscal year issued by Toplus 
Global Co., Ltd. The previous edition was published in 2024, with the next edition 
scheduled for 2026. The report is published annually. This edition primarily discloses 
information covering the 2024 fiscal year (January 1 to December 31, 2024), including 
corporate sustainability and social responsibility commitments, policy pledges, 
stakeholders, material issues, and corporate governance. 
 Report Publication Date: August 2025 
 Report Frequency: Annually 
 Assurance Provider: Crowe (TW) CPAs 
Reporting Boundary and Approach 
The issue boundary of this report encompasses topics of concern to stakeholders both 
within the organization (Toplus Global) and externally, including shareholders/investors, 
individual customers, public associations, and suppliers. The content is structured around 
material topics defined by Toplus Global, with other issues presented separately as special 
topics. The reporting boundary encompasses the Head Office, 7 Amazing Hall banquet 
venues, 1 DingXian Tainan Danzai Noodles outlet, 1 YanPang Handmade Bakery, 3 

 
10 
Hokkaido Keki cake shops, as well as Amazing Duck and Cross Strait Club, totaling 20 
locations. This report's framework details Toplus Global's actions and recent achievements 
across environmental, social, and governance dimensions, discloses material issues, and 
links these to the 17 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). It also 
references the "Regulations Governing the Preparation and Filing of Sustainability Reports 
by Listed Companies," adheres to Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) standards, and follows 
the Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB) framework and the Task Force on 
Climate-Related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) guidelines. 
 
 Restatements of information 
In 2024, Toplus Global introduced the premium brand "Cross Strait Club" and the shopping 
mall brand "Amazing Duck." Organizational changes necessitated a realignment of reporting 
boundaries, with the following impacts: 
(1) Growth in Employee Numbers 
The Group's total workforce increased by nearly 40%, primarily due to the expansion of 
operational teams for new brands and additional kitchen and front-of-house staff. This 
multi-brand strategy enhances market competitiveness while elevating overall brand 
image and profitability. (See Section 4.1.1 Workforce Structure in this report) 
(2) Changes in Organizational Greenhouse Gas Emissions 
In response to organizational changes, the greenhouse gas emissions baseline year has 
been reset to 2024. The combined emissions from Scope 1 direct emissions and Scope 2 
indirect energy emissions increased by 5.57% compared to the previous year.

Primary Products and Services 
Toplus Global Co., Ltd. operates a portfolio of renowned dining brands including "Ding Xian 
Tainan Danzai Noodles," "Amazing Hall," "Cross Strait Club," "Amazing Duck," "Hokkaido 
KEKI Cake House," "YanPang Handmade Bread Shop," " Amazing Dim Sum," and " Amazing 
Cantonese Roast Duck Restaurant." With 14 production facilities, Toplus Global holds a 
significant position in Taiwan's food service market. 
Beyond its stable food service operations, Toplus Global established subsidiaries " Royal 
Living Travel Service " and " Vision Living Co., Ltd " in 2023 to expand into tourism and hotel 
management. In 2024, it formally entered the high-end dining and mall-based food service 
sectors by incorporating "Cross Strait Club" and "Amazing Duck " diversifying its culinary 
offerings to provide customers with comprehensive services. Toplus Global aims to integrate 
catering, tourism, and hotel management, striving to transform into a "low-carbon hospitality 
service platform" to advance globally and maximize group value. 
Since 2023, Toplus Global Group has established an ESG team and committed its subsidiaries 
to conducting carbon audits, advancing toward carbon neutrality. The group will collaborate 
with relevant suppliers to join the carbon reduction initiative, building a "Zero-Carbon 
Hospitality Platform."

Competitive Advantage 
As market demands grow increasingly diverse, competition in the wedding banquet 
catering sector intensifies. Beyond traditional chain restaurants, new service formats like 
specialty restaurants, outdoor weddings, and micro-weddings are emerging, reshaping 
traditional banquet spaces and service models. These innovative offerings cater to 
modern consumers' desire for creativity, personalization, and variety, while introducing 
greater competitive challenges. Moreover, modern consumers increasingly value 
regional distinctiveness and originality when selecting catering services. A growing 
number of patrons prefer experiencing cuisine with local character and authentic flavors, 
while also seeking novel cultural and experiential activities. Consequently, catering 
services offering unique offerings rooted in local cultural heritage will gain greater 
market appeal. 
Our company possesses years of catering service experience and continuously enhances 
service quality and innovation capabilities based on this foundation. By integrating hotel 
management expertise with professional travel knowledge provided by travel agencies, 
we will leverage our strengths in the tourism sector to actively develop new markets, 
break through existing competitive landscapes, and thereby create higher business value 
and competitive advantages.

1.1.  Stakeholders and Materiality 
1.1.1.  Stakeholder Engagement 
Sustainable development and success for a company hinge on meeting stakeholder needs. 
Among numerous stakeholders, companies must identify those who directly impact its 
operations or hold significant relevance to it, and understand their concerns and priorities. 
This is crucial for advancing Toplus Global's ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) 
strategy. 
 
1.1.2.  Process Steps 
To ensure the ESG implementation process and outcomes align with stakeholder 
expectations, Toplus Global follows these steps when identifying stakeholders and 
confirming material ESG topics: 
(1) Stakeholder Identification: Identify all individuals or groups relevant to company 
operations and establish accessible communication channels. 
(2) Collecting Issues of Concern: Gathering various issues of interest to stakeholders, 
including historically significant topics. 
(3) Identify material issues: Screen historical Material Topics to select items that remain 
of ongoing stakeholder concern and significantly impact company operations. 
 
1.1.3.  Stakeholder Identification 
Referencing the AA1000 SES (Stakeholder Engagement Standard) and AA1000 AP 
(Accountability Principles), discussions and meetings were held within the internal cross-
departmental sustainability function team. Following review principles of inclusivity, 
materiality, responsiveness, Impact. The identified key stakeholders are: employees, 
customers, investors, suppliers, and media journalists.

1.2.  Annual Material Topic analysis 
For large enterprises like Toplus Global, ESG sustainability is no longer a mere "bonus 
question." It has become a core element for safeguarding brand reputation, reduce 
operational risks, attract capital, ensure compliance, and enhance long-term 
competitiveness. The material topics prioritized by stakeholders become the focal points for 
corporate sustainability initiatives. Materiality analysis enables companies to strategically 
allocate resources and refine operational strategies to meet diverse expectations. 
 
1.2.1.  Sustainability Issue Collection 
Toplus Global formed a cross-departmental sustainability task force, authorized by the Board 
of Directors and organized around the Management Department. This group meets to 
establish the strategic foundation for the company's sustainability goals and information 
disclosure. Its approach is built upon analyzing historical ESG issues raised by stakeholders, 
validating these against past materiality findings, and aligning with the principles of the GRI 
Standards (economic, environmental, and social pillars). 
 
1.2.2.  Stakeholder Materiality Identification 
Based on the GRI principles (inclusivity, sustainability context, materiality, completeness), the 
cross-departmental sustainability task force performed the materiality assessment for this 
report. The assessment involved evaluating each issue's actual or potential positive/negative 
impact on internal operations and external stakeholders. The task force confirmed six 
consistently stakeholder materiality and four applicable SASB industry-specific topics. These 
core topics, supplemented by other secondary issues, guide the reporting and disclosure 
within this document. The methodology and final findings are described in the subsequent 
section.

2.1.  Corporate Governance and Management Structure 
Toplus Global has established a corporate governance framework through shareholder 
meetings, a board of directors with diverse professional backgrounds, functional committees 
chaired by independent directors (including the Audit Committee, Remuneration Committee, 
and Sustainability Committee), and internal governance and audit units. This framework 
ensures operational transparency, efficiency, and regulatory compliance while strengthening 
oversight of the board to protect shareholder interests and promote sustainable 
development. 
2.1.1.  Group Organizational Structure 
This year's reporting scope covers financial and human resources data as group-wide 
common information. Performance and strategy-related information on ESG issues primarily 
pertains to the catering business (marked in red). In 2024, Toplus Global launched the 
premium brand "Cross Strait Club" and the mall-based brand "Amazing Duck" formally 
entering the high-end fine dining and department store food court markets, driving 
operational scale expansion. Employee headcount grew by nearly 40%, primarily due to 
increased staffing for new brand operations teams, kitchen operations, and front-of-house 
personnel. This multi-brand strategy enhances market competitiveness while elevating 
overall brand image and profitability.

2.1.2.  Industry Value Chain 
As an industry leader in the catering sector, Toplus Global occupies a "midstream" position 
within the industry value chain. Its role involves sourcing basic raw materials—such as fresh 
ingredients and general merchandise-from upstream suppliers. Through its operational 
outlets, the company develops signature dishes and food products bearing the Toplus Global 
brand identity, which are then supplied to downstream consumers or group clients.  
 
 
2.1.3. Highest Governing Body   
2.1.3 Highest Governing Body 
 

 
41 
Toplus Global designates the Board of Directors as the company's highest governing body, 
representing the interests of all shareholders and responsible for deciding major corporate 
matters. These include amending the Articles of Incorporation, appointing, and removing 
directors, approving financial statements, and distributing dividends. Functional committees 
are established under the Board to assist in fulfilling oversight duties and strengthening 
corporate governance. 
 
The Sustainability Committee, comprising two independent directors, ensures independence 
and objectivity in identifying and deciding on sustainability issues. The committee first 
conducts materiality analysis, identifying and prioritizing material sustainability issues by 
gathering stakeholder feedback and reviewing internal/external circumstances and potential 
impacts. Analysis results are submitted to the Sustainability Committee for review and 
approval, with the committee concurrently assessing alignment with the company's long-
term strategy and risk management. Following deliberation, the Sustainability Committee 
approves the final material topics and management approaches for disclosure, ensuring 
appropriate resource allocation and target setting. It then oversees the implementation of 
supervision, control, and approval procedures for the sustainability report content.

(six directors and three independent directors). The members collectively bring extensive 
experience and expertise from diverse fields, including finance, business, legal affairs, and 
management. This breadth of knowledge ensures the Board possesses the necessary 
professional skills for robust decision-making, extending beyond the individual members' 
primary areas of expertise. For detailed information, please refer to the "Investor Relations" 
section on the Company's official website and the "Public Observation Information Station."

2.2.3.  Supplier Management and Food Sourcing G3 
Currently, Toplus Global does not procure products compliant with internationally recognized 
product responsibility standards such as ISO or HACCP. In the future, we will actively 
encourage existing suppliers to obtain relevant certifications and seek new suppliers that meet 
internationally recognized product responsibility standards for procurement. 
Our primary raw materials include seafood, meat, fresh produce, and beverages. Supply 
conditions remain favorable, with stable cooperative relationships established with multiple 
high-quality suppliers. This ensures consistent supply without any instances of shortages or 
interruptions, particularly enabling the company to meet peak demand during large-scale 
events such as wedding banquets. To ensure smooth business operations, we have developed 
a raw material supply plan and adjusts strategies based on market demand. Specific strategies 
are as follows: 
(1) Diversified Supply Sources: 
Through a rigorous supplier selection process, Toplus Global collaborates with 
multiple high-quality suppliers to avoid over-reliance on any single source and 
mitigate supply risks. All ingredients (e.g., seafood, meat, produce) are sourced 
from vetted suppliers and adjusted seasonally and according to demand to 
ensure freshness and sufficiency. 
(2) Flexible Demand Adjustment: 
To mitigate risks associated with market fluctuations, Toplus Global flexibly 
adjusts procurement and product mix, especially during peak periods like 
wedding banquets and corporate events, to ensure stable supply. Supply chain 

 
55 
readiness for pre-booked large-scale events and banquets is established through 
advanced planning of delivery schedules and dedicated inventory reserves. 
(3) Quality Control and Verification: 
All ingredients undergo mandatory food safety inspections upon arrival to ensure 
they are free from contamination and chemical additives. Multiple checks are 
performed, and a comprehensive traceability system is established to guarantee 
the origin of ingredients and maintain consistent quality. 
(4) Emergency Response and Stockpiling Mechanism: 
Toplus Global maintains contingency plans to address logistics delays or 
unforeseen circumstances. We proactively stockpile goods based on demand 
fluctuations to ensure stable supply during peak periods such as wedding 
banquets and holidays. We coordinate with suppliers for priority delivery to 
prevent supply disruptions. 
Our emphasis on supply chain management and food sourcing primarily aligns with New 
Taipei City's "Ingredient Registration Platform" product traceability system, which accounts 
for approximately 43% of our traceability management. We also conduct regular supplier 
evaluations. In 2024, we engaged with 218 food ingredient suppliers. All 218 underwent

value in 2024). The evaluation forms were jointly assessed by the procurement and 
acceptance units, covering: quality, delivery time, cooperation, price, and overall impression. 
Additionally, 26 suppliers completed GRI-related surveys (representing approximately 62% of

locally sourced ingredients, relevant food certifications or validations, environmental impact

3.2.  TCFD Climate-Related Financial Disclosure 
As a company primarily engaged in banquet, catering, and clubhouse operations, Toplus 
Global has also actively expanded into high-end dining and overseas markets in recent years. 
Toplus Global's business model faces multiple financial risks and opportunities under climate 
change. Referencing the TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures) 
framework, we explore relevant risks and opportunities from two major perspectives: 
physical risks and transition risks. We employ two starkly different climate pathways from 
IPCC AR5-RCP 2.6 and RCP 8.5 as parameters for scenario analysis and as the basis for future 
climate assumptions. This enables Toplus Global to proactively integrate climate-related risks 
and opportunities into strategic planning and financial decision-making. Not only can this 
effectively manage potential financial impacts, but it also facilitates transformation to 
enhance the Group's long-term competitiveness and sustainable value. 
3.2.1.  Climate-Related Financial Risk Factors 
1. Physical Risks 
Physical risks primarily stem from the direct impacts of climate change, which may adversely 
affect Toplus Global's operations: 
• 
Frequent Extreme Weather Events: 

 
66 
o Risk Factors: Increasingly frequent extreme weather events such as typhoons, 
floods, droughts, and heatwaves may damage banquet halls, restaurant 
buildings, or equipment, increasing repair costs or even causing prolonged 
business closures and revenue losses. Examples include typhoon-damaged 
banquet hall roofs or flooded restaurant basements. 
o Financial Impact: Replacement or repair costs for damaged assets, increased 
insurance premiums, revenue loss from operational disruptions, and damage to 
brand reputation. 
• 
Supply Chain Disruptions: 
o Risk Factors: Climate change may impact ingredient origins, leading to crop 
failures, reduced fishing yields, and consequently driving up ingredient prices or 
causing supply instability. For instance, droughts affecting vegetable supply or 
extreme sea temperatures reducing seafood production. For Toplus Global's 
overseas operations (e.g., Kumamoto, Japan), local extreme weather could 
disrupt supply chains. 
o Financial Impact: Increased ingredient procurement costs, menu adjustment 
expenses, revenue losses due to supply shortages, and heightened inventory 
management pressures. 
• 
Water scarcity: 
o Risk Factors: The food service industry relies heavily on water resources. 
Prolonged droughts may lead to increased water bills or usage restrictions, 
impacting daily operations such as cleaning and cooking. 
o Financial Impact: Increased water bills, higher water resource management 
costs, and potential service capacity reductions if water restrictions are imposed. 
2. Transition Risks 
Transition risks stem from societal policy, regulatory, technological, and market shifts to 
address climate change: 
• 
Policy and Regulatory Risks: 
o Risk Factors: Increasingly stringent environmental regulations domestically and 
internationally regarding carbon emissions, energy efficiency, and waste 
management, such as carbon fee imposition, stricter pollution discharge 
standards, and restrictions on single-use tableware. As a large-scale catering 
enterprise, Toplus Global may have relatively high emissions and be directly 
impacted. 
o Financial Impact: Expenditures on carbon fees or taxes, costs for equipment 
upgrades (e.g., replacing energy-efficient kitchen appliances, installing waste 

 
67 
treatment systems), increased compliance expenses, and potential fines for non-
compliance. 
• 
Technical Risks: 
o Risk Factors: Pressure to phase out traditional high-energy-consumption 
cooking equipment; widespread adoption of emerging energy-saving 
technologies (e.g., smart kitchen systems, high-efficiency air conditioning). 
Failure to invest promptly may result in operational inefficiency. 
o Financial Impact: Increased capital expenditures for new technology adoption; 
failure to transform may result in diminished competitiveness. 
• 
Market Risk: 
o Risk Factors: Rising consumer environmental awareness favors green dining and 
sustainably operated restaurants. Failure to meet these expectations may result 
in customer attrition. Additionally, heightened market demands for "green 
supply chains" may impact partner selection. 
o Financial Impact: Revenue decline due to customer attrition, damage to brand 
reputation, and potential challenges in recruiting employees aligned with 
sustainability values. 
• 
Reputational Risk: 
o Risk Factors: Failure to effectively manage climate-related risks or poor 
sustainability performance may result in negative media coverage, consumer 
backlash, or investor criticism, damaging corporate image. 
o Financial Impact: Brand value erosion, stock price volatility, and potentially 
increased financing costs.

3.6.2.  Waste-to-Energy 
In 2024, Amazing Hall joined the Waste-to-Energy Program. By connecting waste sources 
with upstream and downstream technical supply chains, contracted vendors collect food 
waste weekly from restaurant outlets. This waste is transported to a biomass energy center 
where it undergoes seven processing steps: feeding, sorting, crushing, dewatering, pre-
mixing, maturation reaction, and discharge. Within three hours, discarded food waste and 
waste oil are converted into bio-fertilizer (compost soil) and wastewater suitable for 
agricultural use. 
 
The 2024 reported volume of discarded food waste was 61.097 tons, yielding approximately 
18.33 tons of convertible fertilizer. With a conversion rate of about 30%, the output is 
distributed to the bioenergy center and production-marketing groups, establishing a 
sustainable circular carbon reduction model.

4.2.  Occupational Safety and Health 
4.2.1.  Occupational Safety and Health Management 
Toplus Global has established the "Worker Safety and Health Operating Regulations" in 
accordance with the Occupational Safety and Health Act to manage and ensure workplace 
environmental safety. These regulations outline the responsibilities and authorities of each 
unit's worker safety and health personnel, department supervisors, and on-site operational 
staff regarding worker safety and health management matters. Specific areas include: 
equipment maintenance and inspection standards, occupational safety training, first aid and 
rescue procedures, preparation, maintenance, and use of protective equipment, as well as 
accident notification and reporting protocols. Relevant procedures are as follows: 
 Step 1: Implement emergency response measures according to the Occupational Safety and 
Health Procedures. 
 Step 2: Immediately notify supervisors, on-site personnel in charge, occupational safety 
personnel, and all relevant personnel. 
 Step 3: For major occupational accidents, report to the labor inspection authority within 8 
hours. 
 Step 4: Conduct post-incident investigation and analysis, formulate countermeasures and 
improvement plans. 
 
All business units of Toplus Global are staffed with certified fire safety managers, 
occupational safety, and health supervisors, and first aid personnel. Equipment maintenance 
and inspections are conducted monthly in accordance with the "Occupational Safety and 
Health Regulations." Training programs cover emergency response procedures, firefighting, 
first aid knowledge, and the use of protective equipment to ensure every employee and 
customer enjoys a safe environment. Concurrently, Toplus Global regularly organizes 
occupational safety and health seminars or courses to reinforce essential workplace safety 
awareness among employees. In 2024, Toplus Global conducted 17 workplace safety and 
health-related seminars/courses.

4.4.1 Food Safety 
We place great importance on food safety and the customer dining experience, and are 
committed to providing safe and delicious meals. Over the past year, we experienced one 
incident related to food flavor perception. During a wedding banquet, guests at the main 
table mistakenly believed a zucchini dish had spoiled due to its sweet-and-sour sauce. On-
site staff immediately notified the chef. After tasting the dish, the chef confirmed the zucchini 
itself was fine, and the sourness originated from the specially prepared sauce. The on-site 
manager promptly provided a detailed explanation to the guests, who accepted the 
clarification upon understanding. To prevent similar food safety concerns in the future, we 
have incorporated this incident into our internal review process. Moving forward, for all 
dishes featuring a mildly sour flavor, we will proactively explain to customers upon serving 

 
86 
that this is a characteristic of the dish, not spoilage, thereby enhancing customer trust and 
satisfaction. 
 
Beyond this isolated flavor perception incident, we have received no other food safety-
related customer complaints over the past year. All customer feedback is treated with the 
utmost importance and is handled by dedicated personnel to ensure every concern receives 
prompt and appropriate resolution. 
We will continue to strictly adhere to food hygiene regulations, conduct regular internal 
audits, and cooperate with inspections by government authorities. This ensures all meal 
preparation processes, ingredient sourcing, and storage methods meet the highest 
standards, striving to provide customers with a safe dining environment. Upholding the spirit 
of sustainable operation, we continuously improve in food safety and customer service, 
aiming to create a superior and more reassuring dining experience for all customers. 
 Investment in food safety and quality maintenance has always been a top priority for the 
Toplus Global Group. We take the major food safety incidents in the food and beverage 
industry, extensively reported in the news in 2024, as a cautionary lesson. Drawing insights 
from current events, we have discussed and established Toplus Global's crisis management 
and preventive measures.
