About this Report 
Thank you very much for taking the time to read the 2024 Sustainability Report of Xintec Inc. (hereinafter referred to as the “Company,” “Xintec,” or “we”). This report marks the 11th 
consecutive year that the Company has published its sustainability performance, further demonstrating Xintec’s determination and concrete achievements in continuously dedicating resources 
to the three aspects of environmental protection, social responsibility, and corporate governance (ESG). Only by moving forward steadily on the path of corporate sustainability can we create 
long-term value together with employees, customers, shareholders, and the public, while firmly believing that the growth of a company should go hand in hand with social progress and 
environmental sustainability. 
Reporting Period and Publication Cycle 
This report is an annual report that primarily discloses Xintec’s performance and actions in various aspects of sustainable development in 2024 (from January 1, 2024 to December 31, 
2024). To comply with the reporting principles of the GRI Sustainability Reporting Standards (GRI Standards), some key performance information has also been traced back to 2023. 
 
The previous year's (2023) report was published in August 2024. 
 
This year's (2024) report has been published in August 2025. 
 
Next year's (2025) report is scheduled for publication in August 2026.

Report Quality and Assurance 
The preparation of this report follows the latest standards issued by the GRI Standards for its framework design and content disclosure. In addition, it also complies with the sustainability information 
disclosure requirements of the Taiwan Stock Exchange and the Taipei Exchange. Compared with the reporting period of the previous year’s report, there were no significant changes in the Company’s

report. To enhance the comparability and reliability of the report’s performance information, external assurance was conducted by the British Standards Institution (BSI) in accordance with Type 1 of the 
moderate assurance level of AA 1000AS V3 (2018). The independent assurance statement issued by BSI can be found in the appendix of this report.

Word from the Chairman 
In 2024, businesses faced global inflationary pressures and a complex and volatile supply chain environment, making it difficult to contain rising operating costs. Amid these numerous 
challenges, Xintec actively responded by incorporating ESG principles into its business strategies to enhance the Company’s resilience and competitiveness, thereby creating long-term value 
for all stakeholders. 
In terms of environmental protection, Xintec achieved its annual “123 Project” as scheduled, meeting the environmental management targets of reducing energy consumption and carbon 
emissions by 1%, reducing waste by 2%, and reducing water usage by 3%. For its renewable energy utilization plan, the Company will purchase an additional 1 million kWh this year, bringing 
the total to about 3 million kWh annually. Xintec will continue investing resources in energy conservation and waste reduction to move toward its environmental sustainability goals. 
Fulfilling social responsibility is also Xintec’s commitment to its shareholders and society. The Company has not only passed the audit certification of the Responsible Business Alliance 
(RBA) and occupational safety and health certifications but also adheres to international standards such as the International Labour Organization (ILO) and the United Nations Guiding 
Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs). Xintec is committed to building a workplace that is respectful of human rights, diverse, equal, inclusive, safe, and environmentally friendly, 
striving to create a friendly and fulfilling work environment. We provide competitive remuneration and benefits, comprehensive training and development opportunities, and care for 
employees’ physical and mental well-being, helping them achieve a balance between career development and quality of life. We understand that employees with a spirit of innovation and a 
sense of responsibility are valuable assets to the Company. By creating a happy work environment that attracts and retains outstanding talent, we can continuously inject momentum toward a 
sustainable future. 
The Company also frequently holds charity sales, organizes material and fund donations to support disadvantaged groups, and has made participating in public welfare a regular part of 
employees’ daily lives, with related contributions exceeding NT$1.2 million last year. 
In terms of strengthening corporate governance, the Company has been honored 7 times with a ranking in the top 5% in the “Corporate Governance Evaluation” by the Taipei Exchange. 
Going forward, the Company will continue to strengthen the functions of the Board of Directors by establishing rigorous corporate governance guidelines, implementing ethical standards such 
as preventing conflicts of interest and fulfilling confidentiality obligations, to lay a solid foundation for sustainable business operations. 
Upholding the concept of sustainable development, Xintec will take more proactive actions to respond to stakeholders’ concerns, focusing on important issues such as climate change, 
human rights, and labor rights, and incorporating them into the Company’s long-term development strategies. At the same time, we are also striving to create a better society and environment, 
hoping to work together with stakeholders such as suppliers, customers, investors, and communities to build a sustainable future.

1. Corporate Sustainable Operation 
1.1 Sustainable Development Vision and Mission 
Amid the global wave of sustainable development, and upholding the commitment to environmental, social, and corporate governance (ESG), the Company has set “sound and 
sustainable governance management,” “green and sustainable operation management,” and “creating a sustainable and fulfilling workplace” as its three core pillars to achieve comprehensive 
sustainability goals. The Company firmly believes that by actively integrating ESG principles into its business strategies, it can not only enhance corporate resilience and competitiveness but 
also create long-term value for stakeholders and contribute to global sustainable development. 
The Company firmly believes that through continuous advancement in technological R&D and innovation, and by providing diversified products and services, it can effectively respond 
to rapidly changing social risks and challenges, promote positive interaction and communication between business operations and the social environment, and work together with partners to 
drive the sustainable development of the industry. 
 
Environment 
In the face of increasingly severe climate change challenges, the Company is actively taking concrete actions to transition toward a green and low-carbon production and operation model. 
We are committed to gradually increasing the proportion of renewable energy used each year and have purchased approximately 1.9 million kWh of renewable energy in 2024. At the same 
time, by implementing ISO 50001: 2018 energy management system certification and conducting GHG inventories and verifications, we have formulated and executed proactive carbon 
reduction plans, demonstrating our determination and contribution to mitigating climate change. 
Driven by innovation, the Company strives for more efficient green production methods and actively responds to society’s concerns about climate change and global warming. We 
commit to conducting annual GHG inventories and setting specific reduction targets. At the same time, we actively follow the recommendations of the Task Force on Climate-related Financial 
Disclosures (TCFD) and build a comprehensive climate risk management culture in line with its framework, taking concrete actions to fulfill the goals of the Paris Agreement. 
 
Social 
As a member of the global community, the Company actively responds to and supports the social responsibility standards of the Responsible Business Alliance (RBA) and complies with 
international standards such as the International Labour Organization (ILO) and the United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs). Through implementing RBA 
verification, we have established rigorous social codes of conduct to protect employees’ rights and interests, emphasize workplace safety and health, and strive to create a friendly and fulfilling 
working environment to attract and retain outstanding talent. 
 
Governance 
Sound corporate governance is the cornerstone of sustainable business development. We continue to strengthen the operational effectiveness of the board of directors and the ESG 
Guiding/Executive Committee, implement the Ethical Corporate Management Best Practice Principles, and consistently focus on social sustainability issues by integrating them into the 
development of innovative and high value-added products and services. By enhancing the Company’s overall operational performance, we create a positive cycle of corporate sustainable 
development. We are proud that since the Company’s listing on the TPEx, it has ranked in the top 5% in the “Corporate Governance Evaluation” organized by TPEx for several years, fully 
demonstrating its outstanding achievements in sustainability and corporate governance. 
 
Operational Resilience and Future Outlook 
In the face of a rapidly changing market environment and the growing emphasis on corporate sustainability, enhancing operational resilience has become a key priority for the Company. 
With an innovative mindset and diverse strategies, we actively respond to risks and challenges from environmental, social, and economic aspects. By strengthening ESG core governance 
principles, formulating concrete sustainability strategies and targets, and incorporating them into each department’s business considerations, we continuously promote short-, mid-, and 
long-term sustainability roadmaps, deepening the sustainability mindset and steadily advancing toward sustainable development. 
Looking ahead, the management team will continue to face challenges with prudence and remain committed to investing the talent and capital needed for key technologies and business 
development, in order to achieve the Company’s sustained growth and create greater value for all stakeholders.

1.2 Organizational Structure of ESG Guiding/Executive Committee 
A sound governance structure is the cornerstone of driving sustainable development. To effectively integrate sustainability concepts into business operations and strengthen the execution 
and supervision of related work, the Company has established a two-tier sustainability governance platform under the supervision and guidance of the Board of Directors to ensure the effective 
implementation of sustainability strategies and the creation of stakeholder value. 
 
Two-tier ESG Guiding/Executive Committee structure 
To promote sustainability efforts in a more systematic manner and enhance their level and effectiveness, the Company established the “ESG Guiding Committee” with the approval of the 
Board of Directors in November 2023. At the same time, the original “ESG Committee” was officially renamed the “ESG Execution Committee,” forming a top-down two-tier governance 
structure. 
1. ESG Guiding Committee 
 
Basis of Establishment: Established with the approval of the Board of Directors (November 2023). 
 
Organization Members: Composed of three directors, including two independent directors, with the Chairman of the Board serving as the chairperson. 
 
Operating Mechanism: Holds annual sustainability topic review meetings to discuss various issues, including but not limited to the Company's sustainable governance policy goals, 
the sustainable development measures and performance of each department, supply chain ESG management, and various ESG-related budgets and performance management. 
These initiatives aim to promote the Company's core ESG annual plans and monitor the implementation results t o establish a mindset and culture of sustainability within the 
Company. Additionally, it reports to the Board of Directors on a regular basis, which will help realize the Board's commitment to sustainable development, and discloses externally 
the Company's achievements in corporate sustainability. We aim to foster the balance among economy, environment, and society, as well as to achieve corporate sustainable 
development. 
 
Main responsibilities: Based on the Company’s “ESG Policy,” (1) formulate the Company’s ESG vision and strategies, and (2) oversee the implementation status of the ESG 
Executive Committee. 
2. ESG Executive Committee 
 
Formally known as: The “Corporate Social Responsibility Committee,” established in 2015, was later renamed the “ESG Committee” and ultimately renamed the “ESG Executive 
Committee” in 2023. 
 
Organization Members: Chaired by a senior manager authorized by the Chairman of the Board. The committee, based on professional division of responsibilities and sustainability 
topic management, has three subcommittees: “Corporate Governance,” “Environmental Protection,” and “Social Engagement.” Members are part-time representatives appointed 
from various internal departments of the Company, covering departments such as human resources, environmental safety and health, business, R&D, legal, plant operations, 
procurement, and quality and reliability. 
 
Operating Mechanism: Meetings are held quarterly, during which representatives from each department maintain ongoing communication with stakeholders to collect ESG-related 
issues of concern to them, promote topics related to corporate governance, environmental management, and social responsibility within sustainable operation management, and 
formulate short-, mid-, and long-term ESG goals and implementation plans. Additionally, the committee prepares the Company's annual ESG report, reports to both the Board and 
the ESG Guiding Committee on the annual ESG plan and its implementation results, and provides updates on progress of coordination among various departments to manage 
related stakeholder issues. 
 
Main Responsibilities: Identify material sustainability issues, develop corresponding action plans, facilitate cross-department communication and resource integration and 
coordination, allocate and manage ESG-related budgets for each unit, and implement tasks in areas such as corporate governance, environmental sustainability, social responsibility, 
and enhanced information disclosure in accordance with the instructions of the ESG Guiding Committee.

Operating and Reporting Mechanism 
In our pursuit of promoting sustainable development, and under the oversight and guidance of the Board of Directors, we established the two major platforms: "ESG Guiding Committee" 
and "ESG Executive Committee," aiming to create value for stakeholders with the sustainable development mindset. The chairperson of the ESG Executive Committee reports annually to the 
ESG Guiding Committee and the Board of Directors on the results of ESG implementation, issues of stakeholders' concern, material topics identified for the year, and future work plans. We 
listen to directors' feedback and suggestions to facilitate the pursuit of sustainable management and profits while realizing our goal of sustainable corporate development. 
Through the operation of this two-tier committee structure, the Company ensures that its sustainability strategies are formulated and implemented with the attention and support of top 
management, while effectively integrating the expertise and resources of various departments to jointly drive the Company’s sustainable development. 
In recent years, Xintec has reported the following ESG implementation results to the Board of Directors: conserving electricity, reducing waste, reducing carbon emissions, passing the 
ISO 50001: 2018 energy management system certification, reducing water consumption, organizing public welfare activities, building a working environment that respects human rights, 
diversity, and equality, achieving zero-accident work hours, green procurement, and supply chain management. These concrete achievements demonstrate the Company's efforts and 
determination on the road to sustainable development.

1.3 Stakeholder Identification and Communication 
 
Determining Key Stakeholders 
Establishing good and diverse communication channels with stakeholders is the cornerstone of fulfilling social responsibility and pursuing sustainable development. With an open 
attitude, we place great importance on interaction and collaboration with various stakeholders, actively communicating and providing sincere responses through deeply listening to and 
understanding their needs and expectations. 
The Company conducts a stakeholder assessment survey once a year, carried out by the “ESG Executive Committee Task Force” composed of contact representatives from various 
internal departments. Each task force member lists potential stakeholders based on the groups or organizations they frequently interact with or have influence over, including employees, 
investors, customers, suppliers/contractors, community residents, government agencies, non-profit organizations, the industry sector, partners, and the media. 
Based on the AA 1000 Stakeholder Engagement Standard (SES 2015), the Company then evaluates the closeness of its relationship with each stakeholder according to criteria such as 
responsibility, influence, dependency, diverse perspectives, and tension. Through ESG Executive Committee meetings and feedback and discussion from all employees, the stakeholder 
identification and assessment results are ultimately determined.  
2024 Significant Stakeholders 
Through the above identification and assessment process, and after discussions between the ESG Executive Committee project secretary and the three subcommittee leaders, the 
Company has identified seven categories of stakeholders as the most influential and relevant to its operations in 2024. The stakeholder engagement results, including their identities, issues of 
concern, communication channels, and key points and performance, are reported to the ESG Guiding Committee and the Board of Directors.

1.5 Process of Material Topic Identification 
Xintec has revised its issue identification method based on the GRI Standards and the AA 1000 Accountability Principles, emphasizing inclusiveness, materiality, responsiveness, and 
impact. In the identification process, in addition to considering stakeholders’ concerns, the level of impact of each issue is also regarded as a key factor in assessing materiality. Through the 
double materiality principle, Xintec identifies material issues that have significant impacts on both internal and external economic, environmental, and social aspects. In conducting materiality 
assessments, in addition to regular stakeholder interactions, questionnaires and interviews were also carried out, particularly with key partners and sustainability experts, to gain deeper insights 
into their impacts on and expectations of the Company. The Company also commits to openly and transparently reporting both positive and negative information to help relevant parties make 
better decisions. Xintec will continue to update its materiality issue identification process, regularly assess the impacts of issues, and effectively allocate resources to develop short- to 
long-term strategies aligned with sustainable development.

1.8 Integration and Implementation of the UN SDGs by Xintec 
Xintec firmly believes that as a member of the global business community, sustainable development should be rooted in its core values and integrated with the SDGs, using the SDGs as a 
guide and incorporating them into the Company’s operational strategies and decision-making processes to achieve positive impacts on the economy, environment, and society. 
 
Three-stage SDGs Relevance Identification Process 
 
Identification and Connection: Identify the SDGs most relevant to the Company’s operations and connect them to the business activities of each department. 
 
Assessment and Prioritization: Assess the importance and impact of each SDG on the Company’s operations through in-depth conversation with stakeholders and data 
analysis, and prioritize them accordingly. 
 
Integration and Implementation: Formulate concrete action plans and establish measurable performance indicators to ensure the achievement of the goals. 
 
Correspondence Between Relevant SDGs and the Company’s Material Issues 
Map the 12 identified material issues to the following SDGs, and detail in the report the SDG targets corresponding to each issue, the related indicators, and the concrete action 
plans: 
 
SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being 
Implement legal and regulatory requirements, foster a workplace safety culture and develop diverse health care mechanisms to build a comprehensive occupational safety 
environment. 
 
SDG 4: Quality Education 
Design training blueprints based on job functions, covering technical, management, and sustainable development courses, and encourage employees to pursue continuous 
learning and self-growth. 
 
SDG 5: Gender Equality 
Enhance women’s participation and leadership in the workplace. The Company has established an action plan to promote gender equality and ensures equal pay and gender 
diversity in the workplace. 
 
SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation 
Safe Water and Sanitation and Its Sustainable Management 
 
SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy 
Actively invest in renewable energy technologies, strive to enhance the Company’s energy efficiency, and promote a low-carbon economy. Conduct annual reviews of energy 
consumption and seek additional sustainable energy solutions. 
 
SDG 8: Employment and Economic Growth 
Support the promotion of sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, and achieve full and productive employment. Formulate employee training programs and 
improve employee benefits and working conditions. 
 
SDG 9: Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure 
The Company not only focuses on business innovation but also actively participates in infrastructure upgrades and development, supports smart manufacturing and digital 
transformation, promotes industrial upgrading and technological innovation, and ensures sustainable development. 
 
SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production 
Incorporate responsible raw material sourcing into the Company’s operational processes and promote a circular economy model to ensure efficient resource utilization and 
waste minimization in the Company’s production processes. 
 
SDG 13: Climate Action 
Reduce the Company’s carbon footprint during operations and actively address climate change. Set carbon reduction targets and implement measures such as reducing energy 
consumption and improving energy efficiency to contribute to achieving global climate goals. 
 
SDG 16: Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions 
Xintec is committed to promoting fair and transparent business operations, strengthening its internal governance structure, adhering to ethical standards, and building a 
responsible, transparent, and trustworthy corporate image. 
 
SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals 
Actively seek to establish partnerships with various organizations and stakeholders, especially in promoting sustainable development and sharing resources, and collaborate 
with the government, NGOs, other companies, and academia to jointly advance the achievement of sustainable goals.

Xintec Inc., established in 1998, was the first company to commercialize Wafer Level Chip Scale Packaging (WLCSP) technology. Since itsinception, the company has played a pivotal 
role in the WLCSP and optoelectronic packaging supply chain, upholding integrity, innovation, and customer orientation as its core values, and positioning itself as a leading provider of 
advanced semiconductor packaging solutions. 

 
27 
 
Xintec originated in CMOS Image Sensor (CIS) packaging and has since expanded its capabilities for a wide range of sensor components, MEMS, and customized wafer-level 
architectures serving the consumer electronics, communications, computing, industrial, and automotivesectors. The Company’s competitive strengths lie in the ongoing develop mentof 
advanced 3D wafer-level packaging technologies, deep collaboration with customers, and the integration of capabilities across the upstream supply chain, enabling high- value packaging 
solutions that accelerate time-to market and create share value.. 
 
 
Technological Leadership and Sustainable Vision 
In process integration and technology development, Xintec invests a high proportion of resources each year to continuously enhance its key technologies, including photoresist coating, 
exposure and development, etching, wafer bonding, physical and chemical vapor deposition, metal interconnect processes (such as aluminum and copper), deep via electroplating (Cu-TSV), 
thinning of special materials, and high-precision dicing, establishing a comprehensive packaging service chain spanning from front-end to back-end processes. In addition, Xintec is actively 
developing a 12-inch wafer packaging platform and advancing technologies such as fine-line width interconnects, multilayer metal routing, heterogeneous integration packaging, and gallium 
nitride (GaN) chip packaging to support the mass production needs of emerging applications such as automotive sensors, medical devices, and AR/VR modules. 
Looking ahead, Xintec will continue to strengthen its long-term partnerships with international semiconductor companies, system integrators, and foundry partners, providing 
customer-centric, customized, differentiated, and high value-added wafer-level packaging services. 
While driving technological innovation, Xintec also actively responds to the UN SDGs by integrating ESG core concepts into its operations and development strategies. The Company is 
committed to improving energy efficiency, optimizing resource utilization, and strengthening green manufacturing and supply chain responsibility management to create a sustainable value 
chain that benefits the economy, environment, and society. 
With its continuously innovative R&D capabilities and professional packaging technologies, Xintec will uphold its corporate mission of “driving value through technology and fulfilling 
responsibility through sustainability,” continuously leading the global wafer-level packaging industry toward an efficient, intelligent, and green future.

2.2 Objectives and Strategies of Sustainable Management  
 
Operational Performance Review 
In 2024, despite the challenges of global high inflationary pressures and rising operating costs, Xintec still delivered results that exceeded market expectations through its solid 
operational strategies and technological advantages. The annual consolidated revenue reached NT$7.06 billion, representing a 10.5% increase compared to 2023; net income after tax reached 
NT$1.67 billion, with an annual growth rate of 21.4%; earnings per share were NT$6.15, demonstrating the Company’s ability to maintain solid profitability despite a highly volatile external 
environment. 
The main revenue drivers came from image sensor packaging and 12-inch wafer testing services, both achieving double-digit growth, demonstrating the Company’s technological 
capabilities and the benefits of capacity optimization. Processing services for 3D sensing components also continued to expand, with revenue surpassing that of the previous year, effectively 
contributing to overall profitability. 
 
Market Environment Analysis 
In 2024, driven by the rapid growth of AI applications and the gradual recovery of the global economy, the semiconductor market experienced a noticeable rebound. According to 
forecasts by the World Semiconductor Trade Statistics (WSTS), the total output value of the global semiconductor market will reach USD 611.2 billion in 2024, representing an annual growth 
rate of 16.0%; it is expected to further increase to USD 687.4 billion in 2025, with an annual growth rate of 12.5%, reflecting strong and continuously expanding market demand. 
Taiwan’s semiconductor industry has also achieved remarkable results, with its total output value expected to exceed NT$5 trillion in 2024, representing an annual growth rate of 22%, 
outperforming the global average. Easing global inflationary pressures, recovering end-market demand, and the continued growth of emerging applications such as AI, IoT, and smart 
healthcare are driving the demand for sensors and packaging services, creating favorable conditions for the Company’s operations. 
 
Future Outlook and Strategic Direction 
Looking ahead to 2025, although the overall semiconductor market remains conservative toward non-AI-related sectors, Xintec will continue to focus on technology upgrades and 
product diversification strategies to enhance its market competitiveness and value proposition. 
The Company will prioritize investment in the following strategic directions: 
 
Advanced Packaging Service Enhancement: Strengthen the differentiated advantages of wafer-level CSP to meet the mass production demand for automotive CMOS image sensors 
(CIS) and provide stable and high-quality packaging solutions. 
 
Customized Solution Advancement: Support customers in new product introduction by developing optical sensor packaging and processing programs for applications in medical, 
AR/VR, and other fields. 
 
Mid- to Long-Term R&D Deployment: Accelerate the establishment of System in Package (SiP) packaging technology platforms for sensors and expand capital expenditures on key 
equipment to build future growth momentum in advance. 
 
Sustainability Value Enhancement: Integrate ESG governance with environmental goals, and while pursuing technological innovation, simultaneously promote green processes and 
low-carbon operations, with corporate sustainability as the core driving force. 
In the face of intense competition and market transformation, Xintec will adhere to a dual strategy of solid operations and innovative R&D, working alongside customers and supply 
chain partners to continuously strengthen its competitive advantages and industry influence, moving toward a future with greater growth potential and sustainable value.

Xintec upholds the business philosophy and values of integrity, innovation, and customer orientation, striving to become a leading enterprise in the field of wafer-level chip packaging,

The Company’s highest governance unit is headed by the Chairman, who also serves as the President, centralizing leadership authority and responsibility to ensure consistency between

protection of stakeholders' interests. The Board includes independent directors with diverse core competencies and backgrounds, and the nomination process adheres to the principles of 
fairness, justice, and transparency.

Conflict of Interest Avoidance of the Board  
To implement the principles of ethical corporate management and strengthen corporate governance, Xintec has established a comprehensive conflict of interest avoidance system for the 
Board of Directors to ensure the fairness and transparency of decision-making processes while safeguarding the best interests of the Company, shareholders, and stakeholders. The relevant 
regulations and practical operations are as follows: 
I. Systems, Regulations, and Procedures 
 
Codification of Recusal Regulations: 
According to Article 14 of the “Rules of Procedure for Board Meetings”, if a director has a personal interest or represents a corporate shareholder that has an interest in a proposal under 
discussion, the director shall explain the material aspects of the interest at the current Board meeting. If the interest is likely to be detrimental to the Company’s interests, the director shall not 
participate in the discussion or voting on the proposal, and shall recuse himself or herself during discussion and voting, and may not exercise voting rights on behalf of other directors. 
 
Information Disclosure and Transparent Recordkeeping: 
According to Article 15 of the “Rules of Procedure for Board Meetings”, for any proposal involving a director’s conflict of interest, the meeting minutes shall fully record the director’s name, 
explanation of the conflict of interest, reason for recusal, and the actual recusal situation. These records are strictly executed by the Board Secretary in accordance with procedures to ensure the 
traceability and transparency of the decision-making process. 
 
Balancing Functions of Independent Directors: 
To enhance the Board’s supervisory function, Article 6 of the “Rules of Procedure for Board Meetings” stipulates that if an independent director has an objection or reservation, it must be 
specified in the meeting minutes. In addition, all directors may seek assistance from the Chief Corporate Governance Officer regarding laws, regulations, and meeting procedures to ensure the 
effective performance of their duties and strengthen communication between the Board of Directors and the management team. 
II. Practical Operating Mechanisms 
When the Board of Directors deliberates on proposals involving conflicts of interest, the Chairman of the Board will remind the relevant directors in advance to recuse themselves. If 
necessary, a director without any conflict of interest will act as the acting chairman to preside over the discussion of the proposal. 
All cases involving conflict-of-interest recusals are recorded in detail by the Board Secretary in accordance with regulations and included in the meeting minutes for internal review and 
external disclosure reference. 
The Company has also established the “Code of Ethical Conduct for Directors and Managers” and the “Ethical Corporate Management Best Practice Principles” to clearly define the codes of 
conduct and conflict of interest management principles for directors and managers, thereby strengthening the Company’s overall culture of integrity and governance.

3.2 Tax Management  
The Company pledges to implement a rigorous tax governance strategy and adopt conservative and prudent tax management policies to support sustainable development. Each operating







7.3 Climate Change Management 
Actively respond to climate change and move toward a sustainable future. 
Xintec deeply recognizes that climate change is an urgent global issue and that the environmental risks it generates pose significant challenges to the sustainability of business operations. 
Considering the characteristics of the Company’s industry, energy consumption during the production process inevitably generates GHG emissions, exacerbating global warming. Active 
measures must be taken to address the risks and opportunities brought by climate change, with the expectation of aligning with the global goal of limiting temperature rise to within 1.5°C. 
In response to the implementation of Taiwan’s Climate Change Response Act, which officially began collecting carbon fees in 2025, the development and purchase of renewable energy 
have become urgent. In 2024, Xintec began purchasing 1,900,000 kWh of renewable energy (1,900 certificates) and will continue to pay close attention to renewable energy issues in the 
future. 
In addition, in recent years the factories have actively promoted green processes and various environmental protection, energy-saving, and carbon-reduction measures, including evaluating 
renewable energy purchases, increasing the installation rate of exhaust gas reduction equipment, replacing energy-saving equipment year by year, and establishing plant energy-saving 
regulations. These efforts foster employees’ awareness of energy conservation and carbon reduction, with the aim of reducing total GHG emissions to mitigate and adapt to the operational 
impacts of climate change. 
 
7.3.1 Climate Governance Framework and Responsibilities 
Since 2020, Xintec has adopted the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) framework to systematically assess the potential impacts of climate change on its 
operations and formulate corresponding strategies accordingly. Under the leadership of the Risk Management Committee, composed of senior executives and representatives from various 
departments, the Company follows TCFD recommendations to comprehensively identify and assess the risks, opportunities, and financial impacts that climate change may pose to its 
operations, in order to formulate effective risk management strategies and climate action plans. 
The Board of Directors and senior managers jointly shoulder the important responsibility of climate governance, formulating and implementing short-, mid-, and long-term goals to 
address climate change risks and opportunities, reporting on execution results, and ensuring the achievement of climate governance objectives. Through the above efforts, including board 
oversight, strategy adjustments, strengthened risk management, and the establishment of clear indicators and targets, Xintec has fully integrated climate change issues into its decision-making 
and operations, enhancing its climate resilience and actively seizing opportunities for low-carbon transition. At the same time, the Company also values information transparency by 
proactively disclosing climate-related information and undergoing external reviews, demonstrating its determination to promote climate action and thereby establishing an advantage in 
sustainable operations.

7.5 Waste Management 
Waste is primarily generated during the wafer manufacturing process, and all waste is treated off-site. After proper treatment, environmental impacts are reduced. Xintec regards waste 
management as a core element of sustainable development and continuously optimizes management processes through systematic source reduction, resource recycling, and compliant disposal 
to minimize environmental impacts and enhance the benefits of the circular economy. 
I. Management Framework and Core Measures 
1. Full-process management system 
 
Establish standard operating procedures (SOP) covering waste identification, classification, collection, storage, transportation, and disposal to ensure compliance and traceability. 
 
Strictly select waste disposal contractors, specify regulatory compliance requirements in contracts, and conduct regular audits of their treatment performance and documentation 
integrity. 
2. Three-tier management strategy 
 
Source reduction: Optimize production processes and material management to reduce waste generation (e.g., improve chemical utilization efficiency). 
 
Resource reuse: In 2024, a total of 711.797 tons of waste was recycled and reused. Through waste segregation and collaboration with contractors, resource value was maximized. 
 
Safe disposal: For non-recyclable waste, ensure harmless treatment in accordance with environmental regulations and track the final destination until proper treatment certification 
documents are obtained. 
3. Regulatory compliance and supervision mechanism, implemented in accordance with the Ministry of Environment “Regulations Governing Determination of Reasonable Due Care 
Obligations of Enterprises Commissioning Waste Clearance,” with the following measures: 
 
In-plant control: Conduct quarterly inspections of waste storage and treatment processes, record deficiencies, and track improvements. 
 
Contractor audit: Conduct on-site audits of waste disposal contractors annually to ensure compliance with regulations and contractual requirements. 
 
Document retention: All inspection records and treatment certificates are signed and approved by responsible personnel and retained for at least five years. 
II. Creating a Circular Economy and Strengthening Recycling and Reuse Technologies 
Xintec puts the concept of “Turning Waste Into Resources” into practice through specific actions 
In the face of limited resources and increasingly severe environmental impacts, Xintec actively promotes the strategies of “circular economy” and “waste resource utilization,” 
implementing the principles of waste reduction, reuse, and localized treatment to build a low-carbon, circular, and sustainable supply chain. 
 
Increase the reuse rate and transform resource potential from the source. 
In the past, most organic waste liquids had to be treated through incineration, which effectively reduced volume but resulted in high energy consumption and significant carbon emissions. 
Since 2023, Xintec has actively adjusted its treatment strategy by changing part of the treatment of organic waste liquids from incineration to resource recovery. This not only effectively 
reduces waste generation but also achieves the goal of “Turning Waste Into Resources.”

7.6 Water Resource Management 
Water resource management and risk response - From conservation to reuse, advancing toward a circular sustainable blueprint 
In the face of increasingly frequent extreme weather events caused by global climate change, Taiwan, as an island nation with unevenly distributed water resources, is facing the growing 
challenge of alternating droughts and floods. As a responsible enterprise, Xintec recognizes that water resources are the lifeline of operations and an essential cornerstone of environmental 
sustainability. To mitigate water risks and reduce environmental impacts, the Company continuously optimizes its water resource management strategies, ranging from conservation, recycling, 
and water quality control to the development of backup water sources, thereby establishing a resilient and efficient water resource management system. 
Multi-pronged water conservation strategies: conservation, recycling, and reuse in parallel

and to implement three major aspects of water resource management strategies:

Facilities (Reclaim + Reduction)

Enhance the wastewater recovery rate in facility systems for recycling and reuse. 
 
Evaluate the introduction of reclaimed water and groundwater to reduce reliance on tap water. 
 
Process (Reduction) 


 
Reduce idle water consumption and optimize parameter settings to minimize unnecessary water usage. 
 
Equipment (Reclaim) 
 
Analyze the wastewater quality of equipment and introduce reuse technologies. 
 
Establish recycled water quality standards to enhance water circulation efficiency at the equipment level.

7.7 Hazardous Substance Management 
Promote green manufacturing and fulfill environmental responsibility 
To reduce the impact of processes and products on the environment and ecosystems, and to respond to international environmental regulations and stakeholders’ expectations for 
corporate sustainability responsibility, Xintec continues to strengthen its hazardous substance management system. Each year, based on the latest international regulations, customer 
requirements, and industry trends, the Company regularly reviews and updates the “Green Product Hazardous Substance Management Standards” to ensure the comprehensive implementation 
of environmentally friendly principles in product development, manufacturing, and supply chain management. 
At the stage of raw material and component procurement, Xintec requires suppliers to provide an “Environmental Controlled Substance Guarantee” to ensure that the purchased materials 
comply with international environmental regulations such as EU RoHS and REACH, and imposes strict controls on substances including lead (Pb), cadmium (Cd), mercury (Hg), hexavalent 
chromium (Cr⁶⁺), polybrominated biphenyls (PBB), polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDE), and other substances of very high concern.   
In addition, the Company has established a comprehensive supplier management system that clearly stipulates that the raw materials, components, and packaging materials provided must 
comply with the Company’s procurement guidelines and customer requirements. By implementing source control, the Company effectively prevents pollution and hazards, working together 
with suppliers to build a green supply chain, protect soil, air, and water resources from hazardous substance contamination, and fulfill its commitment to safeguarding the global environment.

8.5 Parental Benefits for Employees’ Peace of Mind in Employment 
Xintec values gender equality and creates a working environment that allows employees to work with peace of mind while balancing work and family. To encourage childbirth and 
ensure that employees have sufficient time to rest, accompany, and care for their families before and after childbirth, the Company provides parental and caregiving benefits that exceed local 
regulatory requirements, in addition to complying with parental leave laws in each region. This fosters a warm and harmonious work environment that enables employees to balance career 
development and family care. 
 
Parental leave and related leaves 
In accordance with the “Gender Equality in Employment Act” and the “Regulations for Implementing Unpaid Parental Leave for Raising Children”, the Company provides an unpaid 
parental leave policy that allows employees to apply before their children reach the age of 3. In addition, the Company also provides family care leave, paternity leave, and other related leave 
policies to support employees’ family caregiving responsibilities. To protect the rights of female employees, the Company also provides menstrual leave and pregnancy health and maternity 
leave. 
 
Breastfeeding time 
The Company provides breastfeeding time for employees who need to breastfeed children under the age of two. In addition to the required rest periods, employees are granted daily 
breastfeeding (or lactation) time, which is regarded as working hours. Regardless of the breastfeeding time or location, employees are still in compliance with clock-in and clock-out attendance 
requirements. 
 
Reinstatement arrangements 
When employees apply for reinstatement after the completion of their unpaid parental leave, the Company will give priority to arranging their return to their original department and 
position to the greatest extent possible to meet employees’ needs.    
 
Flexible working hour arrangements 
Considering that employees may need to adjust their working hours due to childcare or other legitimate reasons, the Company allows employees to apply for schedule adjustments. Upon 
approval, working hours may be adjusted provided that weekly working hours are met, core working hours are observed, and normal business communication is not affected.

employee health. 
For employees with abnormal health examination results, Xintec adopts a tiered health management system, with a professional medical team providing continuous follow-up, assistance 
with return visits, and health education. In addition, for high-pressure work patterns such as night shifts and rotating shifts, the Company has introduced an “abnormal workload prevention 
mechanism” to detect potential health risks at an early stage. 
In addition, the Company has designated nurses who regularly provide consultations for middle-aged and senior employees, pregnant employees, and shift workers, as well as 
psychological counseling services to care for employees’ physical and mental well-being. At the same time, the Company also encourages contractors (such as cleaning and security personnel) 
to participate in health promotion activities, practicing zero discrimination in workplace health.

The development of enterprises is closely connected with social well-being. The Company takes its mission as a responsible corporate citizen, giving back to society through diversified

 
Blood drives:

blood to support medical needs through concrete action.

community.

Children and Families.
