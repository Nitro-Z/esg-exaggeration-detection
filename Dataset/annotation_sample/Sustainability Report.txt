Compilation Guidelines 
The preparation follows the framework provided by the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) 
Universal Standards 2021, which was published in 2021. These serve as the primary disclosure 
framework. Additionally, we have utilized the Task Force on Climate-related Financial 
Disclosures (TCFD) framework and the Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB) 
Sustainability Accounting Standards for compilation.
Report Management
The data disclosed in this report are derived from the Company's internal statistics and 
survey results. The financial data are based on the audited financial statements, certified by 
EY Taiwan, and presented in New Taiwan Dollars (NTD). Additionally, environmental, health, 
and safety performance metrics are reported using internationally recognized standards. 
Certain data have been sourced from publicly available information on government websites 
and are presented using commonly accepted numerical descriptions. Any estimations made 
are clearly indicated in the relevant sections of the report. The Company has entrusted KPMG 
Taiwan to conduct limited assurance on specific performance indicators in accordance with 
the “Regulations Governing the Preparation and Filing of Sustainability Reports by TWSE-
Listed Companies” issued by the Taiwan Stock Exchange and GRI Sustainability Reporting 
Standards (GRI Standards) issued by the Global Sustainability Standards Board (GSSB). The 
assurance conclusions can be found in the appendix of this report.
The Company has established ''Sustainable Information Management Measures'' and 
integrated them into our Internal Control System. Although the content of this report has not 
undergone external assurance, it has been prepared and reviewed by various departments of 
the Company. After consolidation by the Corporate Governance Department, it was submitted 
to the Sustainability Development Committee and finalized for publication following approval 
by the Board of Directors. During the reporting period, there were no significant changes in 
the Company's operations, including scale, structure, ownership, or supply chain.

1.1 Stakeholders Identification and Communication
The Company identifies its key stakeholders by referencing the GRI guidelines, which 
list potential stakeholders including shareholders, financial institutions, government 
organizations, employees, customers, business partners, suppliers, communities/local groups, 
non-governmental organizations, social welfare organizations, academic and industry-
academic cooperation units, etc. The Company first engaged external experts to identify 
stakeholders in accordance with the updated GRI requirements (covering actual and potential, 
positive influence and negative impacts). Subsequently, department heads assessed the 
Company’s relationship with each stakeholder group based on the five core principles of the 
AA1000SES Stakeholder Engagement Standard—dependency, responsibility, influence, diverse 
perspectives, and tension. A weighted average scoring process was applied, and stakeholder 
groups with a total score above 10, upon approval by Company management, were confirmed 
as the Company’s material stakeholders for 2024. The identified stakeholders for the year 
include: shareholders, government agencies, employees, customers, suppliers/contractors 
(business partners), banks, and local communities, etc.

1.2 Identification of Material Topics
The Company listens and responds to the issues of the stakeholders’ concern in the hope of 
identifying the sustainability issues the stakeholders are concerned about through a systematic 
analysis model as the reference basis for the information disclosed in this Report to facilitate effective 
communication with all our stakeholders. The analysis of the material topics of this Report was 
divided into the following four steps. 
Materiality Analysis Steps
STEP 1
Understanding 
the organizational 
context
To understand the organizational context and identify sustainability issues, Jia Wei Lifestyle refers to various guidelines, including 
the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) Standards and Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB). In total, we have compiled 
38 sustainability topics, covering four major sustainability dimensions: corporate governance, economic, environmental, and 
social aspects.

１
 Sustainability Management
14
Materiality Analysis Steps
STEP 2
Identifying actual 
and potential 
impacts of 
sustainability issues
Our department heads and external experts engage in assessment regarding the actual or potential negative impacts and positive 
influences of these sustainability issues on the economic, environmental, and social (people and human rights) aspects. After 
summing the scores for each topic's actual and potential negative impacts, as well as actual and potential positive impacts across 
economic, environmental, and social (including human rights) dimensions, topics with a total score exceeding 15 points are 
prioritized as key issues for the Company. This prioritization also takes into account the identification results from the previous 
year. However, key topics outlined by the SASB standards and the Financial Supervisory Commission's sustainability disclosure 
indicators are given precedence and prioritized after scoring.
STEP 3
Assessing the 
significance of these 
impacts
Drawing on past operational experience, we assessed the significance and likelihood of each issue's impact after discussions 
between external consultants and the department responsible for compiling the sustainability report. Topics with a significant 
impact on the Company were selected as material topics.
STEP 4
Sustainability Issue 
Exposure Report
Ultimately, the results of the material sustainability issues analysis, along with input from external experts and comprehensive 
evaluations across company departments, upon approval from the Company’s management, have led us to consolidate the 17 
material issues into 9 material topics that we aim to communicate and respond to stakeholders through this sustainability report.

2.1Company Overview
Jia Wei Lifestyle was established on April 21, 2005, with the capital exceeding NT$ 
800 million. On February 27, 2008, Jia Wei Lifestyle was listed on TPEx. The Company was 
originally an excellent manufacturer and supplier of professional optical products. To enhance 
the Company's competitiveness and step toward a new milestone, it successfully switched to 
the housewares industry in 2019.
Jia Wei Lifestyle designs and manufactures top-quality home products with a timeless 
and classic aesthetic. The inspiration for our designs comes from the diverse perspectives 
of international designers, emphasizing simplicity in lines and warmth in form. Our home 
products are practical, aesthetically pleasing, and durable, making them an ideal choice 
for creating a comfortable and beautiful living environment. Our commitment to exquisite 
craftsmanship and attention to detail results in high-quality, design-conscious home products 
that enhance the beauty and warmth of life. We believe in savoring the beauty and warmth 
of life. Experiencing a comfortable and cozy lifestyle with a philosophical approach is also 
valued.
With the transformation of the global economy from industrial economy to service 
economy, knowledge economy, creative economy, and even to experience economy, the 
competitive advantages of industries do not merely include land, capital, or manpower. 
Instead, integration of elements such as knowledge, creativity, culture, and unique features 
makes a design not just a design but an object connecting life and culture. As this connection 
becomes more complex and diverse, it leads to the changes in the meaning of life and value of 
consumption.
In the home products industry, a sector that connects people with economy, and reflects 
cultural lifestyles, we aspire to enhance the experience of home life through visual and tactile 
beauty. We aim to fuse the concepts of ease and elegance into refined and delightful lifestyles. 
Jia Wei Lifestyle is committed to staying attuned to the latest fashion trends and elements, 
ensuring a seamless fusion of products and design. With industry-leading competitiveness, we 
offer end-to-end solutions from design to manufacturing, pursuing perfection with minimal 
time gaps and delivering cutting-edge, distinctive products. We provide exceptional business 
capabilities and technical services, and are always attuned to our customers' needs and 
dedicated to achieving the highest customer satisfaction. Innovation is by no means chance; 
it's our shared commitment to aesthetics. We strive to establish new benchmarks through 
fresh ideas, technology, and a mindset that appreciates the beauty in everything. Jia Wei 
Lifestyle looks forward to a future where our core principles of 'life,' 'design,' 'innovation,' and 
'aesthetics' will continue to convey the meaning of beauty of life.

Nominations and Recusal of Interests
The nomination system of the Company is as follows：
1.	Nomination system of Directors：In accordance with the Company Act and the Company's 
Articles of Incorporation, the election of Directors of the Company adopts a candidate 
nomination system, and the Board of Directors shall be elected from the list of candidates for 
Directors in the shareholders’ meeting.
2.	Nomination of Director candidates：Any shareholder holding 1% or more of the total number 
of outstanding shares issued by the Company may submit to the Company in writing a roster 
of Director candidates, provided that the total number of Director candidates so nominated 
shall not exceed the quota of the Directors to be elected. This restrictive condition shall also 
be applicable to the roster of Director candidates nominated by the Board of Directors of the 
Company.
Recusal of Interests：
1.	The Company has established a clear system for the recusal of Directors’ interests. In addition 
to matters that must be avoided stipulated in relevant laws and regulations, matters the Directors 
shall avoid by themselves are also specified to prioritize the interests of the Company and ensure 
fair dealing so as to avoid opportunities for personal gain.
2.	Self-discipline：Directors should uphold a high degree of self-discipline, and if any Director 
or a juristic person represented by a Director is an interested party with respect to any agenda 
item, the Director shall state the important aspects of the interested party relationship at the 
respective meeting. When the relationship is likely to prejudice the interests of the Company, the 
Director may not participate in discussion or voting on that agenda item, and further, shall enter 
recusal during discussion and voting on that item and may not act as another Director’s proxy 
to exercise voting rights on that matter. Where the spouse or a blood relative within the second 
degree of kinship of a Director, or a company which has a controlling or subordinate relation with 
a Director, is an interested party with respect to an agenda item as described in the preceding 
paragraph, such Director shall be deemed to be an interested party with respect to that agenda 
item.
3. The provisions of Article 180, paragraph 2 of the Company Act, as applied mutatis under Article 
206, paragraph 4 of that Act, apply to resolutions of Board of Directors meetings when a Director 
is prohibited by the preceding two paragraphs from exercising voting rights.
4. The aforementioned matters related to Directors' conflicts of interest are clearly stipulated in the 
Company's "Board Meeting Rules" and "Corporate Governance Practices Guidelines."

We may face actual and potential risks in the process of sustainability and development. Through risk

Supplier Screening Methods
Each department's requisition personnel provide relevant information about potential 
suppliers to the purchasing unit for negotiation or quotation. The purchasing unit then 
investigates the supplier information provided by requisition personnel from each 
department.
After the purchasing unit evaluates and determines that a supplier has the capability to 
provide materials or other products to the Company, they send a "Supplier Basic Information 
Inquiry Form" to the supplier via fax or email. The supplier fills out the relevant basic 
information, and after initial approval by the supervisor, the information is entered into 
the "ERP System" for record maintenance. In cases where a supplier is initially assessed as 
not meeting the qualifications, they may still be registered as a qualified supplier under the 
following circumstances:
1.	 For suppliers in monopolistic or oligopolistic markets, on-site evaluations may be 
conducted based on actual needs.

based on actual needs.

ultimately being transformed into remeltable pellets suitable for reuse, which can save energy

appliqué technology. This not only allows exceptional design aesthetics to be fully realized in 
environmentally- friendly materials that meet market and customer demands but also wins 
recognition from our customers. In 2021~2024, R-PET materials accounted for 9%, 16%, 17%,

5.3 Water Resources
The headquarters in Taiwan is our operating base, in charge of the planning, execution 
and management of the global operations. The source of water comes from Taiwan Water 
Corporation, mainly for domestic water and none for manufacturing, and the water 
withdrawal is relatively low. According to the World Resources Institute’s ‘Aqueduct Water 
Risk Atlas,’ the assessment results indicate a low-medium risk. As for our manufacturing 
sites (TSP and TSM) in Heyuan City, China, their source of water resources is from Heyuan 
Qinchengda Water Resources Co., Ltd., providing the Company with water for hydropower 
generation, manufacturing, and domestic use. According to the World Resources Institute’s 
‘Aqueduct Water Risk Atlas,’ their assessment results indicate a medium-high risk. Based

assessment of water stress conditions across all operational sites shows that 99% are located in 
medium-high water stress areas and 1% in medium-low water stress areas. Currently, no water 
withdrawal occurs in areas classified as having high or extremely high baseline water stress. 
The Company will continue to promote water conservation, improve water consumption in 
production processes, and enhance water resource efficiency.

5.4 Waste
The waste generated from the Company's production processes includes waste mineral oil 
and waste packaging barrels from injection molding machine maintenance, small amounts of 
waste paint residue from the painting process, small amounts of waste organic solvents from 
the printing process, waste activated carbon from the treatment facilities in the painting and 
printing processes, waste rags from equipment cleaning, and waste fluorescent tubes from 
factory lighting. Among these, waste organic solvents, waste activated carbon, waste mineral 
oil, and waste paint residue are the most prevalent.
The Company strictly complies with government laws and regulations to protect the 
living environment and public health. The waste generated during the production process 
is sorted and stored in an independent, qualified, and dedicated warehouse for centralized 
management before handed over to the qualified facilities for waste treatment. 
At the Taiwan operation site, household waste is sorted and then handled either by an 
environmental company commissioned by the building management committee or by a 
recycling plant commissioned by the industrial park self-governing committee. At the China 
manufacturing site, household waste is collected and disposed of by a contracted cleaning 
service company.
No major leaks from the Company occurred in 2024. Furthermore, we will continue to 
properly implement waste management in the future.

•

•	
All measures related to labor-management relations are in accordance with relevant laws. Any 
additions or amendments are made after thorough communication and agreement between 
labor and management, and no disputes have arisen. We legally protect the legitimate rights and 
interests of employees and respect their opinions. Coordination, communication, and expression 
of opinions can be done through meetings, employee suggestion boxes, and other means to 
maintain a good labor-management relationship.

following year.

and

increased, or be reduced if the number is small.

matters.

local labor laws and regulations. We declare that no employees under 16 years of age (or 15

employed in any of the production process, nor are the “child labor” and “young workers

counseling and schooling. Related affairs are handled in accordance with the Work Rules and

number of overtime hours shall not exceed 46 hours a month, and employees shall have 
at least one day off every 7 days. All the overtime work is voluntary. Our company has not 
experienced any incidents of forcing employees to work overtime or beyond their scheduled 
hours in 2024.

Jia Wei Lifestyle is headquartered in central Taipei and has an office in Tainan. Since our 
Tainan office is located outside the city center (Yijia Village, Rende District), we are deeply

communities. In addition to providing employment opportunities to local residents, we also 
hope to extend care to more remote areas and contribute to a shared prosperity.

their development directly affects the prospects of society and the country, Jia Wei Lifestyle 
supports the Ministry of Culture’s “Cultural Vouchers” program. In 2024, we identified 
students in rural schools in Tainan eligible for the program and sponsored an additional 500 
cultural voucher points per student for all 581 students of National Houbi Senior High School. 
Through this initiative, we aim to reduce the urban–rural gap, ensure that students in remote 
areas have access to diverse and enriching cultural experiences, and allow them the freedom 
to choose cultural and arts activities they enjoy. This not only broadens their horizons but also 
strengthens the foundation of cultural vitality. In 2024, Jiawei contributed a total of NT$290,500 
toward this initiative.

6.5 Healthy 
workplace 
In 2024, our Group recorded a total of 23 occupational accidents among our employees, accounting for 0.19% of the total workforce. 
These incidents were primarily attributed to improper operation of production machinery by personnel during the production process, as 
well as heat-related injuries such as heat exhaustion caused by high temperatures while operating equipment in the manufacturing sites 
in China. Following each occurrence, the respective department responsible for the affected personnel promptly completed incident 
reports. These reports were instrumental in evaluating the causes of the workplace injuries and proposing measures for improvement. In 
addition, our Human Resources department continues to focus on enhancing the work environment and reinforcing occupational safety 
through education, training, and practical exercises, aiming to prevent occupational accidents and strengthen workplace safety, health, 
and well-being. Furthermore, no fire occurred and no related casualties. 
To achieve standardized safety management, our manufacturing sites in China have carried out hazard identification and classification 
across all positions, departments, operational activities, and equipment/facilities. Risk-based control measures and hazard remediation 
have been implemented, establishing a “Dual Prevention Mechanism” for safety production risk classification and hazard investigation 
and management. This mechanism aims to prevent accidents at their source and to establish a long-term safety production management 
system for the Company. The implementation steps of the “Dual Prevention Mechanism” are as follows:
