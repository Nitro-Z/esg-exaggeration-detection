Message from the Management
In the face of the severe challenges posed by global climate change and environmental issues, Taiwan cannot stand alone. 
As a leading global plasticizer manufacturer, UPC Technology Corporation bears an undeniable responsibility in the transition 
to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions. In addition to our commitment to product research and innovation, focusing on forward-
looking, environmentally friendly, and earth-friendly products, we also actively practice the principles of the circular economy. 
We adhere to the 4R principles of Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, and Recovery to achieve the ultimate goals of zero waste, zero 
pollution, and zero emissions.
In terms of energy-saving equipment, UPC Technology continues to replace energy-saving air compressors, circulating 
water pumps, and variable frequency blowers, and improve steam efficiency. For air pollution prevention, we utilize waste heat 
recovery technology to convert steam and generate energy benefits. Additionally, we have added rainwater buffer tanks to 
increase rainwater recovery and reuse, thereby reducing the impact of water and electricity shortages.
The effort towards sustainable operations has no endpoint. We will continue to enhance our corporate competitiveness in 
"brand, research and development, manufacturing, and distribution" to consistently create maximum value for our stakeholders.

1.1 About UPC Technology Corporation
UPC Technology Corporation, an associate of the MiTAC-Synnex Group, primarily engages 
in the production and sales of raw materials for plastic products. With production bases located 
in Taiwan, China, and Southeast Asia, its market sales network spans across the globe, making it 
the largest and most competitive professional supplier in the region. The company holds a 30% 
market share for phthalic anhydride and plasticizers in the Greater China region and a 60% market 
share in Taiwan (with a 65% market share for environmentally friendly plasticizers in Taiwan). UPC 
Technology Corporation provides prompt local services to customers, reducing logistics, labor, 
and processing costs on the client side, thereby achieving green environmental goals of energy 
conservation and carbon reduction.

Risk Management Committee
To strengthen corporate governance and risk management functions, the Risk Management Committee was established in accordance with the Regulations Governing 
Establishment of Internal Control Systems by Public Companies and the Risk Management Best Practice Principles for TWSE/GTSM Listed Companies. The “Risk 
Management Committee Charter” was formulated. The committee is composed of three independent directors, with Hwang, Jung-Chiou elected as the convener. Together 
with the President and functional executives, the committee reviews the company's operational risk issues. The term of the first Risk Management Committee is from January 
18, 2022, to July 20, 2024. The Risk Management Committee is required to hold at least one meeting per year to report on the execution status of risk management and 
provide improvement suggestions to the Board of Directors. During the reporting period, the committee held two meetings. The corporate governance officer is responsible

3.2 Product Quality Management
UPC Technology Corporation has established and implemented an ISO 9001 quality management system to ensure strict control over product quality. Each year, the 
company entrusts SGS Taiwan Limited to test its products according to the latest standards such as SVHC, RoHS 2.0, EN-71, ASTMF-963, PAHs, and halogens and their 
compounds. This verification ensures compliance with green environmental management substance requirements, providing downstream customers with safe raw materials.
All products come with safety data sheets and hazardous chemical composition certificates to ensure safety. The company strictly controls products required by 
the EU RoHS 2.0 directive, closely monitoring and complying with the regulations on hazardous substances such as lead, cadmium, mercury, hexavalent chromium, Di(2-
ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP), benzyl butyl phthalate (BBP), dibutyl phthalate (DBP), and diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP).
The company's ERP system establishes product control standards to ensure that shipments meet standards and customer expectations. Relevant 
information is published on the company's website and internal KM system and communicated with stakeholders. Upon detecting quality issues, a 
quality anomaly report is prepared for necessary improvements. If a product affects customer interests, a recall procedure is activated, and customers 
are immediately informed for recalls.

Supplier Evaluation
In 2023, audits or surveys were conducted on suppliers of commodities and auxiliary materials, with 
commodities suppliers accounting for 74% of the company’s annual procurement value. Due to the 
specialized nature of the industry, the company primarily works with established professional suppliers 
and rarely adds new ones. However, in 2023, the company successfully developed 47 new suppliers in 
the auxiliary materials and engineering categories (evaluated within three months of contract signing). 
All suppliers were evaluated based on quality systems, supplier management, process control, social 
responsibility, and environmental management. The evaluation results showed no significant actual or 
potential negative impacts, and all suppliers met the company's requirements. In 2023, there were no 
incidents of environmental, labor conditions, human rights, or social impacts with suppliers, and no 
partnerships were terminated. 
Supplier Evaluation Principles
	
■
Evaluation Targets: Existing suppliers of commodities, auxiliary materials, and engineering services.
	
■
Evaluation Frequency and Number of Suppliers: At least 30 long-term or major suppliers selected for 
evaluation annually.
	
■
Exclusion Criteria: Suppliers who have already disclosed ESG reports.
	
■
Scoring Standards: Compliance score/Applicable score ≥ 60%. Suppliers are ranked from excellent to 
poor based on performance scores across five levels (excellent being over 90%, poor being below 60%). 
This serves as a mechanism for managing and selecting suppliers.
	
■
Evaluation Results and Actions: Suppliers scoring below 60% are provided with flexible guidance or 
assistance. If deemed necessary, they are reported to the President for approval and may be classified as 
poor suppliers, potentially resulting in their replacement.

4.2 Workplace Safety Management and Employee Health
Safety is the cornerstone of the company' s sustainable operations. In terms of employee health, fire safety, and process safety, the group has comprehensively 
strengthened on-site management and self-inspection systems. We have also implemented a robust Process Safety Management (PSM) system with the goal of zero 
workplace injuries and zero process safety incidents. UPC Technology Corporation's Linyuan Plant continues to promote the TOSHMS and ISO 45001 Occupational Safety 
and Health Management Systems, adhering to the "Safety and Health Policy" to provide safe and healthy working conditions, comply with safety and health regulations, labor 
rights, and other requirements, eliminate hazards and reduce occupational safety and health risks, and promote worker consultation and participation in safety and health 
matters. The Safety and Health Policy is reviewed, communicated, and enforced through the Occupational Safety and Health Committee, management review meetings, 
and contractor coordination meetings. In 2023, the scope of the occupational safety and health management system establishment and internal/external audits covered the 
Linyuan Plant and its 153 workers (136 Linyuan Plant employees + 17 resident contractors), achieving 100% coverage.
Health Promotion and Activities
To promote the mental health of employees, UPC Technology Corporation offers psychological counseling services to help identify and resolve issues that may affect 
individual work performance. In 2023, the Occupational Physician provided on-site services 7 times, and Occupational Nurses provided on-site services 60 times. Employees 
participated in health services 70 times, with activities including stress management, workplace adaptation, parenting guidance, contractor health consultations, and maternal 
worker health consultations (2 counseling sessions).

5.1 Environmental Management
UPC establishes environmental policies committed to clean production, energy conservation, carbon reduction, and industrial waste reduction. In the Taipei 
office, we turn off lights for an hour during lunch breaks and shut down the central air conditioning at 8 p.m., and we recycle and reuse paper. At the Linyuan plant, the 
process area is included in the Linyuan Industrial Park's monitoring center for safety and environmental monitoring. If an abnormality occurs, we arrange for immediate 
handling and hold regular meetings to review system connectivity rates, minimizing the plant's impact on the nearby community. We have completed the "Health Risk 
Assessment and Actual Testing Program for Residents in the Vicinity of Linyuan Industrial Park," providing a safety barrier for the living environment and quality of life 
in the surrounding area. During the reporting period, the Company did not undertake any development or construction projects, so local community participation in 
environmental impact assessments was not required. In 2023, our air pollution prevention costs decreased compared to 2022 due to the efficiency improvements from 
switching the fuel used in the ortho-xylene and phthalic anhydride manufacturing processes to natural gas, which significantly reduced pollutants. UPC also joined 
CTBC Bank's "Dividend e-Notification" service. Since 2022, we have included a QR code in annual shareholder meeting and dividend distribution notifications to 
promote electronic notifications, reducing paper and administrative costs.

GHG Inventory
UPC Linyuan Plant established a GHG inventory system in 2013 and has since promoted GHG inventory operations, receiving ISO 14064-1 verification of GHG emissions 
from DNV Business Assurance Co. We use the operational control approach to aggregate emissions, with GWP values based primarily on IPCC 2007. The calculation of 
GHG emissions mainly employs the "emission factor method," but for emission sources like urea, acetylene, septic tanks, and refrigerants, where the emission factor method 
cannot be directly applied, we use the "mass balance method" instead.

■	Structural changes within the organization.
	
■	Changes in operational boundaries.

▍  Direct GHG Emissions (Scope 1)
 Direct GHG emissions primarily arise from process gases, natural gas, diesel, and other 
exhaust gases and energy use.
	
▍Indirect GHG Emissions (Scope 2)
Indirect GHG emissions primarily stem from the electricity and steam used in 
production, refrigeration, freezing, and office activities. Electricity is supplied by Taiwan 
Power Company, while steam is provided by Linyuan Advanced Materials Technology Co.
	
▍Other Indirect GHG Emissions (Scope 3)
Our plant's Scope 3 emissions focus on other indirect emissions generated by 
outsourced activities. Since these emission sources are owned or controlled by other 
companies, data collection is challenging and highly inaccurate. Therefore, we primarily 
conducted qualitative assessments before 2022 and switched to quantitative assessments 
in 2023, in line with ISO 14064-1 requirements. In 2023, Scope 3 emissions mainly came from 
activities not owned or controlled by our plant, such as employee commuting, business 
travel, and indirect GHG emissions from outsourced landfill/incineration of waste. We verified 
Scope 3 GHG emissions in 2023 for internal research purposes; therefore, this report does 
not include those details.

▍Air Pollution Prevention
UPC adheres to the principles of green chemistry. At 
the Linyuan Facility, the Company has installed various

units, which have significantly improved air quality. In Taipei,

Climate-Related Information
Excessive economic development has caused 
global warming, rising sea temperatures, and changes 
in Pacific Ocean currents, leading to the El Niño 
phenomenon. This results in increased frequency of 
heavy rains and droughts. Extreme rainfall elevates the 
risk of flooding, while prolonged droughts impact water-
intensive industries, causing supply and production 
disruptions and affecting the entire industrial chain. 
Located in the Linyuan Industrial Park in Kaohsiung, the 
Linyuan Plant commits to environmental protection as 
required by industrial park regulations and enhances 
the maintenance of green spaces within the facility. 
All environmental monitoring reports meet emission 
standards, showing no significant impact on the 
environment or biodiversity. In 2023, no species from 
the International Union for Conservation of Nature 
(IUCN) Red List or the national conservation list were 
found in the Company's locations.
6.1 Governance
 (Disclose the organization' s governance regarding climate-related risks and opportunities)
In 2017, the Company established the "Rules for Performance Evaluation of Board of Directors." 
The Board of Directors conducts an internal performance evaluation at least once a year, assessing 
risks and opportunities related to economic, environmental, and community (including human rights) 
topics and impacts. In 2023, the Board held five meetings.
To enhance corporate governance and strengthen risk management functions, UPC set up a 
Risk Management Committee and formulated organizational regulations for the committee. Three 
independent directors serve as risk management committee members, with independent director 
Hwang Jung-chiou acting as the convener. The committee, along with the Company' s president and 
functional managers, reviews operational risks. The Risk Management Committee must report risk 
management execution status and improvement suggestions to the Board of Directors in a meeting 
held at least once a year. During the reporting period, the committee held two meetings, with the 
corporate governance officer reporting on risk management activities to the Board, which supervises 
the progress of risk management tasks.

reduced wastewater discharge by 4.15 ML.

facility treats its process wastewater using biochemical 
and MBR methods. The treated water is then used to 
replenish the cooling towers, firefighting water tanks, and 
FA facility processes, as well as being reused in the original 
processes to reduce tap water usage and conserve water. 
In 2023, the plasticizer facility reclaimed a total of 33.65 ML 
of wastewater.

2023 Water Pollution Prevention and Response Mechanisms:

evaporators. In 2023, these measures reduced wastewater discharge by 4.15 ML.
(2)	 Plasticizer Facility Wastewater Reclamation: The plasticizer facility treats its process wastewater using biochemical and MBR methods. The treated 
water is then used to replenish the cooling towers, firefighting water tanks, and FA facility processes, as well as being reused in the original processes to 
reduce tap water usage and conserve water. In 2023, the plasticizer facility reclaimed a total of 33.65 ML of wastewater.
